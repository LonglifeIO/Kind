One thing I'd add to the rhythm: after each probe finishes, before starting the research for the next probe, write down what was learned. Decisions that were made, surprises that came up, things that looked one way before the probe and look different now. This is the "working journal" the probes document mentions. Without that step, the lessons of each probe stay implicit and don't propagate forward as clearly. With it, the research questions for the next probe are sharper because they incorporate what just happened.
