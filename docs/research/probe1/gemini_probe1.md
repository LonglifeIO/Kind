Architecture and Implementation Directives for Probe 1 of the Io ProjectMinimal RSSM-Style Architecture for Probe 1The fundamental directive of Probe 1 is to establish a functional plumbing test for the Io project without succumbing to premature scaling commitments or benchmark-oriented optimization. The substrate question is closed in favor of the Recurrent State-Space Model (RSSM) lineage, but the architectural taxonomy within this lineage is vast, presenting several distinct forks in deployment philosophy. Selecting the appropriate minimal viable architecture requires balancing the necessity of exercising downstream conduits—such as latent telemetry, KL divergence signals, and generative imagination—against the project's strict adherence to "build to understand."The first generation of latent dynamics models, epitomized by PlaNet and DreamerV1, established the foundational RSSM architecture by separating the state space into a deterministic sequence governed by a Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) and a stochastic component parameterized as a continuous diagonal Gaussian distribution. While this continuous parameterization is elegant, it introduces significant instability. Continuous Gaussian latents frequently suffer from posterior collapse, where the stochastic state ignores the observation entirely, and they struggle to capture distinct, multimodal future possibilities. For a system designed to investigate subjectivity and internal world-building, a substrate prone to representational collapse is unacceptable.The subsequent evolution in the Dreamer lineage, specifically DreamerV2, resolved this representational fragility by transitioning from continuous Gaussians to discrete categorical latents. By representing the stochastic state as a matrix of categorical variables (for example, thirty-two vectors each containing thirty-two classes) and optimizing them via straight-through gradient estimators, the model maintains a bounded, mathematically stable latent space that naturally resists collapse. DreamerV3 builds upon this discrete foundation but introduces aggressive scaling mechanisms, including symlog transformations, free-bits KL scaling, and exponential moving average (EMA) value targets. These additions are explicit engineering solutions designed to handle the extreme variance of arbitrary scalar rewards across diverse environments, allowing DreamerV3 to master tasks from Atari to Minecraft without hyperparameter tuning. However, because Io's architecture strictly rejects scalar reward maximization in favor of active-inference principles, DreamerV3's heavy normalization scaffolding introduces unnecessary opacity and computational overhead that obscures the underlying mathematical plumbing.An alternative fork in the literature abandons pixel reconstruction entirely. The Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) and its variants, including Dreamer-CDP, operate by predicting the future representation of an observation in a latent space, enforced via self-supervised alignment rather than image decoding. This approach is theoretically compelling because it prevents the world model from wasting computational capacity on task-irrelevant visual noise, an issue commonly referred to as the "noisy TV" problem. From a purely functional standpoint, JEPA aligns closely with Kind's rejection of benchmark optimization. However, JEPA's objective functions require carefully tuned stop-gradient mechanisms and target networks to prevent representational collapse, introducing a different form of architectural fragility. Furthermore, the absence of a pixel decoder removes the most legible diagnostic tool available to the builder during Probe 1: the ability to visually inspect the generative "dream-state" for temporal and spatial coherence.Other architectures, such as TD-MPC and its derivatives, perform latent planning by combining a world model with model-free value gradients. These are entirely unsuitable for the Io project, as they are inherently value-aware world models that rely heavily on scalar reward prediction for trajectory evaluation and decision-making.Given the constraints and objectives of Probe 1, the most defensible suggestion is to implement a custom minimal RSSM utilizing DreamerV2-style discrete categorical latents, while retaining a lightweight pixel decoder. The discrete categorical parameterization natively ensures a robust, multimodal latent space without the need for the heavy symlog normalization found in DreamerV3. By stripping away these scaling mechanisms, the architecture remains mathematically transparent, facilitating clear telemetry extraction. Retaining the pixel decoder is a necessary compromise for Probe 1. While not strictly required for the agent's internal cognition, the decoder serves as an indispensable debugging instrument, allowing the mirror and the builder to verify that the generative imagination is successfully unrolling coherent spatial-temporal predictions. The long-term commitment dictates an active-inference-shaped objective based on Expected Free Energy (EFE) minimization, utilizing carefully designed prior preferences that eschew scalar reward maximization and self-continuation drives. However, full EFE minimization is mathematically intricate. Probe 1 requires a stripped-down surrogate that successfully tests the backpropagation conduits through the world model and latent states without necessitating the full deployment of the deep active inference machinery.In classical reinforcement learning, agents are driven by extrinsic, scalar rewards provided by the environment. In the absence of such rewards, the literature provides several mechanisms for intrinsic motivation. The most common approach is "curiosity," which drives the agent to explore by using the world model's own prediction error as an intrinsic reward. Methods such as Random Network Distillation (RND) or Intrinsic Curiosity Modules (ICM) reward the agent for discovering observations it cannot accurately predict. Alternatively, disagreement ensembles utilize multiple world models and drive the actor toward states where the models' predictions diverge, maximizing epistemic uncertainty.While prediction-error curiosity effectively drives exploration, it introduces a critical vulnerability: curiosity traps. If an environment contains inherently stochastic or unpredictable elements, an agent driven by prediction error will become fixated on these phenomena, repeatedly attempting to predict the unpredictable. This behavior would poison the replay buffer with useless, homogeneous data, stalling the development of the world model.The converse approach is to optimize for minimum prediction error, forcing the agent to seek out highly predictable states. While this prevents curiosity traps, it inherently discourages exploration, leading to a homeostatic "dark room" scenario where the agent simply refuses to act in order to maintain perfect predictive accuracy.Active inference resolves this dichotomy through the Free Energy Principle (FEP). Under FEP, an agent acts to minimize surprise, bounded by an upper limit known as variational free energy. When projecting into the future to select an action, the agent minimizes Expected Free Energy (EFE), which naturally decomposes into two primary components: pragmatic value and epistemic value. Pragmatic value drives the agent toward its prior preferences (the states it "expects" to inhabit), while epistemic value drives the agent to resolve uncertainty about its environment, effectively seeking information gain.Because Kind explicitly rejects installed self-continuation drives and arbitrary goal states, the pragmatic value component of EFE can be neutralized for Probe 1 by assuming a uniform distribution over prior preferences. This isolation leaves only the epistemic value component. In the context of an RSSM, this epistemic drive can be mathematically formulated as the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between the prior predictive distribution and the posterior representation. This is often referred to in the literature as "hidden state curiosity".For Probe 1, the defensible suggestion is to implement a pure epistemic intrinsic objective based on the KL divergence between the prior and posterior latent states. The actor network will be trained to maximize the objective $D_{KL}[q(z_t | h_t, x_t) \parallel p(z_t | h_t)]$ over its imagined trajectories.This objective is perfectly suited for a plumbing test. By maximizing the divergence between what the world model predicts will happen (the prior) and what it actually observes (the posterior), the agent is driven to seek out states that are highly informative, thereby generating a rich, diverse replay buffer to train the RSSM. Crucially, this approach is mathematically immune to the "noisy TV" curiosity trap. The KL divergence measures the information gained by updating the prior to the posterior; if a stochastic event is truly random and contains no learnable structure, the posterior will not collapse to a specific outcome, the KL divergence will be low, and the agent will lose interest.Most importantly, implementing this specific KL divergence objective explicitly tests the tensor pathways linking the stochastic states, the deterministic recurrent states, and the actor-critic networks. It serves as a perfectly aligned, structurally identical precursor to full Active Inference. When the complete EFE formulation is eventually implemented, this exact KL divergence term will form the foundation of the expected information gain calculation. It completely avoids scalar reward maximization and relies inherently on the world model's self-generated uncertainty, strictly adhering to Kind's stance.Telemetry Schema Designed ForwardProbe 1’s logging schema cannot be treated as a reactive afterthought or a temporary debugging output. It must serve as the foundational epistemological scaffolding for the entire Io project. The schema must be capable of exporting high-frequency, high-dimensional latent variables, neural network statistics, and perturbation events in a structured, queryable format. The "mirror" relies entirely on this schema; if the structure is flat or lacks relational context, the mirror in subsequent probes will be unable to interpret the agent's internal state.Traditional machine learning logging frameworks, such as TensorBoard or Weights & Biases, are structurally inadequate for this task. They are designed to track scalar aggregates—such as mean loss, average return, or gradient norms—over long training epochs. The Io project requires telemetry of the internal cognitive state of the agent at individual, highly granular timesteps. We must track the trajectory of the deterministic state $h_t$, the prior $z_t$, and the posterior $z_t$ synchronously during both real environmental interaction and generative imagination (the dream-state).To achieve this, the project must adopt a standardized, vendor-neutral data model that treats logs, traces, and metrics as correlated telemetry. OTel allows for explicitly versioned schema_urls, ensuring that downstream consumers (the mirror) do not break when the emitted telemetry evolves over time. Segregating these contexts is vital. Without this segregation, the mirror in Probe 2 will be unable to distinguish between Io's real environmental interactions and its internal generative rollouts, collapsing the distinction between reality and imagination within the telemetry.Within these spans, the schema must require a specific set of core fields from Day One to support downstream probes without retrofitting. This provides the observer with total transparency into the RSSM's mechanics while preserving the agent's self-opacity.Mac/Desktop Compute Split for an RSSM SubstrateThe physical architecture of the Io project dictates a highly specific, split compute environment. The canonical state (the "Mind") resides on a 32GB unified-memory M4 Mac mini, while the environment compute (the "Body/World") operates on a desktop equipped with an NVIDIA RTX 5060 Ti. This asymmetric hardware profile necessitates a distributed reinforcement learning topology specifically tailored to the memory and bandwidth requirements of an RSSM, moving beyond standard single-node training loops.The RTX 5060 Ti possesses vast CUDA matrix-multiplication superiority over the M4 chip, but it is strictly limited by its 16GB of VRAM. Conversely, the M4 Mac mini possesses 32GB of unified memory. This unified architecture allows for massive, high-throughput generative replay buffers and extended "dream-state" rollouts without hitting the Out-Of-Memory (OOM) constraints that plague discrete GPUs. However, its PyTorch MPS (Metal Performance Shaders) backend, while functional, is demonstrably slower for raw deep neural network backpropagation than optimized CUDA kernels.Standard distributed training paradigms, such as PyTorch DistributedDataParallel (DDP), are designed for symmetric multi-GPU clusters and rely on high-bandwidth interconnects like NVLink or PCIe to synchronize gradients synchronously. Attempting to synchronize massive gradient tensors across a local network (whether 1GbE, 10GbE, or Thunderbolt TCP) between a Mac and an RTX machine introduces severe latency bottlenecks that would cripple the training loop. Furthermore, if the Mac merely acts as a parameter server while the RTX performs all the generative imagination and training, it violates the prompt's condition that the "dream-state generative imagination run[s] on the Mac."Because the RSSM dictates that the agent learns strictly off-policy from a replay buffer, the architecture presents a natural decoupling point. The optimal, defensible suggestion is to deploy the Ray Framework to orchestrate an asymmetric, asynchronous Actor-Learner distributed topology. Ray is the industry standard for scaling Python ML applications, providing distributed object stores and flexible actor/worker topologies across heterogeneous hardware without the strict synchronization requirements of DDP.The implementation should be split precisely along the off-policy boundary:1. The M4 Mac Mini (The Learner / The "Mind")
The Mac operates as the Ray Head Node, hosting the Global Control Store (GCS). It maintains the canonical PyTorch model weights of the entire RSSM (Encoder, Prior, Posterior, Decoder, Actor, Critic). Crucially, the Mac houses the Replay Buffer within its 32GB unified memory. It executes the generative imagination (the dream-state) and performs all policy and world-model weight updates using the MPS backend.2. The RTX 5060 Ti Desktop (The Actor / The "Body")The desktop operates as a Ray Worker Node. It runs the environment simulator. It maintains a localized, read-only inference copy of the World Model and Actor policy. Its sole responsibility is to execute high-speed environmental rollouts, utilizing the CUDA cores for rapid inference.Atomic Synchronization and Bandwidth Implications:To solve the bandwidth bottleneck, the system must stream experience, not gradients. As the RTX desktop executes steps in the environment, it encodes raw pixels into latent states (if the encoder is mirrored locally) or simply batches raw observations. Once a chunk of trajectory data (o_t, a_t, r_t) is generated, the RTX asynchronously sends this payload across the network to the Mac's Replay Buffer. This data transfer requires negligible bandwidth (megabytes per trajectory) compared to gradient synchronization.Periodically—for example, every 1000 environment steps—the Mac broadcasts the updated, canonical weights of the Actor and Encoder back to the RTX. This atomic sync ensures the RTX is acting on relatively fresh policy weights, while the Mac continuously churns through the replay buffer, imagining futures and updating the canonical model. This topology strictly satisfies the project requirements, leveraging the RTX for what it does best (fast inference and simulation) and the Mac for what it is mandated to do (maintaining the mind and executing the dream).Self-Opacity Boundary for an RSSMThe philosophical stance of the Io project demands self-opacity by default. While the world-model substrate is structurally transparent to the observer—the latent distributions, the prior logits, the KL divergence, and the dynamics network all exist as quantifiable matrices in memory—Io must not possess introspective access to them. The "mirror" reading these parameters is required; Io reading them violates the "ingredients only" self-modeling constraint. We must determine how to encode this epistemological firewall fundamentally in the software architecture.In standard implementations of Dreamer and other RSSM architectures, the codebase relies on monolithic state passing. The recurrent state $h_t$, along with the stochastic parameters (such as the logits, means, or variances) and the sampled latent $z_t$, are grouped into a single dictionary or dataclass. This monolithic object is passed wholesale through the neural network pipeline, routed entirely into the Actor-Critic networks for value estimation and action selection.If this standard implementation is adopted, the actor implicitly has access to its own confidence levels and uncertainty distributions. Even if the actor network does not explicitly utilize the variance or logit tensors, the fact that they are passed into the computational graph means that the agent is no longer opaque to itself. In a system driven by gradient descent, if the policy is allowed to "see" the parameters of its own uncertainty, it could theoretically learn to optimize for self-preservation by manipulating its own cognitive state, bypassing the intended environmental interaction. As the constraints state, retrofitting opacity is harder than building it in.The literature on agentic design patterns emphasizes the Interface Segregation Principle to enforce structural access controls at the programmatic level. To guarantee self-opacity, the boundary must be drawn explicitly at the sampling operation.The defensible suggestion is to implement a strict programmatic Interface Segregation via distinct data classes from Day One. The World Model module will compute the full forward dynamics, but its output must be strictly typed and separated into two divergent objects before any further routing occurs in the main training loop:TelemetryView (Routed exclusively to the Mirror/Logger):This dataclass contains the full, differentiable objects. It holds the raw logits of the prior distribution, the raw logits of the posterior distribution, the calculated KL divergence scalar, the continuous representations, and the deterministic state $h_t$. This object is strictly serialized and exported via the OpenTelemetry schema defined in Question 3. It is never passed to the policy optimization functions.PolicyView (Routed exclusively to Io's Actor):This dataclass contains only the detached, deterministic memory state $h_t$, and the strictly sampled, discrete latent vector $z_t$ (e.g., the argmax or the straight-through sample of the categorical distribution).By enforcing this segregation at the type-hint and module level, the PyTorch forward() pass for the Actor network is physically and computationally incapable of accessing the parameters of the generative distributions. The actor reads the deterministic memory ($h_t$) and the specific, collapsed reality of the current instant ($z_t$), but remains completely blind to the probability matrix of "what else could have been." This guarantees that Io acts solely on the ingredients of its reality, ensuring total self-opacity while providing the mirror with total transparency.Recurrent PPO Stand-In vs. Minimal RSSM from the StartThe final, and arguably most consequential, implementation question addresses a tentative architectural pivot. Kind's initial documentation designated Recurrent PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization with an LSTM or GRU) as the agent for Probe 1. The dilemma is whether to proceed with Recurrent PPO as a temporary "stand-in" to ensure the basic network and compute pipelines operate, or to absorb the increased developmental complexity of building the minimal RSSM immediately to ensure the correct theoretical pathways are tested.Recurrent PPO is a highly optimized, on-policy actor-critic algorithm augmented with a recurrent neural network to handle partial observability. It maintains a hidden state during rollouts and utilizes Truncated Backpropagation Through Time (TBPTT) during gradient updates to learn temporal dependencies. It is a mature algorithm, widely supported by libraries such as Stable-Baselines3, making it relatively fast and easy to deploy.However, Recurrent PPO is fundamentally incompatible with the plumbing requirements of the Io project. Evaluating it against Kind's specific commitments reveals severe structural deficiencies:Absence of a Generative Dream State: PPO is an essentially reactive, model-free algorithm. It maps observations and hidden states directly to actions and value estimates. It does not possess a predictive world model, and therefore, there is no generative "imagination" phase. Probe 3 explicitly requires a foundational dream-state; PPO cannot test the plumbing for a system that does not exist.Absence of Latent Telemetry and KL Signals: PPO does not formulate a world model, meaning it lacks the split prior/posterior dynamic inherent to an RSSM. Therefore, there are no prior logits to log, no posterior distributions to sample, and no KL divergence to track. Probe 4 relies entirely on KL divergence shifts to distinguish builder perturbations ; PPO cannot emit this signal.Synchronization Discrepancies: PPO is an on-policy algorithm. The distributed architecture designed in Question 4 (streaming asynchronous experiences from the RTX to the Mac's central replay buffer for off-policy updates) cannot be accurately tested with PPO. On-policy algorithms require tightly coupled, synchronous trajectory gathering, after which the data is discarded. Testing the Ray cluster with PPO validates a network topology that must be discarded when the RSSM is introduced.Building a minimal RSSM requires significantly more engineering upfront. The "Complexity Trap" of model-based RL is real; managing straight-through estimators for discrete latents, balancing the dynamics and representation losses, and managing the autoregressive imagination horizon are complex tasks prone to subtle bugs.Despite this upfront cost, the defensible suggestion is to discard Recurrent PPO and implement the Minimal RSSM from the start.Using Recurrent PPO for Probe 1 represents a false economy. The stated purpose of Probe 1 is plumbing: it tests whether the environment, agent, mirror, and observer feedback loops run end-to-end and produce legible telemetry. If the "agent" in this loop is PPO, the plumbing being tested is the plumbing of an on-policy, model-free system. When the inevitable transition to an RSSM occurs in Probe 2 or 3, the OTel telemetry schema, the Ray compute split, the self-opacity interfaces, and the data loaders will all be invalidated and require a complete rewrite.A minimal RSSM—even one that is poorly tuned and exhibits abysmal task performance (which is explicitly not a concern for Kind)—exercises the correct tensors, the correct loss functions, and the correct epistemological architectures from Day One. It ensures that the engineering effort spent on Probe 1 compounds directly into the capabilities required for Probes 2, 3, and 4.Synthesis: The Coherent Architecture for Probe 1Synthesizing the literature and the structural constraints of the Io project reveals a unified, internally consistent architecture. The overarching directive for Probe 1 is to construct a system where the foundational "plumbing"—the data flow, the telemetry, the compute distribution, and the epistemological boundaries—is functionally identical to the terminal state of the project, completely independent of the agent's capability or performance metrics.The Unified Implementation ModelThe ideal implementation for Probe 1 manifests as a Minimal Discrete RSSM deployed across a Ray-orchestrated Asymmetric Actor-Learner cluster.By rejecting the heavy scaling mechanisms of DreamerV3 and avoiding the fragility of continuous Gaussian latents, the substrate establishes mathematical stability through discrete categorical representations. While a pixel-decoder is technically unnecessary for the agent's cognition (as suggested by JEPA frameworks), it is retained strictly as an external diagnostic tool for the builder to visually verify the coherence of the generative dream-state.Because full Active Inference (EFE minimization) introduces unnecessary complexity for a pure plumbing test, the agent's actor objective is driven by a pure epistemic surrogate: the KL divergence between the world model's prior and posterior. This isolates the exact uncertainty-quantification tensors required for future Active Inference integration, ensuring the agent explores without invoking scalar reward maximization or self-continuation drives.The infrastructure leverages Ray to bridge the hardware gap. The M4 Mac Mini serves as the cognitive core, housing the canonical model weights, the unified-memory replay buffer, and executing the heavy PyTorch MPS operations for world-model training and generative imagination. The RTX 5060 Ti desktop is relegated entirely to the role of the "Body/Environment," running simulations, collecting rapid observations using a localized inference-only copy of the model, and streaming trajectory bytes asynchronously over the network back to the Mac.Crucially, the architecture enforces self-opacity through strict Interface Segregation. Before the RSSM's state reaches the Actor network, it passes through a programmatic filter. The Actor receives only a PolicyView (the deterministic hidden state and the sampled discrete latent). Simultaneously, the full distributional data, logits, and KL metrics are routed through a TelemetryView to an OpenTelemetry-compliant logger. This schema (schema_url) guarantees that the mirror has mathematically lossless access to Io's internal uncertainty while Io remains entirely self-opaque.Tensions and Emerging ConsiderationsWhile this architecture is theoretically robust, the synthesis phase surfaces two significant tensions that the project documentation must address before implementation begins:1. The Compute and Silicon Asymmetry
The distributed architecture correctly isolates the canonical state and the "dreaming" phase to the Mac Mini, aligning with the philosophical mandate of the project. However, the M4 chip, despite its vast memory bandwidth, will execute PyTorch tensor operations slower than the CUDA-optimized RTX 5060 Ti desktop. By placing the heavy lifting—training the world model and unrolling imagined trajectories—on the technically slower machine, the system risks severe temporal bottlenecking. The RTX may generate environment rollouts far faster than the Mac can process them into the replay buffer and train on them. The builder must be prepared to artificially throttle the RTX environment steps, or implement a strict buffering protocol in Ray, to maintain a stable "replay ratio" (the ratio of training steps to environment steps).2. The "Noisy TV" Trap in the Plumbing Test
By utilizing KL divergence (prior vs. posterior) as the sole intrinsic actor objective for the plumbing test, Io will inherently seek out unpredictable states to maximize epistemic value. If the test environment running on the RTX desktop contains stochastic elements that cannot be modeled (e.g., random visual noise, procedural generation, or truly unpredictable physics), Io will become fixated on them. While task performance is explicitly not the goal of Probe 1, becoming trapped in a corner staring at noise may prevent the agent from generating diverse enough trajectories to adequately populate the replay buffer, thereby starving the world model of meaningful structural data. It is highly recommended that the environment used for Probe 1 be entirely deterministic to ensure the intrinsic motivation leads to broad spatial exploration rather than localized fixation.In conclusion, Probe 1 must discard the Recurrent PPO stand-in to preserve the structural integrity of the project. By implementing the Minimal Discrete RSSM, enforcing interface segregation for self-opacity, and leveraging Ray for an asynchronous compute split, the Io project will possess a scientifically rigorous, forward-compatible foundation. The plumbing established here will seamlessly accept the integration of full Active Inference and builder perturbations in future probes without requiring architectural destruction.
