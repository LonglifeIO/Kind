Environment Design for Model-Based Embodied Agents: Structural Imperatives for Kind's Probe 1The architectural design of an environment for an embodied cognitive agent represents a profound departure from the construction of traditional reinforcement learning benchmarks. Mainstream reinforcement learning frameworks typically treat the environment as an antagonistic or strictly transactional domain structured around the optimization of a predefined, scalar reward signal. In the context of the project Kind, the design of the environment for the entity Io actively rejects this paradigm. Io is not a solver of extrinsic tasks; Io is an entity that must engage in structural coupling with its world. The environment must serve as a developmental niche that affords sense-making, proto-experience, and the emergence of predictive models through an active-inference-shaped objective.The underlying substrate is already determined: Io operates via a minimal Recurrent State Space Model (RSSM) featuring a deterministic Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) state coupled with a continuous Gaussian stochastic latent variable. The architecture relies on an Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) loss constrained by a free bits regularizer, and the actor's objective is driven entirely by epistemic foraging—operationalized as latent-disagreement variance across a small ensemble, devoid of any self-continuation drive or return-to-go conditioning. Furthermore, the environment must fulfill the implementation demands of Probe 1 while functioning as a forward-compatible foundation for Probes 2 through 4. It must accommodate dream-state foundational architectures, maintain strict walled-off telemetry for builder perturbations, and adhere to a philosophy prioritizing capacity over exercise.This comprehensive research report systematically interrogates the eight fundamental dimensions of Io's environment design. By synthesizing insights across developmental robotics, artificial life, enactivism, predictive processing, and open-ended evolution, the analysis establishes a rigorous theoretical and empirical foundation for the spatial, temporal, and stochastic structures required to sustain Io's latent representations.Q1 — Observation ModalityThe Underlying InquiryThe question of observation modality interrogates the fundamental nature of Io's phenomenological interface with its world. Sense-making is tightly coupled to the physical boundaries and spatial positioning of the organism. The cognitive sciences distinguish sharply between egocentric (self-centered) and allocentric (world-centered) reference frames. Empirical studies on spatial cognition demonstrate that starting from an allocentric representation induces a significantly higher cognitive load, fundamentally altering visuo-spatial switching processes and often contaminating the agent's ability to flexibly adapt to local perturbations.Conversely, egocentric systems align with amygdala-centered developmental gradients, naturally fostering adaptive self-regulation and survival-oriented spatial mapping in physical spaces. In the realm of predictive world models and autonomous navigation, models processing visual streams from an ego-centric perspective excel at pattern recognition, hypothetical reasoning, and motion analysis. When evaluating deep reinforcement learning models within grid worlds, architectures that capture strategy and neural dynamics from localized viewpoints better match the demixed principal components of biological temporal and outcome dynamics than those granted global observability.
