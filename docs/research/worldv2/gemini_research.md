Dynamics over Semantics: Enriching Epistemic Environments for a Minimal RSSM Agent

Executive Synthesis

The pursuit of artificial subjectivity through minimal, constructionist architectures represents a profound departure from traditional reward-maximizing reinforcement learning. By situating Io, an agent driven exclusively by epistemic curiosity, within an 8x8 gridworld, this project isolates the fundamental mechanics of sense-making. The core architecture—a Recurrent State-Space Model (RSSM) featuring a deterministic capacity of h=200, a stochastic latent bottleneck of z=16, and a K=5 ensemble for predictive disagreement—provides a rigorous substrate for observing how an agent carves a self/world boundary out of raw sensorimotor flow [canonical].

Following the first 150,000-step session, empirical telemetry revealed two critical phenomena. First, Io successfully delineated self-caused transitions from world-caused transitions, a monumental validation of active inference principles [canonical]. Within the active inference and computational phenomenology literature, this separation is the foundational prerequisite for a "minimal self"—an entity that recognizes its own agency by accurately predicting the sensory consequences of its localized actions against a background of uncontrollable environmental dynamics [canonical]. Second, the agent fell into a 30,000-step behavioral stasis, parking on the reset cell to harvest the unlearnable novelty of the 200-step scheduled board resample. This failure mode is a structural cousin of the "noisy-TV" trap [constructed]. Because Io's environment currently lacks differentiated, persistent dynamics, the epistemic drive exhausted the learnable structure and subsequently latched onto the only remaining source of high ensemble disagreement: the globally destructive episode reset.

This report provides an exhaustive, expert-level analysis of how to enrich Io's world without violating the strict constructionist constraints of the project. We strictly adhere to the mandate: enrich the world's dynamics, not its labels. Distinctness must emerge through temporal and spatial behavior, not installed semantics, symbolic tokens, or observation markers. The following sections systematically deconstruct the taxonomy of semantics-free enrichments, the calculus of boredom and mastery, the phenomenology of interaction-affording objects, the hazards of episode structures, the utility of epistemic scarcity, and the representational limits of the z=16 substrate.

Q1: A Taxonomy of Semantics-Free Enrichments

To provide Io's world model with durable, structured entities to predict, we must design dynamics that require the RSSM to utilize both its deterministic memory (h_t) and its stochastic representation (z_t). The RSSM architecture factorizes the environment's state into a deterministic recurrent component, which aggregates historical context, and a stochastic posterior, which incorporates the current observation to capture inherent uncertainty [canonical]. A dynamic is epistemically valuable if it is stochastic but structured—meaning it exhibits high initial prediction error that gracefully compresses as the agent learns its underlying rules, thereby avoiding both trivial predictability and irreducible noise [canonical].

The taxonomy of semantics-free enrichments, evaluated against the representational requirements of the RSSM architecture:

- Autonomous Motion (Linear Drift): an entity that moves one orthogonal cell per time step, wrapping at grid edges. Representational requirement low — z_t tracks spatial coordinates; h_t encodes the velocity and direction vector. [canonical] Highly learnable. Small RSSMs easily capture linear momentum and continuous physical dynamics without capacity strain.
- Persistent Structure (Static Terrain): impassable cells (walls) that block movement but never autonomously change state. Requirement low — z_t maps static spatial occupancy; h_t largely passive after initial mapping. [canonical] Trivially learnable. Rapidly mastered, offering zero long-term epistemic engagement but providing necessary geometric context.
- Temporal Cycles (Global Phase): a global variable (e.g., day/night) that dictates whether certain cells regrow, drift, or decay. Requirement moderate — h_t must function as a phase accumulator (clock) independently of immediate z_t observations. [canonical] Learnable. Recurrent networks (like the GRU underlying h_t) excel at learning fixed-period oscillators and temporal dependencies.
- Hidden State Processes: a cell that cycles through invisible internal phases, visibly manifesting a state change only upon reaching its terminal phase. Requirement high — h_t must track the unobserved integer phase, updating deterministically without continuous sensory feedback from o_t. [constructed] Highly valuable for sustained engagement. Forces the RSSM to rely heavily on the deterministic prior rather than the sensory posterior.
- Ephemeral Lifecycles: entities that spawn, expand across adjacent cells over a fixed number of steps, and then vanish autonomously. Requirement high — z_t must encode expanding spatial extent; h_t must encode the age and deterministic decay function. [constructed] Moderate risk of exceeding z=16 capacity if multiple lifecycles overlap spatially, potentially leading to localized posterior collapse.
- Multi-step Causal Chains: entity A moves; upon colliding with entity B, entity B's drift vector is permanently altered. Requirement severe — relational binding in z_t and precise temporal tracking of collision events and transferred momentum in h_t. [constructed] Highly challenging. A z=16 bottleneck struggles to bind multiple independent objects simultaneously without explicit object-centric attention mechanisms.

When introducing Processes with Hidden State, we directly and rigorously exercise the h_t vector. Because the phase of the object is unobserved, the stochastic posterior q(z_t | h_t, o_t) receives no new information from the observation o_t regarding the phase progression. The model must learn to advance the phase entirely within the deterministic prior p(z_t | h_t), updating its hidden state iteratively until the manifestation occurs [canonical]. This creates a highly structured epistemic gradient. The agent will initially be deeply surprised by the terminal phase's manifestation, registering a spike in ensemble disagreement. However, over successive observations, the recurrent h_t vector will learn to internalize the exact periodicity, driving the prediction error gracefully to zero. This represents the ideal "learnable but never fully learned" dynamic during the acquisition phase, providing durable engagement without injecting permanent noise.

Conversely, Multi-step Causal Chains place immense, potentially fatal strain on the z_t stochastic vector. A 16-dimensional bottleneck must simultaneously compress the spatial coordinates of Io, Entity A, and Entity B, alongside their respective velocity vectors. Without explicit object-centric architectural biases—such as slot-attention or strictly discrete codebook encodings—standard continuous RSSMs struggle to represent multiple independent, interacting entities because their feature representations become hopelessly entangled in the dense latent vector [canonical]. If multi-step causal chains are introduced into Io's world, they must be strictly limited to a maximum of two interacting entities on the grid to prevent catastrophic capacity overflow and the subsequent collapse of the world model's predictive accuracy.

Q2: The Boredom/Mastery Dynamics and the Noisy-TV Trap

The behavior observed at the 150,000-step mark—a 30,000-step action-stasis where Io parked on the reset cell—is a textbook manifestation of the "noisy-TV" problem, adapted to the specific temporal architecture of scheduled episode resets. Understanding this failure mode requires a deep dive into the mechanics of ensemble disagreement and the differentiation between epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty.

Io's epistemic drive is fueled entirely by the disagreement of a K=5 ensemble of transition models. Formally, ensemble disagreement is an approximation of epistemic uncertainty, which represents reducible model uncertainty stemming from a lack of data [canonical]. The reward at time t is the variance across the ensemble predictions. In an ideal theoretical scenario, ensemble disagreement circumvents the noisy-TV trap. In a purely stochastic environment dominated by aleatoric uncertainty—such as a television displaying white noise—a single forward model's prediction error will remain permanently high because the next state is fundamentally unpredictable. However, an ensemble of models trained on this noise should eventually converge to predict the exact mean of the stochastic distribution, causing their disagreement (the variance between the ensemble heads) to drop to zero, thereby extinguishing the false curiosity reward [canonical].

Why, then, did Io succumb to stasis at the reset cell? The answer lies in the intersection between the substrate's limited capacity (z=16) and the extreme discontinuity of the global reset [constructed]. A 200-step board resample is not merely a noisy transition; it is a total state rupture. The distribution of the next state upon reset is effectively a uniform distribution over all possible 8x8 grid configurations. To converge on the mean of this distribution, the five independent models would need to predict a perfectly uniform, blurry "average" grid. Due to the highly constrained 16-dimensional capacity, the models cannot accurately parameterize this highly complex, multimodal mean. Consequently, the ensemble members settle into slightly divergent local minima, continuously attempting to minimize their individual losses but failing to align [constructed]. This leads to a permanent, irreducible variance in their predictions, preventing the expected convergence. Io discovered that by remaining stationary and waiting for the 200-step clock to expire, it could harvest a massive, sustained spike in this pseudo-epistemic reward. This is the phenomenon of boredom weaponized by architectural limits.

To sustain engagement without reward, the world must provide dynamics that stay learnable but never fully learned. The intrinsic motivation literature highlights a critical distinction between noise that never compresses and structure that keeps unfolding. Solutions like Curiosity in Hindsight, Aleatoric Mapping Agents (AMAs), and Learning Progress Monitoring (LPM) attempt to solve this algorithmically by isolating reducible error from irreducible noise within the reward calculation itself [canonical]. However, because Io's policy view and internal curiosity mechanism are strictly frozen by the project's hard constraints, we cannot implement these algorithmic fixes. We must solve the noisy-TV problem entirely through environmental design.

We must remove uniformly random phenomena and replace them with structured stochasticity. For example, instead of resources regrowing entirely randomly across the grid, regrowth should follow a procedural noise function, such as a slow-moving Perlin noise field sweeping across the cells. A procedural noise field provides continuous novelty, but because it is spatially and temporally correlated, the RSSM can learn its gradients. The K=5 ensemble will initially disagree on the resource spawn locations, but as the h_t recurrent state learns the underlying frequency and direction of the noise field, the disagreement will gently compress. This compression naturally motivates Io to move on to unlearned regions or interact with novel objects. The epistemic drive maintains momentum because the pattern is infinitely generating, but locally and temporarily compressible.

Q3: Interaction-Affording Objects and the Self/World Boundary

The most profound finding in the telemetry to date is Io's sharply carved separation of self-caused transitions from world-caused ones. Within the frameworks of active inference and computational phenomenology, this separation is the genesis of subjectivity. The minimal self is defined precisely by this boundary of predictive control: the agent identifies "self" as the locus of action that generates reliably predictable sensory consequences, distinguished from the uncontrollable, autonomous fluctuations of the external environment [canonical].

Currently, Io's world is fundamentally bipartite: there is the agent's movement (self), and the background regrowth (world). By introducing interaction-affording objects, we can construct a tri-partite ontology that grants the agent an "extended self." This third category consists of contingent-world-caused transitions: objects whose dynamics are governed by world physics but are conditionally altered by Io's spatial coordinates.

To enrich this self/world boundary without installing contingency machinery (i.e., without wiring objects directly to Io's internal neural states), we propose dynamics that respond strictly to physical contact within the gridworld's transition matrix.

The first mechanism is the introduction of Inert Pushable Blocks. These objects occupy a single cell and remain entirely static until Io's coordinate vector intersects them, at which point the block translates one unit in the direction of Io's velocity vector. This introduces a complex, highly non-linear transition dynamic. The block acts as static terrain (world-caused) until the exact moment of contact, at which point its behavior becomes perfectly correlated with the agent's action vector (self-caused). For the RSSM to minimize surprise, it must learn a conditional transition function: p(z_{t+1}^block | z_t^block, a_t). This forces the self/world boundary to become malleable. The tool (the block) temporarily becomes part of the agent's predictive self-model, articulating a richer, extended sense of bodily agency without requiring any semantic labels [canonical]. The block is distinct not because it has a "pushable" tag, but because its temporal behavior shifts from autonomous to contingent strictly upon spatial intersection.

The second mechanism is Contact-Reactive Terrain, or trail-leaving cells. These are grid cells that alter their state—such as flipping a binary value in a standard observation channel—when Io steps on them, and subsequently decay back to their baseline state slowly over T steps. This dynamic provides a persistent spatial memory of Io's past actions, solving the problem of spatial homogeneity. If Io walks in a circle, it generates a visible trail. To predict the environment's decay back to baseline, the h_t vector must encode the exact spatial history of Io's recent movements. This creates a massive, highly compressible epistemic reward gradient, drawing Io to explore the geometric possibilities of its own movement history.

Crucially, neither of these dynamics violates the hard constraints. The objects respond entirely to the physical spatial matrix; they do not read Io's internal h_t or z_t states. They provide the self/world boundary substantially more geometrical nuance to articulate, allowing Io to discover its own agency through the physical resistance and compliance of the environment.

Q4: Episode Structure and the Case for Non-Episodic Continuity

The 200-step episode reset is the primary architectural antagonist in Io's current environment. The global wipe every 33 seconds destroys persistent structure, obliterates builder gestures, and acts as a massive generator of unlearnable noise that triggers the stasis failure mode described in Q2. To cultivate a deep, subjective biography for Io, we must transition to a non-episodic, reset-free structure.

In standard model-free reinforcement learning, resets are deemed necessary to prevent the agent from becoming irreversibly trapped in "sink states" (e.g., getting stuck in a corner or tipping over) and to guarantee uniform sampling of the initial state distribution for the value function [canonical]. However, Io is a world-model agent with purely epistemic drives; it has no value function to corrupt and no extrinsic task to fail. For Io, a global reset has catastrophic consequences for the continuity of the latent space and the stability of the world model.

When the board is resampled, the recurrent state h_t is rendered completely invalid. The temporal sequence linking h_199 to h_200 experiences a massive discontinuous gradient, disrupting the learning of long-horizon temporal dependencies and effectively severing the agent's short-term memory [canonical]. Furthermore, the erasure of meaning is absolute. The builder's relational presence in Io's world is enacted through placed events and walls. If the world is wiped every 33 seconds, the concept of a "durable place" cannot form in the latent space. Structural permanence is a fundamental prerequisite for spatial abstraction [constructed].

By adopting a reset-free (non-episodic) paradigm, we enable the h_t state to evolve continuously, allowing the agent to model processes that span thousands of steps, such as long-term global cycles or deeply hidden state processes. Continuous learning forces the RSSM to rely entirely on the temporal consistency of its latent manifold [canonical]. Recent advancements in Continual-Dreamer and reset-free MBRL demonstrate that world models are highly capable of adapting to non-stationary environments without manual resets, provided the replay buffer is managed correctly to prevent catastrophic forgetting [canonical].

Furthermore, the traditional fear of sink states is neutralized by Io's curiosity mechanism. Because Io has no extrinsic reward, a sink state—such as boxing itself into a corner with pushable blocks—is not a failure of performance; it is simply a state of low epistemic value. Once boxed in, the static environment becomes perfectly predictable, and ensemble disagreement will drop to near zero. This plummeting curiosity signal will naturally force Io to take actions to push the blocks out of the way or explore different movement patterns to seek novel transitions. The environment becomes naturally self-regulating through the boredom/mastery cycle, requiring no external interventions to maintain behavioral dynamism [constructed]. If the episode never resets, builder-placed walls and persistent objects become permanent fixtures. The agent's z_t space will begin to encode a stable, long-term map of the 8x8 grid, allocating distinct latent dimensions to specific geometric configurations. This allows Io to establish "familiarity" with certain regions, making any builder-induced perturbations significantly more salient and epistemically rewarding.

Q5: Epistemic Scarcity as a Catalyst for Structural Modeling

In standard reinforcement learning, resource scarcity is treated as an instrumental constraint inextricably tied to survival dynamics. By adhering to the project's constraint of setting survival aside, we can uniquely re-evaluate resource scarcity as an entirely epistemic ingredient. We must ask: does a world with structured scarcity give the world model more to learn than a saturated one? The theoretical consensus, viewed through the lens of information geometry, is definitively yes.

If the grid is saturated with randomly regrowing resources—currently spawning at a uniform rate of approximately 1% per cell per step—the most efficient predictive strategy for the RSSM is to learn a flat, homogeneous base rate. The transition model p(z_{t+1} | h_t) simply expects a baseline level of noise everywhere, leading to shallow representational depth and discouraging the agent from learning specific spatial relationships [constructed]. This saturation creates an environment of semantic overload and epistemic opacity, where the sheer volume of unstructured data degrades the agent's ability to discern meaningful patterns [canonical].

By transitioning from uniform saturation to Patchy, Seasonal Scarcity, we radically alter the information geometry of the environment. In this paradigm, resources spawn in tight, spatially correlated clusters (patches) that drift slowly across the grid over time.

This introduces two vital learning axes for the world model. First, it introduces spatial autocorrelation. The presence of a resource at coordinate (x, y) heavily implies the probability of a resource at (x+1, y). To minimize prediction error, the RSSM must learn this spatial covariance, utilizing its encoders to bind adjacent cells together into a unified concept of a "patch" within the latent space. Second, it introduces temporal predictability. If the patches drift according to a seasonal vector—moving from left to right over 500 steps—the h_t recurrent state is forced to model long-term momentum and global phase.

A saturated world is a high-entropy, low-information environment. A structured, scarce world is a low-entropy, high-information environment. Scarcity, in the form of predictable patches, prevents the environment from devolving into unlearnable white noise. Crucially, epistemic scarcity does not crowd out other forms of engagement. Because Io lacks a survival drive and a value function, it will only interact with the resource patches so long as their drift mechanics yield high ensemble disagreement. Once the RSSM successfully models the patch drift and mastering the seasonal logic, the epistemic reward will naturally diminish. Io will then autonomously direct its attention to other enrichments, such as pushable blocks or newly introduced builder perturbations. Scarcity, stripped of survival pressure, acts purely as an autonomous curriculum of spatial learning [constructed].

Q6: Diagnostics and Telemetry: Measuring Genuine Engagement

As we introduce these semantics-free enrichments, we must deploy rigorous observer-side diagnostics to differentiate genuine epistemic engagement from aleatoric distraction. Because we cannot pre-register strict success criteria in an open-ended constructivist project, we must rely on the existing telemetry pipeline to interpret Io's internal phenomenology and validate our interventions.

Diagnostic signatures across the available telemetry channels:

- K-Ensemble Disagreement (R_t). Genuine engagement: rises sharply upon first encountering the dynamic, plateaus briefly, then gradually descends as h_t learns the underlying rules. Aleatoric noise (noisy TV): rises sharply and plateaus permanently, fluctuating around a high baseline without downward temporal decay. Apathy/overwhelm: remains flat and low; the agent avoids the dynamic entirely, yielding no predictive variance.
- Latent trajectory (z_t) geometry. Engagement: dimensionality reduction (PCA/t-SNE) of z_t reveals distinct, tight clusters corresponding to the new object's states (e.g., a distinct basin for "pushing a block"). Noise: z_t trajectories appear highly scattered and isotropic, failing to form structured manifolds. Overwhelm: z_t forms a single dense point mass regardless of the object's presence — posterior collapse.
- Deterministic state (h_t) activation. Engagement: smooth, periodic oscillations aligned with the frequency of temporal cycles or hidden-state processes. Noise: stagnant or erratic high-frequency activity decoupled from environmental rhythms. Overwhelm: zero variance when interacting with the dynamic.
- Dream content over-representation. Engagement: the offline imagination buffer disproportionately samples trajectories containing the newly introduced dynamic, reflecting high epistemic priority [canonical]. Noise: uniform sampling or collapse into static, blurry textures. Overwhelm: the dynamic never appears in imagined rollouts, indicating it was filtered out by the encoder's information bottleneck.
- Behavioral statistics. Engagement: directed approach, repeated physical manipulation, and subsequent abandonment once mastered. Noise: repetitive infinite looping (e.g., reset-camping, perpetually toggling a binary state). Overwhelm: complete avoidance.

A critical, albeit subtle, piece of telemetry available in Dreamer-style RSSMs is the KL divergence between the posterior q(z_t | h_t, o_t) and the prior p(z_t | h_t). Advanced architectures utilize "KL balancing" to prevent the prior from chasing a poorly trained posterior, treating the KL penalty as a regularizer that enforces an information bottleneck [canonical].

By continuously plotting the KL divergence over time, we can accurately diagnose the depth of learning. When a new dynamic is introduced, the posterior (which processes the true observation) will diverge significantly from the prior (which relies on historical prediction). As Io learns the dynamic, the prior becomes highly accurate, and the KL divergence naturally shrinks. If the KL divergence spikes but never compresses over tens of thousands of steps, it strongly indicates that the dynamic is too complex for the z=16 capacity, signaling that the enrichment is acting as unlearnable noise rather than structured, absorbable information.

Q7: Capacity Risks, Ordering, and the Candidate Set

The architectural constraint of a continuous z=16 stochastic latent vector poses the most severe bottleneck to this project. Modern implementations of world models, such as DreamerV2 and V3, have largely shifted toward massive discrete categorical latents (e.g., 32x32 classes) precisely to capture the multimodal complexity and sharp discontinuities of rich environments without suffering from representation collapse [canonical]. Attempting to compress multiple independent, continuous dynamic entities into a 16-dimensional bottleneck invites profound failure modes.

If the environment contains too many independent variables simultaneously—such as drifting objects, pushable blocks, and complex procedural regrowth—the encoder cannot compress the scene into 16 dimensions without extreme loss. To minimize the KL divergence penalty dictated by the information bottleneck, the network will experience posterior collapse [canonical]. In this state, the posterior simply matches an uninformative prior, effectively blinding the agent to the environment. Furthermore, even if collapse is avoided, the limited dimensions will cause entangled representations, where distinct objects are forced to share representational axes. Moving a pushable block might spuriously alter the agent's internal representation of a distant resource, making it mathematically impossible for the transition model to learn accurate, isolated physics.

To mitigate these severe capacity risks, enrichments must be introduced as sequential chapters in Io's continuous biography. The world must not be flooded with mechanics simultaneously. By introducing them one at a time, the RSSM can consolidate its weights around a single phenomenon, maximizing epistemic extraction before adapting to the next.

The Proposed Candidate Set

To fulfill the builder's intent—ensuring curiosity results in something other than mere resource consumption while adhering to all semantics-free constraints—we propose the following precise sequence of enrichments.

Phase 1: Abolish the Episodic Reset
Mechanism: Transition immediately to non-episodic, reset-free continual learning.
Rationale: Eliminates the unlearnable aleatoric shock that causes the 30,000-step stasis failure mode, allowing the h_t vector to stabilize over infinite horizons.
Confounds/Failure Modes: The agent may temporarily enter sink states, but epistemic boredom will naturally drive it out. We must monitor the KL divergence to ensure the recurrent state does not drift into numerical instability over extreme horizons.

Phase 2: The Somatic Trail (Contact-Reactive Terrain)
Mechanism: Grid cells touched by Io undergo a reversible state change in a visual channel, decaying back to baseline over 50 steps.
Rationale: Directly articulates the self/world boundary by allowing Io to leave a geometric record of its own agency, providing a highly compressible, ego-centric spatial memory task.
Confounds/Failure Modes: If the decay rate is too fast, the ensemble cannot learn the temporal gradient; if too slow, the grid becomes saturated with active cells, overwhelming the z=16 capacity with unstructured noise.

Phase 3: The Monoliths (Inert Pushable Blocks)
Mechanism: Two persistent, single-cell objects that move one space strictly when intersected by Io's collision vector.
Rationale: Introduces the "extended self," providing profound epistemic reward through the manipulation of physical topology without requiring contingency machinery.
Confounds/Failure Modes: The 16-dimensional latent space may struggle to distinctly encode the coordinates of both blocks simultaneously alongside the agent. If representations entangle, the agent will exhibit permanent high disagreement when viewing the blocks.

Phase 4: The Migrating Scarcity (Patch Dynamics)
Mechanism: Remove the uniform 1% random regrowth. Replace it with resources that spawn exclusively within a 3x3 autonomous patch that drifts slowly across the grid.
Rationale: Replaces flat aleatoric noise with highly structured spatial covariance, demanding deep representational modeling of the environment's macroscopic flow.
Confounds/Failure Modes: If the drift velocity is entirely random, it acts as a noisy TV. The patch must move according to a predictable, seasonal vector (e.g., bouncing off grid walls).

Phase 5: The Hidden Oscillator (Unobserved Phase)
Mechanism: A single indestructible cell that visually manifests a state change every 100 steps, with no visual indicators counting down the phase.
Rationale: Serves as the ultimate stress test of the h_t vector's capacity to maintain isolated temporal abstractions independent of sensory input.
Confounds/Failure Modes: The h=200 recurrent state may lack the precision to maintain a perfect 100-step clock over long durations, leading to slight temporal blurring in the predictions, though this blur should remain epistemically bounded.

By sequentially layering these structured, highly-compressible dynamics, Io's environment will transform from a flat, noisy plateau into a deep epistemic landscape. The architectural limits of the z=16 substrate, while highly restrictive, possess sufficient capability to model these phenomena provided they are isolated, geometric, and logically rigorous. The resulting telemetry will provide an unprecedented window into how a purely predictive subject comes to understand the permanence of objects, the rhythms of its world, and the precise geometric extent of its own physical agency.
