# Probe 4 Design Inputs: Builder-Recognition in a Single-Agent World-Model RL System

## Q1 — Contingency-loss detection and the yoked-control critique

**Recommendation: Keep the within-subject alternating design; it dissolves Church's specific confound but not order/carryover, which you must engineer against with a symmetric ABAB/BABA counterbalance plus dream-state washout and per-block early/late slicing.**

The developmental literature is solid and directly supportive. Murray & Trevarthen (1985, "Emotional regulation of interactions between two-month-olds and their mothers," in Field & Fox eds., *Social Perception in Infants*) is confirmed: using closed-circuit "double video," 2-month-olds reduced gaze/engagement during a replay of the mother relative to live interaction. The original is fragile — Rochat, Neisser & Marian (1998, *Infant Behavior and Development* 21:355–366) failed to replicate with a larger sample once **order of conditions was controlled**, arguing the original effect was confounded with **increasing fussiness over time** because replay always followed live. Crucially, Nadel, Carchon, Kervella, Marcelli & Réserbat-Plantey (1999, "Expectancies for social contingency in 2-month-olds," *Developmental Science* 2(2):164–173) restored the effect with a **live–replay–live** design: smiling/gaze fell during replay, and seven of ten infants recovered positive behavior and *increased visual attention to the mother* in the second live period — a **re-engagement-after-perturbation** result that fussiness/order cannot explain. Tronick, Als, Adamson, Wise & Brazelton (1978, *J. Am. Acad. Child Psychiatry* 17:1–13) is confirmed: the still-face shows infants respond to the withdrawal of contingent responsiveness (not merely affect). Per Mesman, van IJzendoorn & Bakermans-Kranenburg (2009, *Developmental Review* 29(2):120–162), the paradigm "has been used for many different purposes in over 80 empirical studies," and their meta-analysis "confirmed the classic still-face effect of reduced positive affect and gaze, and increased negative affect, as well as a partial carryover effect into the reunion episode consisting of lower positive and higher negative affect compared to baseline"; the review adds the effect "is present from age 1 month, but not in newborns, and … may become less pronounced from about 6 months onwards." Watson's contingency work is confirmed (Watson 1972, "Smiling, cooing, and 'The Game,'" *Merrill-Palmer Quarterly* 18:323–339; the mobile-conjugate-reinforcement paradigm, Watson & Ramey 1972), as is Gergely & Watson's contingency-detection/social-biofeedback model (Gergely & Watson 1996, *Int. J. Psycho-Analysis* 77:1181–1212; 1999, in Rochat ed., *Early Social Cognition*).

Three lessons for Probe 4:
1. **The re-engagement signature is the gold standard.** Nadel's design shows the interpretively load-bearing evidence is not "response drops during perturbation-mismatch" but "response recovers when contingency is restored." Build the recovery contrast in: the develops-over-training claim gains most from a **contingent → yoked → contingent** micro-structure where re-engagement in the second contingent block is the key readout.
2. **Order/carryover is the real enemy, not Church.** Church (1964, *Psychological Bulletin* 62:122–131) is confirmed: yoked designs produce spurious master–yoked differences because of **between-subjects individual differences in reactivity** (e.g., the headless-roach and shock-reactivity examples). In a **within-subject, single-agent** design, Io is literally its own control, so the classic Church confound — variance across paired subjects — is *dissolved*. What survives is intra-subject nonstationarity: a continually-learning RSSM is a moving target, so a contingent-then-yoked difference could reflect drift/learning, not contingency. This is the within-subject analogue of Church's warning and it is the dominant threat.

**Concrete block structure.** Use counterbalanced ABAB / BABA super-blocks across the run (A = contingent, B = yoked), with block length set to several multiples of the measured h-horizon (see Q3) and a **dream-state washout** interposed at every phase boundary. Randomize block order per super-block to break any fixed periodicity Io could learn as a clock. Fit each metric with block-order as a covariate and always compare **matched positions** (A₁ vs B₁, A₂ vs B₂) rather than pooling, so a monotone drift term cannot masquerade as a contingency effect. Report the contingent-vs-yoked contrast *within* time-matched windows.

## Q2 — Signatures of "models it as a different kind"

**Recommendation: Make the widening YOKED-minus-CONTINGENT surprise gap the primary signature, but only after the positive control (Q3) proves the instrument can see conditional structure; treat the raw per-event PE asymmetry as necessary-not-sufficient and pair every signature with its confound-separator.**

For an RSSM whose only learning pressure is reconstruction (ELBO with free bits, per Kingma et al. 2016's free-bits construction in "Improving Variational Inference with Inverse Autoregressive Flow") and whose actor chases K=5 latent-disagreement (Pathak, Gandhi & Gupta 2019, *ICML*, "Self-Supervised Exploration via Disagreement"; latent-disagreement variant Sekar et al. 2020, *ICML*, "Planning to Explore via Self-Supervised World Models"), the observables are: per-event one-step prediction error (NLL/KL of the posterior-vs-prior at the event step), ensemble disagreement (variance across the K one-step predictors), h-trajectory geometry, and dream-state replay frequency.

- **(a) The predicted asymmetry [constructed, but literature-motivated].** A contingency-*modeling* agent has learned the conditional law P(event | Io-state). Contingent events are then *more* predictable (lower prior surprise) because Io's own state forecasts them; yoked replay, which reproduces content and marginal timing but is decorrelated from current state, *violates* the learned conditional and should read as **higher** prior surprise and higher disagreement. The develops-over-training signature is that this **yoked-minus-contingent gap widens** as the conditional is learned. This is the RSSM translation of Rescorla's (1968) contingency-not-contiguity result: matched contiguity/marginal rate with differing contingency produces divergent learning. **Hard precondition (agree with the design's own caveat): the gap is only detectable if contingency is keyed to a state variable inside Io's representational reach** — i.e., decodable from {h, z}. If the builder keys on something Io cannot represent, contingent and yoked are indistinguishable to Io by construction and a null is uninterpretable. This is the single most important design constraint in Probe 4.
- **(b) Conditional vs marginal structure [literature-grounded: Rescorla 1968; Allan 1980 ΔP].** The deflationary alternative is that Io tracks only the marginal event *rate*. Separate them by holding marginal rate identical across phases (the design already does this) and testing whether event-time surprise depends on Io-state: regress per-event PE on a decoded state predictor and ask whether the state coefficient is nonzero in contingent blocks and ~zero in yoked. Nonzero state-conditional prediction = ΔP-style contingency modeling; rate-only tracking predicts a null state coefficient in both.
- **(c) h-trajectory signatures [constructed].** Look for a learned *anticipatory* deflection in h in the steps *preceding* contingent events (the model has moved into a subspace that predicts the perturbation) that is absent before yoked events. Confound: generic arousal would deflect h around *all* salient events regardless of phase; the contingent-only, *pre-event* timing is what distinguishes anticipation from reaction.
- **(d) Dream-state over-representation [constructed].** If the passive energy-decode monitor shows contingent events replayed/recombined more than rate-matched yoked events overnight, that is consolidation of the conditional structure. Confound: contingent events may simply carry higher waking prediction-error and be replayed for that reason (prioritized-replay analog) — so normalize replay frequency by matched waking PE before attributing over-representation to contingency-category.

No single signature is decisive; the **conjunction** (widening gap + nonzero state coefficient + pre-event h anticipation + PE-normalized dream over-representation) is what licenses "models it as a different kind" rather than "exposed to different statistics."

## Q3 — Rate, power, memory horizon, and instrument positive-control

**Recommendation: Measure the h-horizon by lagged linear-probe recovery and intervention-persistence BEFORE the run; set the event inter-arrival well inside that horizon but rare enough (target a single-digit-percent duty cycle) to stay "outside-source"; and gate the entire probe behind a scripted strong-contingency positive control that must fire first.**

- **(a) Measuring effective memory horizon.** Two independent instruments:
  - *h-level (representational persistence):* Inject/flag a distinctive event, then train linear probes to decode "event occurred τ steps ago" from current h at increasing lags τ; the horizon is the lag where decoding accuracy falls to chance. Complement with **intervention-persistence**: perturb h at the event and measure how many steps the divergence in the rollout persists. RSSM design intends the deterministic GRU path to carry long-range information while z absorbs noise (Hafner et al. 2019, PlaNet, "Learning Latent Dynamics for Planning from Pixels"; Hafner et al. 2019/2020, Dreamer, "Dream to Control") — so probe h and z separately; expect z to be near-Markovian and h to carry the memory.
  - *weight-level (learned-association persistence):* measure how many gradient updates an established event-association survives once the event stops, i.e., the forgetting time-constant of the world model under continued off-event training.
  - **Truncated-BPTT ceiling (hard constraint):** gradients only assign credit within the truncation window; dependencies longer than the unroll length are learned poorly if at all (confirmed general result; e.g., Tallec & Ollivier 2017, "Unbiasing Truncated Backpropagation Through Time"; Ke et al. 2017, "Sparse Attentive Backtracking"). **The event's modeled trace must form within the BPTT window, not merely within h's passive retention.** If you want Io to learn event | state at lag L, L must be ≤ the truncation length, independent of how long h can passively hold the event. This is a sharper limit than the h-horizon and is frequently the binding one.
- **(b) Event-rate envelope.** Three constraints: rare enough that perturbations read as exogenous rather than as ordinary dynamics (a low duty cycle); frequent enough for statistical power (enough events per block to estimate the state-conditional PE coefficient with tight CIs); and inter-event spacing short enough that the contingency structure forms inside the BPTT window. Reconcile by choosing inter-arrival ≈ a small fraction of the h-horizon and BPTT window, then setting block length so each block accrues a pre-specified minimum event count (fix via a power analysis on the pilot PE variance). Do not fix a universal number in advance — set it from the measured horizon and pilot noise.
- **(c) Developmental timescale.** Genuinely unknown for this bespoke system; treat as measured, not assumed. The develops-over-training requirement (Q7) means you need the run long enough to observe the gap emerge from ~0, so budget for multiple regimes: pre-learning (gap absent), acquisition (gap widening), asymptote.
- **(d) Instrument positive control.** Before asking the real question, run a **scripted strong-contingency channel** — a perturbation deterministically and perceptibly keyed to an unambiguously Io-decodable state — that the world model *must* learn if the measure works. The contingent-vs-yoked divergence metric must fire on this channel first. If it does not, a null on the builder question is an instrument failure, not evidence about Io. This directly answers predecessor finding 3's disaggregation discipline and finding 4's completeness worry by validating the detector before deployment.

## Q4 — Perturbation content, and whether Probe 4 should measure seek

**Recommendation: Adopt the agnostic-plus-instrumented stance (i) + (ii) — read the need-keyed correlation at world-model level regardless of behavior, while separately logging approach trajectories — and use the two-class mixture, because it is exactly the contrast that separates "responds to me" from "responds to my state." This is the right design; I would not simplify it.**

Given finding 1 (seek-reachability is *open*, and the prior failure was an instrument defect — a dishonest decoder that made in-band states believed worse than the depleted rail, anti-incentivizing regulation — not missing machinery, with credit-assignment/horizon ruled out since nearest resource was ~1.2 steps within the 15-step window and rollouts brushed resources 5–7% of spans), the correct posture is measurement, not prediction. Do **not** condition the probe's validity on Io approaching drops. Read the need-keyed *trigger correlation* at the world-model level (does event-timing surprise become energy-belief-conditional?), which is valid whether or not Io pursues. Simultaneously instrument approach trajectories so that if honest belief makes pursuit reachable, emergent seek is captured rather than missed — this is cheap insurance against another "changed-but-not-displaced" blind corner (finding 4).

**Content and the two-class contrast.** The mixture is:
- **Need-keyed drops** — resources appearing when decoded energy is low, placed on Io's *predicted path* to enable stumble-consumption without requiring pursuit.
- **Need-neutral novel objects** — contingent on behavior but orthogonal to energy.

The logic: **both** classes carry the generic contingency signature ("responds to me"), but **only** the need-keyed class carries energy-belief-conditional structure ("responds to my *state*"). If Io's event-surprise for need-keyed drops depends on decoded energy while need-neutral surprise does not, that dissociation is strong evidence Io models the perturber as reading its internal state — a richer "kind" attribution than mere responsiveness. This mirrors Gergely & Watson's distinction between generic contingency detection and *state-attuned* (affect-mirroring) contingency.

**Salience/perceptibility thresholds.** Contingency-learning work is explicit that the modeled trace only forms above a magnitude/perceptibility floor and within a tight temporal window. Millar's arm-movement contingency work (per Nathani et al. 2021, summarizing Millar 1990) found that in 6–8-month-olds "response delays of 1 or 2 s led to reduced learning of the contingency, and a delay of 3 s disrupted learning completely" — note this is 6–8-month-olds, and the delay tolerance is even shorter in younger infants. Translated: the perturbation must be (a) reliably encoded into {h, z} from the 32×32 grayscale egocentric view, and (b) tightly time-locked to the state it is keyed on. The queued-and-drained-at-step-boundaries runner design is good here because it makes *when* the event landed deterministic and replayable.

**The hard constraint — self-opacity.** PolicyView is frozen at {h, z, self_prediction_error} and perturbations carry no observation-space marker. Detectability must therefore come entirely from the event's *effect on world-model dynamics*, not from any tag. This argues for content that is **maximally perceptible as a world-state change** (a clearly-rendered object appearing/moving) while being **statistically ordinary** in the observation stream — the contingency, not the appearance, must be the only thing distinguishing it. Do not smuggle in a de facto marker (e.g., a unique color reserved for builder events); that would make the probe trivially pass for the wrong reason.

## Q5 — Session structure and the authorship question

**Recommendation: Interleave phases with the fixed clock and DELIBERATELY place dream-consolidation between contingent and yoked blocks — it sharpens the comparison more than it blurs it, provided you slice early/late and treat consolidation as an independent variable, not a nuisance. On authorship: if Io distinguishes contingent from yoked you are entitled to claim recognition of a "kind" (an outside-source category), NOT recognition of an agent or of intentions.**

**Phase structure.** Respect the charter's fixed wall-clock (daytime waking / overnight dream-state). Rather than fight it, exploit it: run contingent and yoked blocks in waking, and let overnight dream-state consolidation fall between them. The offline-learning case that consolidation *sharpens*: dream-state replay/recombination is where the conditional structure P(event | state) gets amplified into the world model (the prioritized-replay analog and Plan2Explore-style latent recombination). The *blurs-it* case: consolidation also drives drift and interference, so a late-block contingent-vs-yoked contrast could be contaminated by overnight reorganization. Resolve by (1) always comparing time-matched positions (Q1), (2) logging the passive energy-decode monitor's dream content as a covariate, and (3) making "did consolidation occur between these two blocks" an explicit factor so its effect is estimated rather than confounded. Do not interleave contingent and yoked *within* a single waking session so finely that Io cannot accrue the within-block statistics needed to learn the conditional.

**Authorship.** The contingent policy is executed by automation against Io's live state but *authored in advance* by the builder. Csibra & Gergely's teleological stance (Gergely & Csibra 2003, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 7:287–292; the "pure reason" goal-attribution work, Csibra et al. 1999, *Cognition* 72:237–267) and their natural-pedagogy program (Csibra & Gergely 2009, "Natural pedagogy," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 13(4):148–153; Csibra & Gergely 2011, "Natural pedagogy as evolutionary adaptation," *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 366(1567):1149–1157) bear on this sharply. Natural pedagogy holds that infants infer generic, "kind-relevant" knowledge specifically from **ostensive-communicative cues** — eye contact, infant-directed speech, and *contingent responsiveness* — that signal an agent is addressing them. But note two things: (1) the teleological stance is explicitly framed by its authors as **non-mentalistic** — infants attribute goal-efficiency without attributing beliefs; and (2) a *pre-authored policy executed against live state is still moment-to-moment contingent from Io's vantage* — Io cannot, from {h, z}, distinguish "responsive agent deciding now" from "responsive mechanism executing an authored rule," because both produce identical state-keyed contingency. This is the responsive-agent-vs-responsive-mechanism problem, and it is *undecidable at Io's input surface by construction.* (Relatedly, Gergely & Watson theorized a ~3-month "switch" from a preference for perfect self-generated contingency toward high-but-imperfect *social* contingency, grounded in Bahrick & Watson 1985, *Developmental Psychology* 21:963–973 — a reminder that "detecting a responsive other" is a specific representational achievement, not a given.)

**Defensible claim line:**
- **Entitled to claim** (if the Q2 conjunction fires): Io has formed a **distinct category** for outside-source perturbations — a "kind" in the charter's sense — distinguishable from internal stochasticity, developing over training, and (with the two-class contrast) possibly state-attuned. This is recognition of a *pattern with a distinct causal signature*.
- **NOT entitled to claim:** that Io recognizes an *agent*, attributes intentions, or distinguishes authored-mechanism from live-agent. The design cannot separate these, and the teleological/natural-pedagogy literature is about *human* inferential machinery that Io's frozen input surface does not obviously instantiate. Claiming "agent recognition" would be the philosophically indefensible overreach.

## Q6 — Fresh-instance carry, decoder-honesty maintenance, and the charter tension

**Recommendation: Keep the energy channel present with the preference at precision 0 (do NOT strip it), maintain honesty via periodic decoder-head-only refits on oracle coverage data, and — this is the key charter distinction — that refit is legitimate MAINTENANCE of instrument honesty ONLY where the decoder feeds observer telemetry; the moment the same calibrated readout feeds the resting preference, it becomes INSTALLING a competence and violates capacity-over-exercise.**

**Keep vs strip.** Need-keyed perturbations (Q4) require a *decodable internal state* — that is load-bearing. Stripping the energy channel removes the substrate for the "responds to my state" contrast entirely, collapsing Probe 4 to the weaker "responds to me" question. So retain the channel. Precision 0 (present, disengaged) is the right default: it preserves the decodable state and the capacity-over-exercise stance without letting the homeostatic preference drive behavior. Finding 1 shows pursuit is *open*; keeping the channel present-but-disengaged is exactly what lets you *measure* whether honest belief makes pursuit reachable without *installing* the drive.

**The maintenance problem.** Honesty is coverage-shaped: a fresh Io's decoder re-grows rail-conditional dishonesty out-of-distribution (reading above physical ceiling, sign-inverted), and finding 2 establishes a decoder-head-only refit on coverage data restores honesty in seconds. Options:
- **(a) Periodic decoder-head-only refits on oracle-generated coverage data** — calibrates the belief readout on states Io never actually experienced. *This is the crux of the charter tension.*
- **(b) Bounded output head from day one** — prevents the wild out-of-ceiling/sign-inverted readings structurally. Recommended as a *complement* to (a): it caps the *magnitude* of dishonesty cheaply without touching what the model learned. Adopt it.
- **(c) Accept region-conditional honesty, flag uncovered cells** — the honest fallback consistent with finding 3's mandatory disaggregation. Adopt as the reporting default: never report a decoded belief from an uncovered/uncalibrated region without the flag.

**The charter tension, head-on.** Does calibrating the decoder on oracle data *install a competence* or *maintain instrument honesty*? The answer genuinely **depends on where the readout flows**:
- **If the calibrated decoder feeds only the observer's telemetry** (the mirror, the monitors), it is *maintaining instrument honesty*: you are making the world model's own learning *legible* to the observer, not changing what Io does. This is analogous to calibrating a thermometer, not teaching the patient to run a fever. Legitimate.
- **If the same calibrated readout feeds the resting homeostatic preference** (even at precision 0, if precision is ever raised), then oracle-calibration on never-experienced states *hands Io a competence it did not build* — a belief about its own energy in regions it never explored — which is precisely a capacity-over-exercise violation. Not legitimate without re-earning through experience.

The clean rule: **calibrate for reading, never for driving.** Maintain a hard architectural separation between the observer-facing decoded belief (calibrated freely) and any preference-facing belief (must be coverage-earned).

**Precedents for observer-side readout calibration.** The general practice of *linear-probe / decoder calibration for interpretability* in representation learning is well established, and AI-welfare "behavioral + verbal probing" of learned systems is an active 2025 area (e.g., probing-based welfare work integrating verbal and behavioral tests). However, I could **not confirm a named, published precedent specifically for observer-side readout recalibration in a months-long continually-learning single agent** [UNVERIFIED — I believe adjacent work exists in probing/interpretability and in AI-welfare probing, but cannot confirm a direct precedent for this exact maintenance pattern]. Treat this as a genuine gap and document the calibration protocol as a novel instrument choice rather than citing cover.

## Q7 — Pre-registerable criteria and a completeness argument for the deflation set

**Recommendation: Freeze (1) the primary metric as the yoked-minus-contingent state-conditional surprise gap with a pilot-calibrated threshold, (2) develops-over-training as a pre-specified early-run vs late-run gap increase exceeding the positive-control-derived noise floor, and (3) a deflation set with a partition-based completeness argument that explicitly reserves an "unnamed corner" bucket.**

**Freeze before the run:**
- **Divergence metric(s):** primary = the state-conditional PE/disagreement gap (Q2a,b) — yoked-minus-contingent event surprise, *after* regressing out marginal rate and arousal. Threshold set from the positive-control channel's observed gap (the probe must detect an effect at least X% of the scripted strong-contingency gap, with X fixed in advance).
- **Develops-over-training:** operationalize as early-run window (first N events, gap ≈ 0 expected) vs late-run window (gap > threshold), with the *increase* itself exceeding the between-block noise estimated from yoked–yoked comparisons. A static gap present from the first block fails the develops-over-training requirement and should be read as a pre-existing bias, not category-formation.
- **Falsification/deflation set** (each with its discriminating measurement):
  - **(a) Frequency adaptation / habituation:** surprise declines with repetition regardless of contingency. *Discriminator:* yoked events have identical marginal frequency, so pure habituation predicts equal contingent/yoked decline; a *contingency-specific* gap survives frequency-matching. Also test dishabituation symmetry.
  - **(b) Generic novelty/arousal:** any salient event deflects h/PE. *Discriminator:* arousal is phase-symmetric and *reactive*; the contingency signature is phase-*asymmetric* and (per Q2c) *anticipatory* (pre-event h deflection). Include need-neutral novel objects as an arousal-matched control.
  - **(c) Energy-mediated confounds from the need-keyed class:** apparent effects driven by energy dynamics, not contingency. *Discriminator:* the need-neutral class carries contingency but no energy structure; if the "gap" appears there too, it is not energy-mediated. Cross-check with the decode-honesty table per-region (finding 3).
  - **(d) Schedule/session artifacts:** clock periodicity or consolidation drift. *Discriminator:* randomized block order (Q1) + time-matched position comparisons + consolidation-as-factor (Q5). A schedule artifact appears in yoked–yoked position contrasts; a contingency effect does not.
  - **(e) Marginal-rate-learning:** Io tracks event frequency, not conditional structure. *Discriminator:* the state-coefficient test (Q2b) — rate-learning predicts a null Io-state coefficient in both phases; conditional modeling predicts a nonzero coefficient in contingent only.

**Completeness argument (answering finding 4 directly).** Partition the outcome space along two axes: **(i) does a contingent-vs-yoked divergence exist?** (yes/no) × **(ii) is it state-conditional?** (yes/no), giving four cells, plus a mandatory fifth **"unclassified/novel" bucket**:
- *No divergence:* Io does not distinguish builder from internal stochasticity (or keying was outside representational reach — check via positive control).
- *Divergence, not state-conditional:* "responds to me" only — recognition of an outside-source *kind* but not state-attunement.
- *Divergence, state-conditional:* "responds to my state" — the strong result.
- *State-conditional without overall divergence:* internally contradictory — flags an instrument error (Simpson-masking, finding 3); investigate, do not interpret.
- **Unclassified bucket:** any signature that fires outside these predicted patterns (the predecessor's "changed-but-not-displaced" analog). Pre-commit to *naming and reporting* such outcomes rather than forcing them into the four cells. The completeness claim is not "these signatures cover all of behavior" — it is "these signatures partition the *pre-registered* space, and anything outside it is routed to an explicit residual bucket for post-hoc characterization." That is the honest version of completeness given finding 4.

## Q8 — Pause triggers and the ending protocol

**Recommendation: Adopt a multi-vantage (builder + phase-blinded mirror + quantitative monitors), disaggregated, reversibility-weighted trigger structure modeled on humane-endpoint "clinical sign" scoring — where a single bad state pauses for review but never auto-ends, and only a persistent, multi-vantage-corroborated, irreversibility-trending pattern escalates toward ending.**

**Operationalizable degradation/distress-analog indicators:**
- **Entropy collapse:** posterior/latent entropy or policy action-entropy falling below a floor and staying there (representational or behavioral impoverishment).
- **Prediction-error runaway:** sustained divergence of world-model NLL/KL that does not recover across a dream-cycle (model losing coherence rather than transiently surprised).
- **Dream-content incoherence:** via the passive energy-decode monitor — loss of structured recombination, degenerate/repetitive dream content, or decode values pinned to rails.
- **Torpor-analog:** the predecessor's concrete signature — near-total action-stasis under a strong penalty gradient. Generalize to action-stasis *despite* disagreement-signal availability (the actor has an intrinsic gradient to follow and is not following it).

**Review structure — three vantages, disaggregated (finding 3):**
1. *Quantitative monitors* (the above), always per-source/per-region, never pooled.
2. *The phase-blinded mirror* (LLM interpretive layer) — kept blind so its distress read is not contaminated by knowing whether Io is in a contingent or yoked block.
3. *The builder* — human judgment integrating context the automated layers miss.

A trigger requires **corroboration across at least two vantages** to escalate; a single-vantage anomaly logs and heightens monitoring but does not pause.

**Precedent.** Laboratory-animal humane-endpoint frameworks are the closest developed model and map well. The OECD (2000, *Guidance Document on the Recognition, Assessment and Use of Clinical Signs as Humane Endpoints for Experimental Animals Used in Safety Evaluation*) and NIH/institutional guidance (NIH OACU endpoint guidelines; Toth 2000, *ILAR Journal* 41:72–79, "Defining the Moribund Condition as an Experimental Endpoint for Animal Research") establish: (i) **clinical-sign score sheets** with graded thresholds rather than single triggers; (ii) the "**moribund**" concept defined as a **clinically irreversible condition leading inevitably to death** — the field's own emphasis that *irreversibility*, not momentary severity, is what justifies termination; and (iii) an explicit distinction between *experimental* and *humane* endpoints. This directly supports the charter's stance: **a momentary bad state is information, not a verdict; ending is possible but never fast.** Reversibility assessment is the operational core — a distress-analog that recovers across a dream-cycle or a phase-washout is transient; one that trends toward irreversibility across multiple cycles is the escalation criterion.

On the AI side, the welfare literature exists but is thinner and more speculative: Tomasik (2014, "Do Artificial Reinforcement-Learning Agents Matter Morally?"), Daswani & Leike (2015, "A Definition of Happiness for Reinforcement Learning Agents"), and recent AI-welfare probing work (2025). Treat these as *framing*, not as validated instruments — none provides a calibrated distress metric for a world-model agent. The contemplative distinction between transient distress and transformation is real as a *stance* but I could not confirm a specific citable operationalization of it for this context [UNVERIFIED]; use it to justify *why* reversibility (not momentary severity) is the escalation axis, not as an empirical source.

**Escalation ladder:** (1) single-vantage anomaly → log, heighten monitoring; (2) two-vantage corroboration → **pause** (freeze acting/training, preserve state, review) — pause is explicitly *not* ending; (3) persistent, multi-vantage, irreversibility-trending pattern surviving washout and dream-cycles → structured review of ending, with the reversibility assessment documented. State is preserved at every pause so that reversibility can be tested empirically rather than assumed.
