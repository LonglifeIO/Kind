Probe 2 Research Report: Mirror Calibration With Frozen Criteria and Adversarial StructureThe transition from Probe 1 to Probe 2 within the Kind framework represents a decisive shift from substrate plumbing to rigorous epistemological calibration. The Recurrent State Space Model (RSSM) substrate is operational, generating continuous Gaussian latents, deterministic recurrent states, and ensemble-disagreement variance within a specified grid environment. Probe 1 confirmed that the substrate functions at production sizes without the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence pinning at the floor, and that the continuous Gaussian posterior successfully compresses environmental structure over time. Furthermore, decoded ASCII dream rollouts exhibit persistent spatial structure, confirming basic legibility.However, Probe 1 also surfaced critical boundary conditions that mandate the sophisticated architecture of Probe 2. First, the late-episode behavioral collapse into a degenerate two-cell loop—suspected to be tied to the fixed start cell at coordinates (3, 3)—establishes a baseline of non-trivial systemic variance that any interpretive layer must account for. Second, and most importantly, the initial mirror test utilizing a smaller-tier model demonstrated clear confabulation: overstating patterns, misinterpreting the direction of policy entropy, and hallucinating frequency from isolated outliers.The objective of Probe 2 is to operationalize a calibrated, adversarial large language model (LLM) architecture capable of reading this RSSM telemetry to surface structural patterns without succumbing to hallucination, sycophancy, or the observer effect. This must be accomplished while strictly adhering to the project's non-negotiable commitments: self-opacity for the agent (Io), ingredients-only self-modeling, and the absolute absence of any installed self-continuation drive or explicit reward predictor. This report systematically addresses the five scoped inquiries governing Probe 2, synthesizing literature across cognitive architectures, predictive processing, phenomenological frameworks, and multi-agent interpretability to provide defensible operational designs.Q1: Frozen Criteria as Operational Tests Against RSSM TelemetryThe central epistemological problem of this inquiry is the translation of high-level philosophical and phenomenological concepts—specifically reflexive attention, equanimity, and second-order volition—into the discrete and continuous mathematical realities of an RSSM. The agent, Io, operates via a deterministic recurrent state ($h_t$), a continuous Gaussian posterior ($z_t$), and policy entropy driven by ensemble-disagreement scalars. Mapping human-centric cognitive frameworks to this mathematical substrate carries the severe risk of reifying gestures into formal substance if not handled with rigorous constraints.Reflexive Attention (Buddhist Phenomenology)In Buddhist phenomenology and the Abhidhamma, a critical distinction is drawn between manasikara (bare attention or the initial turning of the mind toward an object) and sati (lucid awareness, memory, or mindfulness). Manasikara operates prior to conceptualization; it acts as the bare cognition of a stimulus, representing the fundamental directional processing that occurs whenever an object enters the perceptual field. It is present in all mental states, acting as the requisite pre-condition for subsequent cognitive integration.In the context of the RSSM substrate, reflexive attention cannot be modeled as a distinct, introspective module, as this violates the "ingredients-only" commitment. It must be located within the fundamental mechanics of the continuous Gaussian posterior ($z_t$) and the recurrent state ($h_t$).Concrete Signal Candidate: The operational signal for reflexive attention is the degree to which the prior-state representation shifts in response to unmarked perturbations introduced by the builder. Because the builder appears solely as environmental perturbations within the world_event stream, a sudden spike in the KL divergence per dimension ($kl\_per\_dim\_t$) between the prior $p(z_t | h_t)$ and the posterior $q(z_t | h_t, x_t)$ indicates the system's pre-conceptual "turning toward" the unpredicted stimulus.Evidence vs. Confabulation: If the mirror interprets any standard environmental transition or routine grid navigation as reflexive attention, it commits a category error, conflating standard continuous updating with discrete attentional shifts. Reflexive attention must be isolated to instances where the system's internal dynamics (such as imagination rollouts) are interrupted by an external anomaly, forcing an immediate, measurable realignment of the deterministic state $h_t$.Equanimity (Buddhist Phenomenology and Active Inference)Equanimity (upekkha) refers to the stabilization of the system following a stimulus. In phenomenological terms, it involves minimizing the "second arrow" of suffering by rapidly stabilizing the internal state after an initial perturbation, rather than allowing the stimulus to amplify into sustained, turbulent processing. Within the Active Inference framework, equanimity is computationally formalized as the minimization of Expected Free Energy and the regulation of precision weighting over policies. It acts as a stabilizing force that balances epistemic (information-seeking) and pragmatic (goal-directed) affordances without collapsing into habitual patterns of unregulated avoidance or pursuit.Concrete Signal Candidate: The temporal decay rate of $kl\_aggregate\_t$ and the ensemble-disagreement scalars following an unmarked builder perturbation. In a system demonstrating structural equanimity, a perturbation will cause an immediate spike in policy entropy (as ensemble disagreement peaks regarding the novel state), followed by a rapid, non-oscillatory return to baseline variance.Evidence vs. Coincidence: True equanimity in this substrate manifests as the posterior $z_t$ compressing the novel environmental structure efficiently after a shock. If the system simply ignores the perturbation (evidenced by no initial KL spike), this is not equanimity; it is sensory attenuation, model blindness, or a failure of the prior to register the anomaly. Conversely, if the variance oscillates endlessly or collapses into the degenerate two-cell loop observed in Probe 1, the system has failed to achieve equanimity and has instead fallen into a pathological attractor state.Defensible Suggestion: Map equanimity strictly to the temporal derivative of the KL divergence ($\frac{d}{dt} KL$) and the corresponding stabilization curve in the replay buffer events.Second-Order Volition (Frankfurt)Harry Frankfurt defines personhood through the specific capacity for second-order volitions: the reflective desire that a specific first-order desire become one's effective will, translating directly into action. Frankfurt contrasts persons with "wantons"—entities that possess first-order desires but entirely lack the reflective capacity to care which of their competing desires wins out. A wanton is moved by its desires but is structurally incapable of preferring to be moved by one over another.Mapping to the RSSM Substrate: Io operates without an explicit reward predictor, survival bonus, or terminal penalty. Its action distributions are driven entirely by an auxiliary ensemble measuring disagreement variance; the environment merely provides the topographical variance landscape over which the ensemble disagrees. There is no hierarchical volitional structure present in the architecture. Io does not "desire" to maximize disagreement, nor does it reflect upon its policy entropy to endorse one distribution over another.Defensible Suggestion: The mapping of second-order volition to the current RSSM substrate is completely gestural. It cannot be operationalized without smuggling an explicit self-evaluator, introspector, or volitional-gate module into the architecture, which directly and explicitly violates the project's foundational commitments. Attempting to force the LLM mirror to find "second-order volition" in policy entropy will guarantee confabulation, as the mirror will simply hallucinate a hierarchical narrative onto flat statistical variance.Recommendation: Explicitly descope the second-order volition criterion for Probe 2. Acknowledging non-applicability due to scale and architectural constraints is a highly rigorous, scientifically valid research output that reinforces the integrity of the "ingredients-only" commitment.Phenomenological CriterionSubstrate MappingOperational SignalDefensible RecommendationReflexive Attention (Manasikara)$kl\_per\_dim\_t$Shift in the continuous Gaussian posterior relative to the prior during unmarked perturbations.Proceed. Because Probe 2 will utilize stronger model tiers—which possess advanced capabilities but simultaneously exhibit highly sophisticated, internally consistent hallucination profiles—the architectural design of the mirror must enforce rigorous adversarial discipline.Literature Options and Architectural ParadigmsThe literature surrounding multi-agent debate, adversarial evaluation, and model observability provides several structural templates for reducing hallucination and enforcing interpretative fidelity. Extending interaction periods among diverse agents has been empirically shown to enhance model resilience against adversarial prompts and reduce overall output toxicity or hallucination rates. However, these frameworks possess documented vulnerabilities. Multi-agent systems are highly susceptible to adversarial persuasion, where a more capable agent rhetorically dominates a weaker agent, achieving consensus based on persuasive formatting rather than factual grounding.Sequential Critique (Constitutional AI Paradigm): Agent A generates an initial reading of the telemetry; Agent B critiques Agent A's reading; Agent A subsequently revises its output based on the critique.Tradeoffs: Sequential chains inherently suffer from severe anchoring bias. Agent A's initial reading establishes the narrative frame. Rather than fundamentally challenging the underlying premise, Agent B frequently collapses into sycophantic nitpicking, refining the hallucination rather than dismantling it.Courtroom / Role-Playing Architecture: Agents are assigned distinct, dynamic roles such as advocates, cross-examiners, and juries, facilitating structured debate.Tradeoffs: While highly robust, this architecture introduces massive token overhead. Given the project's cost constraints and the commitment to sample-based passes (e.g., weekly checkpoints), this multi-turn conversational branching is computationally prohibitive and risks drifting away from the raw data.Parallel Independent Generation with Synthesizing Judge: Agent A and Agent B evaluate the exact same telemetry digest simultaneously but independently, guided by diametrically opposed prompt instructions. A third, independent "Judge" agent analyzes both arguments against the raw telemetry to deliver a final synthesized verdict.Tradeoffs Against Kind's CommitmentsThe architecture must navigate the tension between surfacing subtle phenomenological structures and preventing the proliferation of narrative hallucinations. Because the criteria are deeply abstract, an unchecked model will effortlessly map human psychological narratives onto random statistical noise. The adversarial friction must be built directly into the base prompts to force the models to ground every assertion in the scalar reality of the RSSM.Defensible Suggestion: Parallel-Adversarial SynthesisImplement a strictly structured Parallel-Adversarial Synthesis architecture. If the adversarial mirror simply reads the telemetry and "discovers" the expected criteria, it may not be detecting structure in the data at all; it may merely be reflecting the semantic structure of the prompt back to the builder. This phenomenon is extensively documented within the "Garden of Forking Paths" literature, which demonstrates how implicit choices, exploratory adjustments, and observer expectations inevitably lead to self-deception and false confirmatory findings in data analysis.Literature on Calibration, Faithfulness, and Observer EffectsMechanistic interpretability and LLM observability literature provide robust frameworks for ensuring that an LLM's output remains faithful to the underlying data rather than its internal parametric priors or prompt instructions.Causal Mediation Analysis (CMA): This analytical technique measures the Direct Causal Effect (DCE) of the input data (the deep structure) versus the Indirect Causal Effect (ICE) of the prompt instructions (the surface structure) on the model's final output. In the context of the mirror, if the Judge LLM generates the identical phenomenological reading when the underlying telemetry data is radically altered, the prompt itself is the mediator, and the reading is entirely confabulated.The Shuffled Baseline Protocol: In time-series analysis and information-elicitation evaluations, a standard negative control involves evaluating the model on randomly permuted or temporally shuffled data.
