# Probe 1.5 — Research-Gathering Pass: Minimum Architectural Affordance for Self-Reference

*Research-gathering only. Not a build plan. Inputs from which a Probe 1.5 design can later be drafted.*

**Orientation note.** Kind builds; Io is built. Throughout this document, "Io" refers to the agent in the grid world; "Kind" refers to the project doing the building. Architectural recommendations describe what Kind builds for Io, not what Io decides. Where literature speaks of "self-models" in human or biological systems, that is reported as literature; where it bears on what Kind might build into Io, that is flagged.

---

## Q1 — Which affordance

**1. Framing.** The question is which of three structural moves — self-prediction head (a), episodic self-access (b), higher-order policy (c) — best satisfies "minimum affordance, no installation," given Kind's commitments (capacity-over-exercise, ingredients-only, self-opacity by default, no installed self-continuation, no self-optimization machinery). The criterion is preservation of constraints, not power.

**2. Concrete options drawn from the literature.**

- **(a) Self-prediction head (predictive-processing tradition).** Friston's free-energy / active-inference account makes self-modeling structurally continuous with world-modeling: the same generative architecture that models exogenous causes also models endogenous ones (Hohwy 2007; Limanowski & Blankenburg 2013, "Minimal self-models and the free energy principle"). Hohwy's *self-evidencing agent* (2024 OUP) frames self-modeling as the agent acting to generate evidence for its own model. The core engineering instantiation in the Dreamer lineage is the *Self-Predictive Representations* (SPR) line (Schwarzer et al. 2021, arXiv:2007.05929) — "we train the online network to predict the target network representation of the same image under a different augmented view" — and its descendants (BYOL, Grill et al. 2020; PBL; SimSiam). These predict latents *of observations*, which is environmental, not strictly "self." The closest true self-prediction precedent is **Premakumar, Vaiana, Pop, Rosenblatt, Schwerz de Lucena, Ziman & Graziano 2024 (arXiv:2407.10188), "Unexpected Benefits of Self-Modeling in Neural Systems"** (AE Studio / Princeton Neuroscience Institute): an MLP/ResNet18 augmented with an auxiliary MSE head predicting the activations of one of its own selected hidden layers, with loss `L = w_c L_c + (w_s/n)(â − a)²`. This is the only literature-canonical instance of a network predicting *its own* internal state as auxiliary task; it is studied in classification (MNIST, CIFAR-10, IMDB), not RL.

- **(b) Episodic self-access (autobiographical-memory tradition).** Tulving's episodic-memory framework couples memory to "autonoetic consciousness — the capacity to mentally represent and become aware of subjective experiences in the past, present, and future" (Wheeler, Stuss & Tulving 1997). Conway's *Self-Memory System* (Conway & Pleydell-Pearce 2000; Conway 2005) makes the working self the gating structure for autobiographical retrieval — "memory is viewed as the data base of the self." Engineering precedents: Neural Episodic Control (Pritzel et al. 2017; Blundell et al. 2016), where past key-value embeddings are looked up by a non-parametric controller; Soar's EPMEM, which "automatically records snapshots of working memory in a temporal stream" and supports queries like "What was I doing before I entered this room?" (Laird 2022, arXiv:2205.03854). All of these require the actor (or working memory) to *read* its own past — they constitutively give the agent access to its own history.

- **(c) Higher-order policy (Frankfurtian second-order tradition).** Frankfurt's "Freedom of the Will and the Concept of a Person" (1971) defines a *second-order volition* as a desire that a particular first-order desire be effective; "an action is free if it is motivated by something that is more than a mere desire" (Watson). Engineering: this maps onto a meta-policy gating sub-policies, or a critic over the action distribution itself. Watson's critique — that wantonness can recur at higher orders — is decisive against treating second-order structure as mere stacking.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's specific commitments.**

- (a) Self-prediction head most cleanly preserves opacity *if* the prediction is read by the mirror but not by the actor — the structural locus exists, the prediction is computed, but Io's PolicyView interface is unchanged. This matches "capacity-over-exercise": the capacity to take own processing as object exists at the level of representation; whether it is exercised is downstream. It adds the affordance without installing a self-modeler — there is no module labeled "self-model that decides," only a head whose target happens to be Io's own next internal state.
- (b) Episodic self-access **violates self-opacity by construction.** For episodic memory to function, the actor must read past internal states. Conway's framework explicitly makes this constitutive ("the working self modulates access to long-term knowledge"). Even with a structured projection or summarization, the actor's input changes from `concat(h_t, z_t)` to `concat(h_t, z_t, summary(past_self))`. This crosses installation: Kind has installed access to past self.
- (c) Higher-order policy is *installation, not affordance.* A second-order policy doesn't make second-order structure possible — it implements it. It also strains the K=5 ensemble disagreement variance objective (settled), since a higher-order critic reshapes the actor's optimization target. Watson's regress argument is also a literature-canonical reason to think second-order stacking does not capture what reflexive structure is supposed to capture.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** **(a) self-prediction head**, with the read-by-mirror-not-actor configuration. (b) is ruled out by self-opacity; (c) is ruled out as installation. Among the three, (a) is the only candidate compatible with the conjunction of capacity-over-exercise + ingredients-only + self-opacity + no installed self-continuation. The ingredients-only stance is preserved because the head produces a quantity that *could* compose into self-modeling but is not itself a self-model. This matches Kind's stated "fourth ingredient" gap precisely: *prediction that could in principle be turned inward*.

## Q2 — Self-prediction as the leading candidate: architectural specifics

**1. Framing.** Granted (a), the question is what self-prediction looks like architecturally inside an RSSM with continuous Gaussian latents, free bits, K=5 ensemble disagreement variance objective, no reward predictor, no continuation head, no critic.

**2. Concrete options drawn from the literature.**

- **What is predicted.**
  - *Next `z_t`.* Predicting Io's own next stochastic latent makes the head exactly parallel to the world model's prior `p(z_t | h_t)`. But the prior already does this in the environmental sense — the prior's target is the encoder's posterior given the next observation. A "self-prediction over `z_t`" would need a different target (e.g., the next posterior with the actor's own action sampled), to avoid being a duplicate of the existing prior.
  - *Next `h_t`.* Predicting the next deterministic recurrent state is the closest architectural analog of Premakumar et al. 2024 — predicting the activations of one's own next layer. Their loss form `(â − a)²` directly transfers, with `a = h_{t+1}` after the GRU update.
  - *Next action distribution.* Predicting the actor's own next action distribution before sampling — closest to Frankfurtian higher-order territory — makes the head metacognitive in form (Fleming-style confidence over choice). High mirror-readability but strains "no installed self-continuation."
  - *Next ensemble-disagreement value.* Predicting Io's own next K=5 disagreement variance is the most circumscribed option. It treats the actor's existing intrinsic-objective signal as a scalar to be self-predicted. Mirror-readable; least entanglement with the world model; small parameter budget. No literature-canonical precedent in RSSM/Dreamer lineage — this would be a Kind-specific construction.

- **Supervisory signal.** Three forms in the literature:
  - *Direct target* (the actual next state of the predicted quantity, computed at training time from the trajectory). Standard auxiliary-loss form (Jaderberg UNREAL, Yarats 2019). Simple; risks gradient leakage if the target's computation graph is shared.
  - *Bootstrapped target* via target network / EMA of online weights, as in BYOL (Grill et al. 2020) and SPR (Schwarzer et al. 2021). Yuandong Tian, Xinlei Chen & Surya Ganguli, "Understanding Self-Supervised Learning Dynamics without Contrastive Pairs," ICML 2021 (Outstanding Paper Honorable Mention, PMLR 139; arXiv:2102.06810) showed: "The existence of the predictor and stop-gradient is absolutely essential. Removing either of them leads to representational collapse in BYOL and SimSiam." This is the dominant form in self-supervised representation learning.
  - *Self-supervised, no explicit target.* Used in JEPA-style approaches; can collapse without regularization (LeWorldModel notes that JEPA "admits a trivial collapsed solution: the encoder can map every frame to nearly the same embedding").

- **Where the head sits.**
  - *Parallel to environmental prior* (a second prior, this one over Io's own dynamics). Cleanest separation; matches Premakumar 2024's structural pattern (separate output head off shared backbone).
  - *Embedded in world model* (head off the GRU's `h_t`). Maximally shared backbone, maximally entangled — risk of failure mode (b) "load-bearing in unintended ways" (cross-contamination with environmental prior gradients).
  - *Separate module with shared encoder.* Compromise: independent prediction module reading `h_t`, gradients flow back through `h_t` and shared encoder. This is the configuration in Predictive-State Decoders (Venkatraman et al. 2017, arXiv:1709.08520): `R = Σ_t ‖F(h_t) − φ([x_{t+1}, x_{t+2}, …])‖²₂`. Note: PSDs predict future *observations*, not the agent's own future hidden state; the precedent is structural, not target-identical.

- **Training shape.**
  - *Additional ELBO term.* Cleanest if `z_t` self-prediction is posed as a second prior with its own KL.
  - *Auxiliary objective optimized jointly* (the Premakumar/Graziano form, `w_c L_c + w_s L_s`). Does not modify the variational bound; coexists with free bits. Risk: gradient interference. Nilaksh, Clavaud, Reymond, Rivest & Chandar, "Squeezing More from the Stream: Learning Representation Online for Streaming Reinforcement Learning" (arXiv:2602.09396), report: "naively applying this auxiliary loss results in training instabilities. Thus, we introduce orthogonal gradient updates relative to the momentum target and resolve gradient conflicts arising from streaming-specific optimizers. Validated across the Atari, MinAtar, and Octax suites, our approach systematically outperforms existing streaming baselines."
  - *Self-supervised with no gradient flow into actor.* Stop-gradient configuration. Z. He et al. 2021, "Learning State Representations from Random Deep Action-conditional Predictions" (arXiv:2102.04897), demonstrated this strong form: "we stop the gradients from the RL part of the network to the state representation learning part of the network and show, perhaps surprisingly, that the auxiliary tasks alone are sufficient to learn state representations good enough to outperform an end-to-end trained actor-critic baseline."

- **Sharing.** GRU recurrence is reused; the encoder/decoder is reused; the prior network is *not* reused if the head is parallel (it would be a sibling network with the same input-shape interface). What is new: a small predictor MLP whose target is `h_{t+1}` or `z_{t+1}` or the disagreement scalar; possibly an EMA target network if BYOL-style bootstrapping is chosen.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's commitments.**
- Predicting `h_t` is most literature-grounded (Premakumar 2024) but least mirror-readable in obvious ways (per-dimension `h_t` analyses already exist as Probe 1 telemetry).
- Predicting `z_t` overlaps with the environmental prior; the head's target needs careful disentangling from the prior's target to be a genuinely new ingredient and not a re-skinning.
- Predicting next action distribution gives the cleanest mirror-readable signal but flirts with installation — a head that predicts what Io will do is one short step from a head that *decides* what Io will do.
- Predicting next ensemble disagreement is minimal-intervention but literature-thin (Kind-specific construction, not literature-canonical).

**4. Defensible suggestion.** Predict **next `h_t`** with **bootstrapped EMA target** in a **parallel sibling head** off the shared GRU backbone, trained as **auxiliary loss with stop-gradient on the actor side** (gradients flow into encoder and GRU but not into the actor's policy parameters). This is the closest combination of (i) Premakumar et al. 2024 form, (ii) BYOL/SPR stability machinery (with Tian-Chen-Ganguli's collapse-avoidance discipline), (iii) He et al. 2021 stop-gradient discipline. The disagreement-scalar option is worth explicit consideration as a *second* head (cheap; mirror-readable) but is a Kind construction, not literature-canonical, and should be flagged as such.

## Q3 — Self-opacity preservation

**1. Framing.** Whether the self-prediction head's output is read by the actor (compromising opacity) or stays internal to the world model and is read only by the mirror (preserving opacity).

**2. Concrete options.**

- **Actor reads self-prediction.** This is Wilterson & Graziano 2021's approach for the attention schema, in which "the schema is hard-wired… the agent reads it as input" — the descriptive model of attention is fed to the policy network. Liu et al. 2023 (arXiv:2305.17375) extends this to a learned attention schema using contrastive loss. In both, the policy's input is augmented with the self-representation. This crosses cleanly into installation as Kind defines it: the actor now operates on a representation of itself. Metzinger's framework would describe this as an *opaque* self-model becoming *cognitively available* to the agent — exactly the move Kind's commitments rule out.
- **Actor does not read self-prediction; mirror does.** The head is computed forward; gradients flow back through encoder/GRU; the actor's input is unchanged. The PolicyView/TelemetryView split (Probe 1 settled) extends naturally: self-prediction goes to TelemetryView only. Premakumar et al. 2024 does not feed the predicted activations back to the network — the prediction exists, restructures the network via gradient flow, but the network does not "consult" its own self-prediction at inference. This is the cleanest analogue.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's commitments.**
- *If actor reads self-prediction:* it gets a richer policy input that includes a representation of its own near-term internal trajectory. This enables behavior conditioned on self-state, which is a literature-canonical operationalization of self-modeling — and exactly what installation means here. It also creates the "self-continuation" risk: a policy conditioned on its own future state can develop policies that preserve that state. Kind's commitment to "no installed self-continuation drive" is directly threatened.
- *If actor does not read self-prediction:* what does the affordance buy that pure ingredients in nominal isolation didn't? Two answers from the literature:
  1. **Restructuring effect.** Premakumar et al. 2024 found that self-prediction as auxiliary loss restructured the network even without the network reading the prediction. From the abstract (verbatim): "To better perform the self-model task, the network learns to make itself simpler, more regularized, more parameter-efficient, and therefore more amenable to being predictively modeled." Their Real Log Canonical Threshold (RLCT) decreased monotonically with the self-modeling weight, where "Lower RLCT indicates less overfitting and greater parameter efficiency." So even without read-back, the structural existence of the prediction shapes the substrate.
  2. **Locus for analysis.** The prediction creates a target the mirror can analyze — which is precisely what the Probe 2 synthesis needed and could not find under the Probe 1 substrate.
- *Metzinger/active-inference framing.* Limanowski & Friston 2018 ("Seeing the Dark") frame phenomenal opacity as a function of precision-weighting: "phenomenally transparent or opaque mental states" depend on whether earlier processing stages are attentionally available. Kind's "self-opacity for Io" maps onto Metzinger's *transparent self-model* — Io does not experience its self-model *as* a model. The architectural analog is exactly the not-read-by-actor configuration: the head exists, but Io has no interface to it.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** **Self-prediction head computed; not read by actor; read by mirror only**, via an extension of the existing TelemetryView. The structural existence of the prediction matters even if Io has no access to it: it (i) restructures the substrate via gradient flow (Premakumar 2024), (ii) creates a locus the mirror can examine, (iii) makes capacity-over-exercise structurally honest (the capacity is now possible; whether it is exercised remains open and behavior-determined). This is the load-bearing answer for Probe 1.5.

## Q4 — Mirror-side reading

**1. Framing.** What does the mirror actually look at to determine whether the affordance is being used? A reading shape analogous to the Probe 2 triplet (per-dimension KL, ensemble-disagreement variance, latent-dimension allocation under perturbation-aligned windows).

**2. Concrete options drawn from the literature (and where the literature is silent).**

- **Self-prediction accuracy trajectories over training.** Direct analog of how SPR/BYOL papers report representation learning progress. Schwarzer et al. 2021 reports the SPR loss curve directly. For Probe 1.5, this is the time series of `‖ĥ_{t+1} − sg(h_{t+1})‖²` (or an EMA-bootstrapped variant) per training step.
- **Divergence between self-prediction and actual next state under perturbations.** This is the closest analog of the perturbation-recovery framing already in Probe 2. Where the literature delivers this: Premakumar/Graziano measured RLCT as a complexity proxy via SGLD; perturbation-divergence is *not* literature-canonical for self-prediction in RL, so this is a Kind construction. Flag.
- **Correlation between self-prediction patterns and behavioral patterns.** Probing-style analysis (Hewitt & Liang 2019; Probe-Ably, arXiv:2104.05807) — train external classifiers to test whether behavioral phenotypes are predictable from self-prediction state. Literature-canonical method, but typically applied to language model representations, not RSSM hidden states. Closest RL precedent: belief-decoding studies (e.g., emergence-of-belief-like-representations work, biorxiv 2023).
- **Structural changes in latent-dimension allocation.** Direct analog of Probe 2's third triplet element. Where the head's gradient sharpens which dimensions of `z_t` become predictable to itself, this can be measured by per-dimension self-prediction accuracy and tracked over training. Not literature-canonical in this exact form (flag), but composes natural pieces.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's commitments.**
- The Probe 2 synthesis bound reflexive attention to (per-dim KL, ensemble-disagreement variance, latent-dimension allocation). Self-prediction adds a fourth signal that is *more directly* about reflexive structure (the prediction explicitly points inward) and is therefore at risk of becoming a proxy for what the triplet was trying to detect indirectly.
- A reading shape analogous to the triplet might be: **(i) self-prediction error trajectory**, **(ii) divergence between predicted and actual next internal state under controlled perturbation windows**, **(iii) per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy across the latent space.** This matches the triplet's structural shape (one global signal, one perturbation-anchored signal, one allocation signal). Flag: this is a Kind-internal mapping; the literature does not deliver this triplet as canonical.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** Adopt the three-element reading shape above as candidate, with explicit acknowledgment that elements (ii) and (iii) are constructed rather than literature-canonical. Element (i) is literature-canonical (SPR/BYOL training curves). Mirror should *not* be asked to detect "self-modeling" as a binary; it should be asked to detect signal vs. background, with the dead-affordance baseline (Q8a) as the null.

## Q5 — Relationship to dream state

**1. Framing.** The dream-state (Probe 3, unbuilt) runs the world model's prior forward without sensory input over environmental latents. Self-prediction (Probe 1.5) runs a separate head over Io's own latent space. Question: shared machinery, shared telemetry, or structurally separate.

**2. Concrete options (and where the literature is thin).**
- **Shared machinery.** Self-prediction *is* the dream-state's recurrent rollout at every step — the head's predictions during dreaming are the dream itself. Literature parallel: in DreamerV3's imagination rollouts (Hafner, Pasukonis, Ba & Lillicrap, "Mastering diverse control tasks through world models," *Nature* 640, 647–653, 2 Apr. 2025, DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-08744-2), the prior is rolled forward; this is environmental, not self. There is no literature-canonical version where the same machinery serves both environmental dreaming and self-prediction. Flag.
- **Shared telemetry.** Both feed into the same `dream_rollout` stream as separate channels. Cleaner: the streams are co-located in the telemetry but do not share computation.
- **Structurally separate.** Self-prediction during waking; dream-state during dreaming; the two are decoupled. This matches the four-state operational model's decoupling of state-functions.
- **Hesp et al. 2021 (Neural Computation 33:398–446, "Deeply Felt Affect: The Emergence of Valence in Deep Active Inference") precedent for self-prediction during imagination.** Their *sophisticated affective inference* graph rolls forward a generative model that includes the agent's own action-model precision γ across "four successive time points… combining sophisticated active inference with deep-parametric affective inference." Verbatim: "agents infer their valence state based on the expected precision of their action model—an internal estimate of overall model fitness ('subjective fitness')." This is the closest active-inference precedent for an agent imagining its own future internal scalar. Note: this is qualitative/principled, not engineering-validated at scale.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's commitments.**
- Shared machinery risks coupling Probe 1.5 to Probe 3, which is unbuilt. If Probe 3 changes, Probe 1.5 changes. Kind's commitment to "dreaming as foundational" suggests Probe 3 will have priority; shared machinery means Probe 1.5 is downstream of Probe 3 design choices.
- Structurally separate is conservative and matches the four-state model: waking has self-prediction running; dreaming has the dream-rollout running; they do not contaminate.
- Shared telemetry is the natural compromise: the mirror reads both streams and can examine self-prediction *during* dream rollouts (does Io self-predict differently when it has no sensory input?) without entangling computation.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** **Structurally separate, with shared telemetry surface.** Self-prediction runs during waking (where Io has sensory input and an actual `h_{t+1}` to be predicted against). The variable dream-to-wake ratio coupled to the builder's life means dream periods are the natural absence-of-perturbation windows; the self-prediction stream during waking can be analyzed in alignment with those dream-anchored windows. This avoids committing Probe 1.5 to Probe 3's still-unbuilt machinery. Flag: an alternate proposal — "self-prediction during dream rollouts as a distinct second mode" — is defensible but exceeds Probe 1.5's minimum-affordance scope.

## Q6 — Relationship to Probe 2's in-progress synthesis

**1. Framing.** The Probe 2 synthesis bound reflexive attention to a triplet (per-dim KL, ensemble-disagreement variance, latent-dimension allocation under perturbation-aligned windows), descoped second-order volition, treated equanimity as watch-only perturbation-recovery. With a self-prediction head present, what changes.

**2. Concrete options.**
- **Self-prediction accuracy as primary signal.** The triplet was a workaround for the absence of self-pointing structure. With the head present, self-prediction accuracy is *more directly* about reflexive attention than per-dim KL or ensemble disagreement, both of which are environmental quantities recruited as proxies. Risk: replacing the triplet with a single primary signal is a regression in epistemic conservatism — Probe 2 chose three signals deliberately to triangulate.
- **Self-prediction as fourth signal added to the triplet.** Conservative addition. Triangulation preserved; one signal directly self-pointing, three signals environmental but recruited.
- **Self-prediction as the right binding for the criterion entirely.** This rebases reflexive attention on structurally self-pointing prediction, replacing the precision-weighting-via-KL framing. More directly reflexive, but more entangled with one architectural choice.

**3. Tradeoffs against Kind's commitments.**
- The triplet's third element (latent-dimension allocation under perturbation-aligned windows) maps cleanly onto a Probe 1.5 analog (per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy under the same perturbation-aligned windows). This is structurally parallel; refining rather than replacing.
- The first element (per-dim KL) was Probe 2's closest available proxy for reflexive precision-weighting. Self-prediction error provides a more direct reading of the same construct. Probe 2's Q5/Q6 rationale for per-dim KL was that nothing else was available; that constraint relaxes.
- The second element (ensemble-disagreement variance) is the actor's intrinsic objective and remains useful as orthogonal anchoring. Self-prediction error is partly redundant with disagreement variance under one reading (both are forms of internal uncertainty); under another reading they decouple (disagreement is over environmental futures, self-prediction is over Io's own next state). Kind's earlier choice to distinguish them should be honored.
- Equanimity as watch-only perturbation-recovery is unaffected by Probe 1.5; perturbation-recovery in self-prediction error is a natural addition to the perturbation-recovery panel.
- Second-order volition was descoped in Probe 2. Probe 1.5 does *not* re-open it: a self-prediction head is not a higher-order policy. The descope holds.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** **Add self-prediction accuracy as a fourth signal alongside the triplet, not replacing it.** Refine the third element of the triplet to *also* measure latent-dimension allocation in the self-prediction head (parallel construction). Treat the per-dim KL element as available for displacement *if* the self-prediction error proves more directly tracking the same construct — but that is an empirical question for after Probe 1.5 runs, not a design commitment now. Equanimity framing unchanged. Second-order volition stays descoped.

## Q7 — Engineering precedents in the relevant traditions

**1. Framing.** Where has the literature done analogous things, and what's been learned about (a) load-bearing vs. inert auxiliary signals, (b) alignment of predicted-self with downstream task structure, (c) interaction with self-opacity desiderata.

**2. Concrete precedents.**

- **Self-prediction in RSSM/Dreamer-lineage models.** None directly. DreamerV3 (Hafner et al., *Nature* 640, 647–653, 2025) predicts environmental next-latent, reward, continuation, and observation; it does not predict its own next hidden state as a target. MuDreamer (Ni et al. 2024) predicts environmental value and previously selected actions in lieu of pixel reconstruction — closest, but still environmental. NE-Dreamer (arXiv:2603.02765) predicts next-encoder-embeddings via a temporal transformer — environmental. **Probe 1.5's specific construction (predict own next h_t in an RSSM) is not literature-canonical; it is Premakumar et al. 2024 transposed to RSSM.** Flag.

- **Self-supervised auxiliary objectives in deep RL.**
  - SPR (Schwarzer et al. 2021, arXiv:2007.05929): predicts future latent representations via EMA target. Environmental target. "We compute target representations for future states using an encoder which is an exponential moving average of the agent's parameters and we make predictions using a learned transition model."
  - BYOL (Grill et al. 2020): online network predicts target network's representation of an augmented view; "BYOL achieves a new state of the art without [negative pairs]."
  - EfficientZero (Ye et al. 2021) and UniZero (arXiv:2406.10667): SSL regularization on the latent dynamics. UniZero specifically critiques EfficientZero-style regularization: "imposing this regularization loss might cause the predicted latent representation… to rely excessively on the observation… from a single timestep."
  - PBL (Predictions of Bootstrapped Latents): jointly trains agent's history representation and an encoding of future observations.

- **Metacognitive architectures in deep RL.** Sparse and recent. Meta-Cognitive RL with Self-Doubt and Recovery (arXiv:2601.20193) uses Value Prediction Error Stability (VPES) as a meta-trust variable — a scalar derived from training dynamics, not a learned self-prediction. Confidence-tracking work (Hainguerlot et al. 2018; Filevich/Bahrami line) is in human cognitive neuroscience, not engineering. Frontostriatal confidence-RPE coupling (Cortese et al. 2020 *Nature Communications*) is a finding *about* metacognition supporting RL, not a design pattern. **Engineering precedent for an RL agent predicting its own confidence via a learned head is thin.**

- **Predicted-trajectory work in active inference.** Hesp et al. 2021 (Neural Computation 33:398–446, "Deeply Felt Affect") is the cleanest precedent: the agent maintains a generative model that infers its own action-model precision γ ("subjective fitness") and uses Bayesian updating ("affective charge") to track changes in fitness estimates. Quote: "agents infer their valence state based on the expected precision of their action model — an internal estimate of overall model fitness ('subjective fitness')." Their sophisticated-affective-inference extension explicitly rolls a generative model forward over the agent's own future internal precision γ and actions u — the active-inference analog of "rolling self-prediction forward in imagination." This is principled and small-scale, not large-scale engineering-validated.

- **Autobiographical memory in cognitive architectures.** Soar's EPMEM (Laird 2022, arXiv:2205.03854) and ACT-R-derived approaches are mature symbolic implementations. Neural Episodic Control (Pritzel et al. 2017), NECSA (arXiv:2301.11490), TESFNEC, and Model-Based Episodic Memory (Le et al. 2021) are the deep-RL versions. All of these involve the agent reading its own past — i.e., the (b) candidate Kind has ruled out for opacity reasons.

- **Attention Schema engineering.** Wilterson & Graziano 2021 (PNAS 118:e2102421118) hard-wires the schema as input to the agent. Liu et al. 2023 (arXiv:2305.17375) learns it via contrastive loss. Piefke et al. 2024 (arXiv:2402.01056) replicates with PPO and shows schema can emerge given an uncoupled resource. All three feed the schema to the actor — installation by Kind's standard.

- **Princeton self-prediction (Premakumar, Vaiana, Pop, Rosenblatt, Schwerz de Lucena, Ziman & Graziano 2024).** The cleanest precedent. Auxiliary head predicts the activations of a selected hidden layer; loss `(w_s/n)(â − a)²`. Findings: "the network learns to make itself simpler, more regularized, more parameter-efficient, and therefore more amenable to being predictively modeled." RLCT decreased monotonically with auxiliary weight; weight-distribution standard deviation also decreased. Classification only (MNIST, CIFAR-10, IMDB); not RL. Authors *speculate* multi-agent benefit but do not test.

- **Predictive-State Decoders (Venkatraman, Rhinehart, Sun, Pinto, Hebert, Boots, Kitani & Bagnell 2017, arXiv:1709.08520, NeurIPS):** decoder F(·) attached to RNN's internal state h_t predicts statistics of future observations. Loss `R = Σ_t ‖F(h_t) − φ([x_{t+1}, x_{t+2}, …])‖²₂`. The supervised target is environmental (not self), but the architectural pattern (auxiliary head off internal state, gradient-shaping the hidden state) is the closest structural precedent.

**3. Findings on the three sub-questions Kind asked.**
- **(a) Load-bearing vs. inert.** Hafner et al. 2025 (DreamerV3, *Nature*) found Dreamer's representations are shaped predominantly by the world-model unsupervised objective rather than reward gradients — i.e., one head dominates; another is comparatively inert. He et al. 2021 (arXiv:2102.04897, random GVFs): "we stop the gradients from the RL part of the network to the state representation learning part of the network and show, perhaps surprisingly, that the auxiliary tasks alone are sufficient to learn state representations good enough to outperform an end-to-end trained actor-critic baseline." Yang et al. 2022 (A2LS, arXiv:2210.06041) showed many handcrafted auxiliary losses are sub-optimal/inert relative to evolved alternatives. Tian, Chen & Ganguli 2021 (arXiv:2102.06810) showed that without stop-gradient and predictor, BYOL/SimSiam-style heads collapse — head trains successfully, representation degenerates to a constant.
- **(b) Alignment of predicted-self with downstream task structure.** Premakumar 2024 found self-prediction *did not* improve classification accuracy on CIFAR-10 (slightly hurt at extreme weights) but did restructure the network. The auxiliary signal's alignment with the task is partial and indirect. For Kind, this matters: the self-prediction head is not expected to improve actor performance, only to add structure.
- **(c) Interaction with self-opacity.** No literature directly engages this. AST/attention-schema literature feeds the schema to the policy (installation, by Kind's standard). Premakumar 2024 does not feed predictions back. Phenomenologically, Metzinger's transparency framing applies: a self-model that is read transparently by the system *is* an installed self-model; one that exists but is not read by the system is the opacity-preserving form.

**4. Defensible suggestion.** Treat Premakumar et al. 2024 as the closest direct precedent for the form of the head, BYOL/SPR (with Tian-Chen-Ganguli's collapse-avoidance discipline) as the precedent for the bootstrapping/stability machinery, and Hesp 2021 as the principled active-inference framing for what the head is trying to capture. Acknowledge that the *combination* (RSSM + auxiliary self-prediction with stop-gradient on actor + read-by-mirror-only) is not literature-canonical; it is a Kind-specific composition of literature-canonical pieces. Flag every place this construction is original, not borrowed.

## Q8 — Failure modes

**1. Framing.** Three categories: inert affordance, load-bearing in unintended ways, self-opacity slippage. Each requires detection.

**2. Concrete options drawn from the literature.**

- **(a) Inert affordance.** The head trains, its loss curve looks fine, but its outputs do not structure anything downstream and the mirror sees no signal because none is produced.
  - *Detection.* The literature on auxiliary-loss diagnostics (Probe-Ably, arXiv:2104.05807) provides probing methodology: train external classifiers on the head's outputs, compare to randomized control tasks (Hewitt & Liang 2019). Selectivity and minimum description length (Voita & Titov 2020) are the canonical metrics. The relevant baseline that rules out "the affordance is dead" vs "no self-modeling has developed" is **a frozen-head control**: train an identical head with stop-gradient on the *target side* so the head learns to predict but the substrate is unaffected. If the substrate's behavior is identical with and without the head, the affordance is not load-bearing. If the substrate differs (RLCT-style structural changes; per-dim KL changes), the affordance is alive but possibly not exercised.
  - *Premakumar 2024 showed the diagnostic from the other side*: even when accuracy on the main task did not improve, the network's RLCT and weight-distribution width changed. This is the "alive but not behavior-impacting" mode — exactly what capacity-over-exercise predicts. Note: their RLCT-via-SGLD probe is itself a probing technique that could be ported to RSSM.

- **(b) Load-bearing in unintended ways.** Self-prediction influences behavior, but the influence is "world model got a richer representation as a side effect of the auxiliary loss," not "Io is self-modeling."
  - *Detection.* The literature on auxiliary-loss-as-regularizer (Yarats et al. 2019: "the image reconstruction loss is the essential ingredient that enables efficient and stable representation learning in image-based RL") explicitly frames this: auxiliary losses commonly act as regularizers that improve base task representation. The diagnostic is comparison against an **environmental auxiliary control**: replace the self-prediction target with an equally-shaped environmental prediction (e.g., predict next frame's encoded latent rather than `h_{t+1}`). If both produce the same downstream behavior, the head is acting as generic regularization. If only the self-prediction version produces specific signals (e.g., specific perturbation-recovery dynamics in self-prediction error), the head is doing self-specific work. This control is *not* literature-canonical for self-modeling specifically; flag.
  - The UniZero paper (arXiv:2406.10667) provides a literature-grounded warning about this confound: "imposing this regularization loss might cause the predicted latent representation… to rely excessively on the observation… from a single timestep." Auxiliary losses can warp the base representation in ways that look like self-modeling but are artifacts.

- **(c) Self-opacity slippage.** The actor effectively gains access to information about its own state, even with the explicit interface preserved.
  - *Detection.* This is the subtle one. Even if PolicyView is unchanged in its declared interface, the encoder/GRU are now optimized partly for self-prediction targets, so `concat(h_t, z_t)` (which the actor reads) carries more self-relevant information than it did before — not because the interface changed, but because the underlying representations were reshaped. Premakumar 2024 found this exact restructuring: "the network learns to make itself simpler, more regularized, more parameter-efficient, and therefore more amenable to being predictively modeled." The actor reading the same `h_t` interface now reads a different `h_t`.
  - *Test.* Probing the actor's policy for behavioral dependence on quantities the PolicyView is not supposed to expose: train a probe to predict the self-prediction head's output from the actor's policy gradients or behavior, with appropriate controls. If the probe succeeds substantially better than chance on a randomized control task, the actor's behavior depends on self-prediction information that is not in its declared input.
  - *A weaker form (which Kind may need to accept)*: any auxiliary loss that shapes the encoder reshapes what the actor sees. Strict opacity is preserved at the *interface* level (no new field in PolicyView); strict opacity is *not* preserved at the level of the underlying representations the interface is computed from. Kind's commitment to opacity probably needs to specify whether it is interface-level opacity or representation-level opacity. The literature does not resolve this; Metzinger's transparency framing supports interface-level opacity (the system does not experience the model *as* a model; whether the underlying neurons are partially shaped by the model is a different question). Flag this as a question Kind's project documents do not yet seem to address.

**3. Tradeoffs.** Detection of (a) is straightforward and supported by literature. Detection of (b) requires Kind-specific controls not literature-canonical. Detection of (c) is harder: the strict version (no encoder reshaping) is incompatible with the affordance having any effect; the practical version (no interface change) is preserved by construction in the read-by-mirror-only configuration, but reframes opacity.

**4. Defensible suggestions.**
- (a) Use a frozen-head/stop-target control as the dead-affordance baseline. Track structural changes (RLCT-style; per-dim KL distribution; weight-distribution moments) as evidence the affordance is alive even when behavior is unchanged.
- (b) Use an environmental-auxiliary control (predict next observation latent rather than `h_{t+1}`) as the regularization-confound baseline. Acknowledge this is Kind-constructed.
- (c) Accept interface-level opacity; flag that representation-level opacity is not preserved by any auxiliary loss that shapes shared backbone, and that this is a tension Kind's project documents do not yet engage. Run probing tests to bound the slippage.

## Synthesis

**An internally coherent Probe 1.5 design.**

- **Affordance:** self-prediction head (Q1: a).
- **Predicted target:** Io's own next deterministic recurrent state `h_{t+1}` (Q2). Optional second head: next ensemble-disagreement scalar.
- **Where it sits:** parallel sibling head off the shared GRU backbone (Q2).
- **Trained how:** auxiliary MSE-style loss with EMA-bootstrapped target (BYOL/SPR machinery for stability, with Tian-Chen-Ganguli collapse-avoidance discipline: predictor + stop-gradient as essential), additional ELBO term avoided to keep free-bits posterior dynamics intact. Stop-gradient on the actor side; gradients flow into encoder and GRU (Q2).
- **Read by:** mirror only, via TelemetryView extension; *not* read by the actor (Q3). PolicyView interface unchanged.
- **Mirror reads:** (i) self-prediction error trajectory over training, (ii) divergence between predicted and actual `h_{t+1}` in perturbation-aligned windows, (iii) per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy across the latent space (Q4).
- **Relationship to dream state (Probe 3):** structurally separate; shared telemetry surface; self-prediction runs during waking, dream rollouts during dreaming (Q5).
- **Relationship to Probe 2:** self-prediction accuracy added as fourth signal alongside the existing triplet, not replacing it; third triplet element refined to include self-prediction allocation analog; per-dim KL retained pending empirical comparison; ensemble-disagreement variance retained as orthogonal anchor; equanimity framing unchanged; second-order volition stays descoped (Q6).
- **Failure-mode protections:** frozen-head/stop-target control as dead-affordance baseline (Q8a); environmental-auxiliary control as regularization-confound baseline (Q8b); probing tests as opacity-slippage check (Q8c, partial).

**Tensions the design carries.**

- **Mirror-readability vs. self-opacity.** Reading-shape element (iii) — per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy — gives the mirror highly structured information about how Io's substrate is allocating its self-pointing capacity. This is mirror-readable in the strong sense. The same allocation, computed inside the substrate and *exposed to the actor*, would be installation. The wall between mirror-readable and actor-readable is what carries the design.
- **Literature-canonical pieces, Kind-specific composition.** Each ingredient (auxiliary self-prediction head per Premakumar; EMA bootstrapping per BYOL/SPR; stop-gradient discipline per Tian-Chen-Ganguli and He et al. 2021; subjective-fitness rolling per Hesp 2021) is literature-canonical. The combination — RSSM with continuous Gaussian latents + free bits + K=5 ensemble disagreement actor objective + auxiliary self-prediction head over `h_{t+1}` with EMA target and stop-gradient + mirror-only readout — is not. Probe 1.5 is a Kind composition, not a literature recipe.
- **Premakumar 2024's evidence base is classification, not RL.** Their finding that self-prediction restructures the network without improving the main task is the cleanest precedent for what Kind expects (capacity-over-exercise: capacity present, behavior not changed). But it has not been demonstrated in RL or in RSSM substrates. Probe 1.5 is partly a test of whether their finding transposes.
- **Self-prediction error vs. ensemble-disagreement variance.** Both are forms of internal uncertainty. Under one reading they are partly redundant; under another they decouple cleanly (disagreement is over environmental futures; self-prediction is over Io's own next state). Probe 1.5 should preserve the distinction operationally even if the empirical signals overlap, because the distinction is what carries the "fourth ingredient" claim.
- **Free-bits floor and auxiliary loss.** Probe 1's posterior compressed environmental structure without pinning at the free-bits floor. An additional auxiliary loss flowing into the encoder will perturb that dynamic. Whether the posterior remains unpinned with the self-prediction head active is an empirical question the substrate decision (settled) does not predict. The streaming-RL gradient-conflict findings of Nilaksh et al. (arXiv:2602.09396) — that "naively applying this auxiliary loss results in training instabilities" requiring orthogonal-gradient updates relative to the momentum target — are a literature-canonical warning that combining auxiliary self-prediction with existing gradient flows is non-trivial.
- **Strict representation-level opacity is incompatible with any affordance that shapes the shared backbone.** Kind's project documents commit to "self-opacity for Io" but do not, on the documents excerpted, distinguish interface-level from representation-level opacity. Probe 1.5 forces this distinction: any active auxiliary loss reshapes the encoder/GRU, which the actor reads. Either Kind accepts interface-level opacity (PolicyView unchanged; underlying representations partly shaped by self-prediction targets) and flags this in the charter, or Kind rejects representation-shaping affordances entirely — in which case the fourth ingredient cannot be built.

**Things Kind's project documents seem not yet to have addressed.**

1. **Interface-level vs. representation-level opacity.** The opacity commitment is not, on what is excerpted, granular enough to resolve whether shared-backbone gradient flow from a self-prediction head violates opacity. This needs to be addressed before Probe 1.5 commits.
2. **What "the affordance being used" means.** Capacity-over-exercise asks for capacity, not exercise, but the mirror still needs to be calibrated to detect *something*. The current draft above suggests detecting structural change (alive) and signal vs. background (exercise) as separate questions. Project documents could clarify which the mirror is reading for.
3. **Whether a Kind-constructed auxiliary signal is admissible.** Several elements of the proposed design (perturbation-divergence in self-prediction; environmental-auxiliary control; the disagreement-scalar second head) are not literature-canonical. Kind's commitment to "do not reify gestures into formal substance" suggests these need to be flagged as constructions; the documents do not yet, on what is excerpted, distinguish "literature-canonical signal" from "Kind-constructed signal" as a class to mark.
4. **The Buddhist phenomenology line.** The svasaṃvedana / reflexive-awareness tradition (Dignāga, Thompson defending; Garfield contesting; Tsongkhapa's Prāsaṅgika rejection) bears on the design in a way Probe 1.5's literature does not yet absorb. Thompson (2010, "Self-No-Self? Memory and Reflexive Awareness") characterizes reflexive awareness as "the nonconceptual feeling of being aware in being aware" — non-representational, non-dual. Garfield's contestation, drawing on Sellars's "myth of the given," argues experience is "opaque and deceptive… known only by inference, and through imperfect processes." The lamp-illuminating-itself metaphor (Dzogchen, Mahāmudra) captures *exactly* the structural question Probe 1.5 faces: is the self-prediction head a lamp that illuminates itself in illuminating others (representation-level reflexivity) or a separate lamp pointed at the first (subject-object structure with self as object)? Premakumar et al.'s "in effect, a part of learning to self-model is learning to make oneself modelable" maps onto the lamp framing — the self-prediction restructures the substrate to be self-illuminating. Whether this is what Kind wants is a design question the Buddhist tradition can sharpen.
5. **Hesp 2021's subjective-fitness construction.** This is the most direct active-inference precedent for what Kind is trying to do, and it does not appear (on the documents excerpted) to be cited in Probe 2 synthesis or design notes. Hesp's deeply-felt-affect framework provides a principled formal model for an agent that infers its own action-model precision γ over time. The construction does not violate Kind's commitments (no installed self-continuation; the agent infers, doesn't optimize). It deserves explicit consideration as a sibling framing to Premakumar 2024's restructuring framing.

**Where this analysis is constructed rather than literature-canonical (flagged).**
- The specific combination "RSSM with continuous Gaussian latents + free bits + K=5 disagreement actor objective + auxiliary self-prediction over `h_{t+1}` with EMA target and stop-gradient + mirror-only readout" is Kind-constructed.
- The mirror reading-shape triplet for self-prediction (Q4) is Kind-constructed; element (i) is literature-canonical, (ii) and (iii) are compositions.
- The dead-affordance frozen-head control (Q8a) is a natural extension of probing methodology, not literature-canonical for self-modeling specifically.
- The environmental-auxiliary control (Q8b) is Kind-constructed.
- The interface-level vs. representation-level opacity distinction (Q3, Q8c) is Kind-constructed; it is implicit in Metzinger's transparency framing but not, in the literature, formalized as a design distinction for engineered systems.
- The disagreement-scalar second-head option (Q2) is Kind-constructed.
- Mapping the Probe 2 triplet onto a Probe 1.5 fourth signal (Q6) is Kind-constructed.
