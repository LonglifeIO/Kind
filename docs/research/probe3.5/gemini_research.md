If an agent's internal energy dictates its operational duration, the drive to keep energy in-band is fundamentally an imperative to survive. Furthermore, Buddhist phenomenology identifies bhava-tanha (the craving for existence) as emerging precisely when transient experiential states are falsely reified into a continuous self that must be defended against annihilation.[constructed] Conversely, the strongest case that a preference-over-observations is categorically different from continuation-as-imperative is structural. A prior preference is a localized target probability distribution over sensory inputs, $p(o)$, establishing a gradient for Expected Free Energy (EFE) minimization. It operates symmetrically to epistemic drives, without overriding them via an infinite-horizon existential penalty.[constructed] To keep the implementation on the permitted side, concrete design features are required. First, the interoceptive variable's depletion must never cause mechanical episode termination; the environment runs regardless of energy state. [canonical] In deep active inference, Expected Free Energy (EFE) naturally decomposes into an epistemic term (information gain or ambiguity reduction) and a pragmatic term (expected utility or risk). The pragmatic term is evaluated as the expected log-likelihood of anticipated observations under a target prior distribution, $p(o)$. In homeostatic models, a Gaussian distribution around a setpoint provides a standard log-preference, yielding a quadratic cost for deviations.[constructed] Attaching the preference to the latent state $p(s)$ violates self-opacity, as it requires the actor to hold explicit mathematical preferences about its own internal neural representations. Attaching it to the sensed interoceptive observation $p(o_{intero})$ maintains opacity: the agent simply acts to expect a specific sensory input.[constructed] At rollout evaluation time, the actor calculates EFE by taking the negative log-probability of the predicted interoceptive observation under the fixed prior preference, and subtracting the epistemic value (the existing $K=5$ variance over latent predictions). To control the influence of this pragmatic pull, a preference precision parameter scales the term. [canonical] High precision shifts behavior toward goal-directed exploitation, while low precision allows epistemic exploration to dominate.
