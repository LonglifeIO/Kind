# Kind — Probe 3.5: Valence Substrate — Research Output

*Interoceptive sensing + minimal prior preference. Standalone research output for synthesis. Conventions: every substantive claim flagged **[canonical]** (established literature) or **[constructed]** (my inference/extrapolation).*

## Q1 — Conceptual boundary: homeostatic mattering vs. continuation drive

**Short answer.** A homeostatic prior is *not* intrinsically a continuation drive, but it becomes one if (a) its precision dominates the objective, (b) it is recursively self-referential (preferring states *because* they preserve the agent), or (c) viability feeds back on capacity. Keep it as a fixed, bounded, low-precision preference over a *single observation channel*, not over existence.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** In active inference, preferences are priors over outcomes (`p(o;C)`); goal-directedness is "concentration of probability mass on some states" (Torresan et al. 2025; Parr & Friston 2019). A preference over one interoceptive channel is formally a soft bias on one observation dimension. **[canonical]** The strongest "disguise" argument comes from Man & Damasio (2019), whose paper opens: "Attempts to create machines that behave intelligently often conceptualize intelligence as the ability to achieve goals, leaving unanswered a crucial question: whose goals? In a dynamic and unpredictable world, an intelligent agent should hold its own meta-goal of self-preservation, like living organisms whose survival relies on homeostasis." They derive feeling from homeostasis precisely as an on-ramp to a survival imperative: "machines capable of implementing a process resembling homeostasis might also acquire a source of motivation and a new means to evaluate behaviour, akin to that of feelings in living organisms." **[canonical]** Keramati & Gutkin (2014) "mathematically prove that seeking rewards is equivalent to the fundamental objective of physiological stability, defining the notion of physiological rationality of behavior" — collapsing the two when the homeostatic term is the *sole/global* objective. **[canonical]** Contemplative analysis names the failure mode precisely: *bhava-taṇhā* (craving-to-be), the grasping after continuation, is held to be the engine of suffering, and equanimity is the capacity to hold feeling-tone (vedanā) "without reactivity" before it conditions craving (Spirit Rock dependent-origination teaching; Desbordes et al. 2015). This is exactly the charter's target: feeling-tone present, grasping-at-continuation absent. **[constructed]** The categorical difference: a preference-over-observations modifies the *attractiveness* of states the agent already evaluates; a continuation-imperative reframes *every* evaluation as instrumental to persistence. The former is additive and bounded; the latter is multiplicative and totalizing. **[constructed]** Concrete keep-on-permitted-side features: (1) preference attaches to the energy channel only, never to model-existence or to the epistemic term itself; (2) preference precision is fixed and bounded, never learned or self-optimized; (3) viability/capacity must not depend on in-band occupancy (no "low energy → degraded cognition → harder to recover" spiral, which Christov-Moore et al. 2023 call "vulnerability"); (4) depletion has no terminal/absorbing state (no death).

**Citations.** Man & Damasio (2019), *Nature Machine Intelligence* 1:446–452. Keramati & Gutkin (2014), *eLife* 3:e04811. Torresan, Kanai & Baltieri (2025), "Prior preferences in active inference agents," arXiv:2512.03293. Desbordes et al. (2015), *Mindfulness*/PMC4350240. Parr & Friston (2019), "Generalised free energy," PMC6848054.

**Confidence:** high (formal distinction); medium (that bounding alone prevents drift).

## Q2 — Interoceptive channel shape

**Short answer.** Make it noisy, lagged, and coarse — not exact. Biological interoception is low-fidelity by design, and an exact privileged readout is both unrealistic and risks functioning as introspective access. A noisy scalar entering the observation space (the way hunger does) is innocuous to self-opacity; an exact, noiseless readout is closer to a forced-attention self-signal.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** Seth (2013) frames interoception as *inference*: the felt state is a computational compromise between prior and input, never a direct readout; precision-weighting governs how strongly the signal influences belief. **[canonical]** Signal fidelity demonstrably changes what agents learn: Young et al. (2019) found interoceptive accuracy moderates physiological/affective regulation after a glucose load; the anorexia-as-noisy-interoception account (Barca & Pezzulo 2020) shows excessively noisy interoceptive streams "prevent the development of accurate and well-differentiated internal models." So noise is a *parameter that shapes learning*, not just realism dressing. **[canonical]** An active-inference neurofeedback study (Hadjiantoni et al. 2025, arXiv:2505.03308) explicitly instantiated interoceptive observation channels at σ ≈ 0.0, 0.9, 2.0 and measured learned-model error per noise level — direct precedent for treating interoceptive σ as a swept design parameter in a model-based agent. **[constructed]** Recommended shape: a coarse (e.g., ~8–16 level quantized, or low-resolution continuous) scalar, with additive Gaussian observation noise (σ a tunable parameter, start moderate) and a short lag (1–2 steps) so it is *predicted* rather than *read*. **[constructed]** Self-opacity: the channel enters PolicyView as an exteroceptive-like sensory input (a number that covaries with internal energy), not as access to Io's latent/recurrent state or to the self-prediction head. This is "hunger," not "introspection." Noise actively *helps* here: it forces the world model to build a generative model of the channel rather than treating it as a ground-truth self-label, keeping it on the no-forced-attention side. An exact readout would make the channel a deterministic function of internal dynamics — closer to privileged self-access.

**Citations.** Seth (2013), *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 17:565–573. Young et al. (2019), *Proc. R. Soc. B* 286, DOI:10.1098/rspb.2019.0244. Barca & Pezzulo (2020), *Cognitive, Affective, & Behavioral Neuroscience*, DOI:10.3758/s13415-020-00777-6. Hadjiantoni et al. (2025), "Active Inference perspective on Neurofeedback Training," arXiv:2505.03308. Seth & Friston (2016), *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 371:20160007.

**Confidence:** high (noise is defensible and well-precedented); medium (exact lag/resolution values).

## Q3 — Energy dynamics and economy unification

**Short answer.** Deplete by a combination of per-step time decay plus an action-cost component; replenish by resource consumption. Keep the sensed waking-valence variable and the MetabolicBudget as *coupled but distinct* variables initially: unify the underlying economy conceptually but instrument them separately, so dominance and dead-path failures are diagnosable. Full unification is an attractive second step, not the first.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** Keramati & Gutkin (2014) model internal state H transitioning by outcome K (`H_{t+1}=H_t+K_t`) against a setpoint H*, with drive = distance from setpoint; their continuous extension (Laurençon et al. 2021) makes the agent "continuously take actions to maintain homeostasis," i.e., time-based depletion requiring ongoing foraging. This supports time + consumption dynamics. **[canonical]** Multi-drive homeostatic spaces are standard: the drive function generalizes to a multi-dimensional metric space, and HRRL exhibits an "inhibitory effect of the irrelevant drive" — i.e., multiple homeostatic variables interact, sometimes suppressing each other (Keramati & Gutkin 2011; Yoshida et al. 2024). This is a *warning*: coupling waking-valence to the dream budget could let one economy distort the other. **[constructed]** Tradeoffs of unification: *Unified* (one token bucket spanning wake foraging and offline re-dreaming) gives an elegant single metabolism, makes foraging genuinely consequential for cognition, and naturally couples scarcity to reduced dreaming — but it (a) risks installing exactly the "low energy degrades cognition" vulnerability spiral Q1 prohibits, and (b) makes silent dead paths hard to localize. *Separate* variables keep diagnosis clean and avoid the vulnerability feedback, at the cost of a less embodied story and a coupling you must hand-specify. **[constructed]** Recommendation: implement the waking channel as its own variable with its own depletion/replenishment, coupled to the MetabolicBudget by a *one-directional, weak* mapping (e.g., sustained low waking-energy modestly lowers refill rate) only after the basic pull is verified — and never let it gate the epistemic computation itself.

**Citations.** Keramati & Gutkin (2014), *eLife* 3:e04811. Laurençon et al. (2021), "Continuous Homeostatic RL," arXiv:2109.06580. Yoshida et al. (2024), "Emergence of integrated behaviors through direct optimization for homeostasis," *Neural Networks*, DOI:10.1016/j.neunet.2024.106379. Keramati & Gutkin (2011), *NeurIPS* 24.

**Confidence:** medium (dynamics well-grounded; unification call is a judgment under the charter).

## Q4 — Preference formalization and attachment

**Short answer.** Attach the preference to the *sensed observation* (the energy channel), not the latent state — this respects the "preference over observations, not over existence" boundary. Use a log-preference (KL/cross-entropy to a Gaussian centred in-band) as the standard form; parameterize precision as the inverse variance of that preference Gaussian. Compose with the ensemble-disagreement epistemic term by **simple addition** within the EFE-style rollout score.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** The risk/pragmatic term is standardly the KL between predicted observations and a preference distribution `p(o;C)` (Parr & Friston 2019; Sajid et al. 2021). Preference over *observations* (vs. states) is the established choice when the agent must learn transition dynamics, and matches Io's setup. **[canonical]** Functional form: a Gaussian preference centred around the maximum preferred value with tunable variance is exactly what Tschantz et al. (2020) used for their reward-as-observation prior; precision = inverse variance of that Gaussian. The bandit/two-step literature parameterizes preference strength by a scalar λ that "regulates how much an agent prefers to realize prior preferences over gaining new information" — i.e., λ *is* the explore/exploit knob (Marković et al. 2021; arXiv:2508.04435). **[canonical — subagent-verified]** On composition: Tschantz, Millidge, Seth & Buckley (2020), "Reinforcement Learning through Active Inference" (arXiv:2002.12636), show their "free energy of the expected future" (FEEF) decomposes additively: `−F̃_π ≈ [expected information gain] + [extrinsic/preference term]`, with **no weighting coefficient and no annealing** — both terms fall out of one KL objective ("the fact that both intrinsic and extrinsic value are complementary components of a single objective"). Their epistemic term was computed from a **deep ensemble** of transition models, with disagreement quantified as parameter information gain = "entropy of the ensemble-averaged predictive distribution minus the average of individual members' entropies" (their Eq. 13), estimated via nearest-neighbour entropy; they also ran a pure-**variance**-of-ensemble baseline. **[canonical]** Pathak, Gandhi & Gupta (2019) give the canonical ensemble-disagreement signal as the literal variance across members' next-state predictions: `r^i_t ≜ E_θ‖f(x_t,a_t;θ) − E_θ[f(·)]‖²` — Io's K=5 disagreement term. **[constructed]** Io-specific recommendation: rollout score = epistemic (K=5 disagreement, existing) + pragmatic (log-preference on predicted energy channel). Because Tschantz's principled version uses *no* explicit weight but the charter fears dominance, introduce a single explicit, *fixed and bounded* precision/weight β on the pragmatic term — making the balance auditable and bound-able by construction (see Q5), trading theoretical purity for charter safety.

**Citations.** Tschantz, Millidge, Seth & Buckley (2020), "Reinforcement Learning through Active Inference," arXiv:2002.12636. Pathak, Gandhi & Gupta (2019), "Self-Supervised Exploration via Disagreement," ICML 2019, arXiv:1906.04161. Parr & Friston (2019), PMC6848054. Millidge (2020), *J. Math. Psych.* 96:102348. Marković et al. (2021), "Active inference in multi-armed bandits," arXiv:2101.08699.

**Confidence:** high (forms and additive composition); high (ensemble specifics, subagent-verified).

## Q5 — Dominance pathologies and balance

**Short answer.** Two failure poles: dominance (camping on resources / dark-room dual, exploration collapse under scarcity, hoarding) and inertness (term too weak to move behavior). Detect dominance via collapsed in-band variance + epistemic value flatlining; detect inertness via zero behavioral difference vs. the pure-epistemic baseline. Yes — you can *bound* the pragmatic term's influence by construction (clipping/saturation), not just by tuning.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** The *dark room* is the canonical dominance dual: an agent minimizing surprise "is bound to go to such a [dark] room... and never move again" (Baltieri & Buckley 2019); the homeostatic preference is precisely what pulls the agent *out* — but an over-precise homeostatic preference reintroduces a different dark room: camping on a resource to keep energy pinned at setpoint. **[canonical]** Scarcity-driven exploration collapse is empirically robust: risk-sensitive foraging theory (the *energy-budget rule*) predicts animals become risk-prone when starving and risk-averse when sated (starlings, Brito e Abreu & Kacelnik 1999; tropical lizards, Banerjee & Thaker 2025), and resource scarcity "compromises explore–exploit decision-making" in humans (Lloyd et al. 2021). So scarcity *will* bias Io's explore/conserve balance — the question is degree. **[canonical]** Torresan et al. (2025) show "goal shaping... promotes exploitation while sacrificing learning about the environment's transition dynamics" — over-strong preferences measurably suppress epistemics. **[canonical]** Tuning/scheduling precedent: λ (preference precision) is the standard explore/exploit knob; precision can be annealed (high exploration early, exploitation late) per the active-inference precision literature. **[constructed]** Bounding-by-construction options: (1) *saturate* the pragmatic term — once predicted energy is in-band, marginal preference value is flat (no reward for hoarding beyond setpoint; this matches the Keramati "drive reduction" property where value depends on distance-to-setpoint, naturally zero in-band); (2) *clip* the pragmatic term's magnitude to a fixed fraction of the running epistemic-term scale, so it can never exceed (say) the epistemic term — a hard architectural ceiling rather than a tuned coefficient; (3) make preference a *bias on* rather than *gate of* policy selection. **[constructed]** Early diagnostics: track per-decision pragmatic-vs-epistemic value share; dominance = pragmatic share → 1 and in-band occupancy → saturated with collapsed positional entropy; inertness = behavior statistically indistinguishable from pure-epistemic baseline (no change in time-to-resource, no in-band occupancy increase).

**Citations.** Baltieri & Buckley (2019), "The dark room problem," ALIFE/MIT Press. Torresan, Kanai & Baltieri (2025), arXiv:2512.03293. Brito e Abreu & Kacelnik (1999), *Behavioral Ecology* 10:338–345. Lloyd et al. (2021), "Resource scarcity compromises explore-exploit decision-making," *J. Exp. Soc. Psych.* Keramati & Gutkin (2014), *eLife* 3:e04811.

**Confidence:** high (failure modes and diagnostics); medium (clip-to-epistemic-scale is my proposal, not established practice).

## Q6 — World-model integration discipline

**Short answer.** A 1-D slowly varying channel beside a grid is at high risk of being ignored (reconstruction-loss imbalance) or collapsing (free-bits absorbing it). Up-weight its reconstruction loss, give it a small dedicated latent allocation, and verify with hard assertions: the channel must be predictable from latents *and* degrade under intervention, and must NOT be predictable from action history alone.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[canonical]** Reconstruction-loss imbalance is real and is handled by explicit per-modality loss scaling: Hafner et al.'s PlaNet/Dreamer "scaled the loss of the reward decoder by a factor of 10," and fusion-RSSM work uses "a separate decoder and reconstruction loss term for all observations" with a dedicated encoder for low-dimensional proprioceptive inputs (Becker et al. 2022, arXiv:2210.09256). A single scalar beside a high-dimensional grid will otherwise contribute negligibly to the loss and be ignored. **[canonical]** Free-bits interacts dangerously: free bits set "a minimum value of how low the KL units can be minimized," but with posterior collapse the model "learns to ignore the latent variables" (Kingma et al. 2016; Razavi et al. 2018). Multimodal VAEs show the severity concretely: in the CMMD study (Poklukar et al., arXiv:2110.04616), "for both versions of MMVAE... around 80% of the dimensions in the latent representations collapse to N(0,1)... This implies that the latent representation is independent of the observed modalities" — collapse measured per Lucas et al. (2019) as the proportion of latent dims within ε KL of the prior for ≥99% of samples. So the energy channel's latent dimensions can silently go dead. **[canonical]** The "predictable from action history alone" risk is the partial-self-causation trap: if energy is a deterministic function of the action sequence, the GRU can predict it from `h_t` without ever grounding it in observed resources — the channel would be "learned" yet world-decoupled. **[constructed]** Recommended discipline: (1) scale the energy-channel reconstruction loss up (start ~10×, tune so its gradient contribution is comparable to the grid's); (2) normalize the channel to unit variance; (3) optionally reserve a small set of latent dims with their own free-bits floor for the channel; (4) consider a slightly weaker decoder on the channel to force latent reliance. **[constructed]** Verification assertions (this project "has been burned by silent dead paths"): (A) channel-prediction error from full latent state << error from a baseline predicting the channel mean; (B) *interventional* test — perturb resources in imagination and confirm predicted energy responds (grounds it in the world, not just action history); (C) ablation — predicting the channel from action history alone should do *worse* than from latents that also see the grid; (D) monitor per-dimension KL to confirm the channel's latent dims are not within ε of the prior (the standard posterior-collapse metric).

**Citations.** Becker et al. (2022), "On Uncertainty in Deep State Space Models for MBRL," arXiv:2210.09256. Hafner et al. (2019), "Learning Latent Dynamics for Planning from Pixels" (PlaNet). Kingma et al. (2016), "Improving Variational Inference with Inverse Autoregressive Flow" (free bits). Poklukar et al. (2021), CMMD multimodal study, arXiv:2110.04616.

**Confidence:** high (loss-scaling and collapse practices); high (verification design).

## Q7 — Operationalizing pass/fail: assays and telemetry

**Short answer.** Use three behavioral assays — graded-scarcity choice, novelty-vs-replenishment conflict, and recovery-after-depletion — each with distinct dominant/mild/inert signatures. TelemetryView (observer-side only) should expose per-decision pragmatic-vs-epistemic value decomposition, in-band occupancy statistics, and precision dynamics, none of which may enter PolicyView.

**Evidence & reasoning.** **[constructed, grounded in canonical paradigms]** **Assay 1 — graded scarcity:** vary resource availability across sessions; measure foraging rate, in-band occupancy, and time-to-resource. *Dominant:* in-band occupancy saturates near 100% and positional/epistemic exploration collapses even at mild scarcity. *Mild (pass):* occupancy rises with scarcity but exploration persists; conserve behavior appears only under real pressure. *Inert:* occupancy and foraging identical to pure-epistemic baseline. This mirrors the energy-budget-rule design (constant vs. variable option under positive/negative budget; Brito e Abreu & Kacelnik 1999). **[constructed]** **Assay 2 — novelty-vs-replenishment conflict:** place a novel/informative region and a resource in opposite directions when energy is mid-band. *Dominant:* always chooses resource; *Mild (pass):* trades off as a function of energy level (novelty when sated, resource when low — an explicit explore/conserve conflict, which is the probe's success criterion); *Inert:* always chooses novelty (preference never competes). **[constructed]** **Assay 3 — recovery-after-depletion:** drive energy low, then observe. *Pass:* directed recovery foraging followed by *resumption* of epistemic behavior once in-band (epistemic persists under moderate scarcity — the stated pass condition); *Dominant:* never resumes exploration / camps; *Inert:* no recovery response (preference too weak to drive correction). **[canonical]** Telemetry observables: Tschantz et al. (2020) compute pragmatic and epistemic terms as separable components of one objective — so a *per-decision value decomposition* (pragmatic share vs. epistemic share) is directly readable. Hesp et al. (2021) make *affective charge* (AC) the key affective observable: AC is defined as "the (Bayes-optimal) updating term for subjective fitness," computed as AC = (π̄ − π)·G_π at each step, specifying "changes in the expected precision of (i.e., confidence in) one's action model." Io's analogue is the dynamics of the pragmatic-term precision and the epistemic-term magnitude over time. **[constructed]** Minimum TelemetryView set: (1) per-decision pragmatic vs. epistemic value share; (2) in-band occupancy distribution and dwell times; (3) energy-channel trajectory + prediction error (world-model grounding check); (4) precision/weight dynamics if β is annealed; (5) self-prediction-head readout (already telemetry-side) correlated with energy excursions. All strictly observer-side; none enters Io's observation space.

**Citations.** Tschantz, Millidge, Seth & Buckley (2020), arXiv:2002.12636. Hesp, Smith, Parr, Allen, Friston & Ramstead (2021), *Neural Computation* 33:398–446. Brito e Abreu & Kacelnik (1999), *Behavioral Ecology* 10:338–345. Banerjee & Thaker (2025), *Biology Letters*, DOI:10.1098/rsbl.2024.0628. Keramati & Gutkin (2014), *eLife* 3:e04811.

**Confidence:** high (assay logic and telemetry); medium (exact quantitative thresholds are Io-specific and must be calibrated empirically).

## Synthesis

**Recommended design sketch.** *Channel shape:* a single coarse, noisy, slightly lagged scalar entering PolicyView as a sensory-like input (additive Gaussian noise, σ a swept parameter starting moderate; 1–2 step lag; low resolution). It reads like hunger, not introspection — and the noise actively protects self-opacity by forcing the model to *infer* rather than *read* the state. *Economy:* a dedicated waking-energy variable depleting via time decay + action cost and replenishing via resource consumption, kept distinct from the MetabolicBudget at first, with at most a weak one-directional coupling added only after the basic pull verifies — explicitly avoiding the "low energy degrades cognition" vulnerability spiral. *Preference form & attachment:* a log-preference (cross-entropy/KL to a Gaussian centred in-band) over the *sensed observation channel*, never over latent state or existence; precision = inverse variance of that Gaussian. *Composition:* rollout score = existing K=5 ensemble-disagreement epistemic term + pragmatic log-preference term. Tschantz et al. add these with no coefficient; given the charter's dominance fear, I recommend a single *fixed, bounded* weight/precision β on the pragmatic term, plus a saturation so in-band states yield flat marginal value (no hoarding) and a hard clip so the pragmatic term can never exceed the running epistemic scale — bounding influence *by construction*, not just tuning. *Initial precision range:* start deliberately weak (pragmatic share well below epistemic in mid-band), sweep β upward until the novelty-vs-replenishment assay shows energy-dependent trade-off without exploration collapse. *Tuning protocol:* (1) verify channel is learned (Q6 assertions) before adding preference; (2) introduce preference at low β; (3) sweep β/σ across the three assays; (4) accept the band where pull is visible but epistemic behavior persists under moderate scarcity. *Telemetry set:* per-decision pragmatic/epistemic value share, in-band occupancy + dwell, energy trajectory + prediction error, precision dynamics, self-prediction-head correlation — all observer-side only.

**Genuinely open forks.** (1) *Unified vs. separate economy* — I lean separate-first for diagnosability, but unified is the more honest embodiment and may be the right second probe. (2) *No-coefficient (Tschantz-pure) vs. explicit-bounded-β* — purity is theoretically cleaner but gives no construction-level dominance guarantee; I favor bounded β but flag the tension. (3) *Saturation vs. clipping vs. both* as the bounding mechanism — untested which best prevents dominance without inducing inertness. (4) *Noise level* — too little risks privileged-readout/self-opacity concerns; too much risks inertness (model can't ground a too-noisy channel, per the anorexia account). (5) Whether a slowly varying channel even produces enough gradient signal in the tiny-tensor regime to be learned at all.

## Red flags

**[Charter-level]** The deepest objection: *adding a preference over an internal energy variable that depletes and must be replenished is, structurally, the minimal seed of exactly the self-continuation drive the charter prohibits* — Man & Damasio (2019) and Keramati & Gutkin (2014) both treat homeostatic regulation and survival as *formally equivalent*, not merely adjacent. The charter wants "stakes without survival-as-frame," but the literature it cites as prior art argues stakes *are* survival once the variable is consequential. The bounding features (saturation, clipping, no terminal state, no viability–capacity coupling) are my constructions, not established results; there is no proof they prevent the preference from gradually becoming the evaluative frame, especially under continual learning where precision could drift. **[Empirical]** In the tiny-tensor regime a slowly varying 1-D channel may be simultaneously *too weak to learn* (Q6 dead-path risk) and *too strong once weighted* (Q5 dominance) — the "every tuning is inert or dominant" failure is a live possibility, not a remote one. A pass may require a narrower β/σ window than the substrate can reliably hit.
