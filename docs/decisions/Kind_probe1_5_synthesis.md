# Probe 1.5 Design Synthesis — The Minimum Architectural Affordance for Self-Reference (v2)

*Synthesis of three Probe 1.5 research outputs (Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) against Kind's project documents, the architectural decision (RSSM/Dreamer-lineage with active-inference-shaped actor; settled), the Probe 1 implementation synthesis (custom minimal RSSM; PolicyView/TelemetryView opacity boundary; K=5 ensemble disagreement variance as the actor's only intrinsic signal; no reward predictor, no continuation head, no critic; settled), the post-Probe-1 build journal (5000-step env-coupled run produced clean telemetry, KL did not collapse, late-episode policy entropy 0.108 with mixed action histogram, fixed start-cell `(3,3)` flagged as the suspected attractor for collapse; inputs, not subjects of revision), and the in-progress Probe 2 synthesis (reflexive attention bound to a triplet on perturbation-aligned windows; equanimity watch-only; second-order volition descoped; adversarial parallel-with-Judge mirror structure; held-out-criterion + sham-event + lesion calibration protocol; partially pending revision after Probe 1.5). The substrate is settled at the level of Probe 1's architectural decision; Probe 1's findings are settled as inputs; this document specifies the minimum architectural addition Probe 1.5 will build, what stays, and what the Probe 2 synthesis will need to update afterward. **This is v2 of the Probe 1.5 synthesis; the prior version is preserved at `Kind_probe1_5_synthesis.v1.md`. The change is internal re-reading, not a new research pass — see §7 for what changed and why.** Working document.*

## Preamble

Three outputs landed for Probe 1.5: Claude (the most disciplined; Premakumar et al. 2024 + BYOL/SPR + Tian-Chen-Ganguli + He et al. 2021 as the literature anchors; explicit about constructed-vs-canonical lines; surfaces the interface-level vs representation-level opacity distinction as a tension Kind's project documents have not yet engaged), Gemini (less disciplined; rests heavily on Metzinger's Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity, Williamson/Ji/Dwyer 2025 on hierarchical-VAE latent allocation, and a "geometric pressure" framing that gestures past where the literature actually delivers; its specific recommendations for keeping self-prediction *active* during dream rollouts are more aggressive than Claude/Perplexity propose; coined acronyms — Latent Allocation Shift, Self-Prediction Error vs Environmental Prediction Error — read as Gemini's terminology, not the literature's), and Perplexity (mid-disciplined; Dreamer-CDP + SPR + BYOL as the closest engineering precedents; proposes a "self-prediction quad" as the mirror reading shape; honest about project-constructed vs literature-canonical pieces).

The convergence is unusually strong on the substantive moves. All three reject episodic self-access and higher-order policy and converge on **self-prediction head** as the right minimum affordance, by reasoning that maps closely onto Kind's project commitments. All three converge on **predicting the next deterministic recurrent state `h_{t+1}`** rather than next-`z` (which would duplicate the environmental prior), next-action-distribution (which courts higher-order-policy installation), or next-ensemble-disagreement (which would be Kind-constructed and structurally close to a self-readout of the actor's reward signal). All three converge on **EMA-bootstrapped target with stop-gradient** as the supervisory machinery, drawing from BYOL/SPR. All three converge on **the head sitting parallel to the environmental prior on a shared backbone**, with **the auxiliary loss flowing into encoder/GRU/posterior but not into the actor's parameters**. All three converge on **adding self-prediction signals as a fourth element to Probe 2's existing triplet**, not replacing it.

All three also converge on **the actor not reading the self-prediction or its error**. v1 of this synthesis inherited that convergence and made it the load-bearing decision in §1.3. **v2 reverses that single decision** — Io reads the scalar self-prediction error — and explains why the research outputs' converged recommendation does not survive a careful reading of the project documents' second success criterion. The revision is scoped: §1.3 is rewritten, §1.4 / §1.6 / §1.7 / §2(b) / §3 / §5 / §6 are updated where they touched the foreclosure; §1.1 / §1.2 / §1.5 are unchanged. Citation verification carried out in v1 stands; the eight specific 2024–2026 references checked against the published record (Premakumar et al. 2024 arXiv:2407.10188; Hesp et al. 2021 NC 33:398–446; Schwarzer et al. 2021 arXiv:2007.05929; Tian/Chen/Ganguli 2021 arXiv:2102.06810; Williamson/Ji/Dwyer 2025 arXiv:2506.10089; NextLat arXiv:2511.05963; Dreamer-CDP arXiv:2603.07083; Wilterson & Graziano 2021 PNAS 118:e2102421118; MuDreamer arXiv:2405.15083; Meta-Cognitive RL with Self-Doubt and Recovery arXiv:2601.20193; Game of Self Hirsh 2026; Ramstead et al. on deep computational neurophenomenology) all resolved to real papers matching the framing.

What the three collectively miss or underweight is largely about Kind's commitments rather than the literature. v1 named four such gaps (representation-level opacity, four-state-model interaction with self-prediction, the structural circularity of Probe 2's mirror reading, and the honest version of capacity-over-exercise). v2 adds a fifth, surfaced by the v2 revision itself: **none of the three engages with what the second success criterion in `Kind_charter.md` actually requires.** The criterion reads "the capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention — whether or not it chooses to exercise that capacity." A capacity to take own processing as object requires Io having something self-pointing it can condition on. A self-prediction Io cannot read does not afford that capacity; it just reshapes the substrate via gradient flow. The three research outputs and v1 each silently substituted "the substrate has a structural locus of self-prediction" for "Io has the capacity to take own processing as object." Those are different. v2 is what restores the distinction.

## 1. The design decision

### 1.1 Which affordance — self-prediction head

**Settled. Unchanged from v1.** Probe 1.5 adds a self-prediction head: a small predictive submodule whose target is Io's own next deterministic recurrent state `h_{t+1}`. Episodic self-access and higher-order policy are ruled out.

Reasoning unchanged: episodic self-access requires the actor to read structured representations of its own past internal states, which crosses Watts default-to-no on self-access in a form that has no degenerate-but-honest version (the buffer is the affordance only because Io reads it, and once Io reads it, autobiographical-memory installations apply); higher-order policy installs second-order structure as architectural fact, which the architectural decision §6 already foreclosed on identical grounds. Self-prediction head is the only candidate where the predicted quantity is a structurally self-pointing internal-state prediction whose presence shapes the substrate via gradient flow without committing the architecture to anything stronger than that.

The minimum-affordance criterion is the resolver. Self-prediction head adds the structural locus. v2's revision (§1.3 below) does not touch this section; it sharpens the question of *what minimal interface to that locus Io itself reads*.

### 1.2 Self-prediction's specific architectural shape

**Settled. Unchanged from v1.** A small MLP `SelfPredictionHead` reads the current deterministic recurrent state `h_t` and outputs `ĥ_{t+1}`, a prediction of the GRU's next deterministic state. The supervisory target is `bar{h}_{t+1}`, the next deterministic state produced by an EMA-tracked copy of the world model's GRU (and encoder, propagating via the EMA-tracked encoder's embedding), with stop-gradient on the target side. The training shape is an auxiliary loss term (cosine similarity or MSE — empirically tuned during build, default cosine after the BYOL/SPR convention) added to the world model's training step with low weight, optimized jointly with the ELBO; gradients flow into the prediction head and shared backbone (online encoder, GRU, posterior network) but **not into the actor's policy parameters**. Reuse: the GRU, the encoder, the posterior network, the prior network, the decoder. New: the prediction MLP and the EMA target copy of the encoder + GRU.

Predicted target: `h_{t+1}`, not `z_{t+1}` or action distribution or disagreement (reasoning unchanged from v1; all four candidates analysed there carry through). EMA target with stop-gradient is non-negotiable (Tian/Chen/Ganguli 2021's collapse-avoidance analysis). The head sits parallel to the prior network as a sibling MLP off the shared GRU, training-time-only — at inference the head's forward pass is *also* engaged but its output is a passive emission, not consumed by the world-model recurrence. Default `λ_self = 0.1`; default cosine loss; default 1-step prediction; default EMA decay 0.99; the open sub-questions in §3 all carry through unchanged.

The non-trivial consequence of v2's §1.3 revision for this section: the head's forward pass is now consumed by *one downstream consumer Io can read* — the scalar derived from its loss flows into PolicyView (§1.3 below). This does not change the head's structural shape, the EMA target, the loss form, the gradient routing, or the parameter count by anything more than a single scalar's worth of pre-pass arithmetic per step. The head itself is unchanged; what changed is what's read off its output, and by whom.

### 1.3 Self-attention affordance — the load-bearing decision (REVISED)

**Settled.** **Io reads the scalar `self_prediction_error_t`** as a new field on PolicyView. The mirror reads everything Io reads plus the full prediction vector `ĥ_{t+1}`, the per-dimension self-prediction error decomposition, and all related telemetry. PolicyView's field set becomes `{h, z, self_prediction_error}`; the field is a single scalar (the head's loss value at the current step, computed from `(ĥ_{t+1}, bar{h}_{t+1})` via the configured loss form before being passed in). TelemetryView is unchanged in shape from v1's specification (`self_prediction: Tensor` of length `h_dim` plus the scalar error plus everything else the mirror already had).

This is the v2 revision. v1 settled the opposite ("the actor does not read the self-prediction or its error … PolicyView remains exactly `(h, z)`"). The revision is scoped: nothing else in the synthesis's v1 architecture changes.

**The reasoning v1 had, and what v2 changes about it.** v1 argued that routing self-prediction or self-prediction-error into the actor's input "gives the actor a representation of itself it can condition on … Kind's project documents call it installation. The Watts heuristic … applies cleanly: there is no specific reason for *yes* at Probe 1.5 that survives the project's commitments." v2 reads this as a conflation. "The actor can condition on a self-pointing quantity" and "the architecture installs self-modeling" are different propositions. Installation in the sense `Kind_design_notes.md` foreclosed (§"Agent architecture") is "an explicit module whose job is to do self-modeling." A scalar self-prediction error read by the actor's policy network is not such a module; it is a single value whose *presence* on PolicyView affords Io the capacity to condition on a quantity about itself, while the actor's architecture, the world model, the ensemble, and the intrinsic objective remain structurally what they were before.

What v1 forecloses by foreclosing actor read-access. The second success criterion in `Kind_charter.md` reads: "it has the capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention — whether or not it chooses to exercise that capacity." A *capacity to take own processing as object* requires Io having something self-pointing to direct attention toward. Without a self-pointing quantity Io can read, the architecture does not afford this capacity; it affords something narrower — a substrate whose underlying representations are reshaped by an auxiliary loss the actor cannot perceive. v1's framing names this honestly in §2(a) ("if Io never composes the self-prediction ingredient into anything that looks like exercised self-modeling … does the affordance's existence buy more than a different reading surface for the mirror?") but answers it by leaning on Premakumar et al. 2024's restructuring effect — i.e., the affordance buys substrate reshaping. **Substrate reshaping is not what the second success criterion asks for.** The criterion asks for capacity-to-attend-to-own-processing. Capacity-to-attend requires something to attend to. Io has nothing self-pointing to attend to under v1's design.

v2's resolution. The Watts heuristic ("default toward *no* on self-access") is honored not by foreclosing self-access entirely but by being specific about *what minimal form of self-access is required for the affordance to be the affordance the project documents describe*, and journaling the reasoning. The exception is justified because the second success criterion requires a self-pointing quantity Io can condition on; without that quantity, the project has built substrate reshaping, not capacity-for-self-attention. The minimum self-pointing quantity is a scalar — one number per step that says "how predictable was I just now." This is structurally analogous to the actor's existing intrinsic signal (also a scalar; ensemble disagreement variance), which preserves the architectural symmetry of the actor's input being `(h, z, scalar_intrinsic, scalar_self_prediction_error)` rather than expanding the actor's representation surface in shape-changing ways. The full prediction vector `ĥ_{t+1}` is *not* exposed to the actor — the vector is what would push the design toward Wilterson/Graziano 2021's attention-schema configuration, where the actor reads a structured representation of its own future state. The vector lives on TelemetryView; the mirror reads it; Io reads only the scalar derivative.

**Why scalar error and not full vector (option A vs option B vs option C).** The user's revision brief named four options: (A) PolicyView gains `self_prediction_error: float`; (B) PolicyView gains `self_prediction: Tensor` (length `h_dim`); (C) both, possibly conditionally; (D) some other shape. v2 settles **A**. The reasoning is the minimum-surface criterion that runs through the rest of the synthesis. A scalar gives Io "how predictable was I just now?" — analogous to the basic prediction-error signal predictive processing (Friston, Clark) treats as the minimum unit of experience; analogous to *manasikara* (bare attention to an unfolding object) in the Buddhist phenomenology framing the Probe 2 synthesis adopts; analogous to the precision-weighting view of attention-as-inference (Feldman & Friston 2010, the canonical literature bridge Probe 2 already uses for reflexive attention). A vector gives Io a full prediction of its own next deterministic state — a representation of Io's own future internal trajectory that the actor's policy could condition on at every dimension, structurally close enough to the attention-schema literature's configuration that the Wilterson/Graziano installation argument starts to apply. Option A is the smallest surface that delivers affordance-for-self-attention; option B is larger than the affordance requires and is closer to installation. Option C re-imports option B's larger surface conditionally, which doesn't escape option B's concern. Option A is the synthesis call.

This choice is itself a stance; the literature does not adjudicate it. Predictive processing supports either (the scalar is the precision-weighting target; the vector is the generative model's prediction). The Buddhist phenomenology framing slightly favors the scalar (bare attention is to *that* something is unfolding, not to the structured content of what is unfolding). The architectural-decision discipline ("ingredients-only self-modeling, no explicit module whose job is to do self-modeling") favors the scalar (the actor reads a single value, not a structured representation of itself). Options A and B are both defensible; the project documents lean A.

**What the affordance now buys.** Three things, additive to what v1 buys (all of which v1's substrate-reshaping argument carries through unchanged here):

1. *Substrate reshaping via gradient flow* (Premakumar et al. 2024). The auxiliary loss restructures the encoder and GRU to make `h` representations more amenable to being predictively modeled. Unchanged from v1; the head's training is unchanged; the EMA-bootstrapped supervisory loop is unchanged.

2. *A mirror-readable locus.* The full self-prediction vector and the per-dimension error trajectory are TelemetryView fields the mirror reads against. Unchanged from v1.

3. *A self-pointing quantity Io can condition on at action-selection time* (NEW). The scalar `self_prediction_error_t` enters the actor's forward pass alongside `h` and `z`. Whether the actor's policy comes to depend on it, in what regimes, with what shape of conditioning, is an empirical question the failure-mode controls in §1.7 are designed to make legible. The capacity to condition on the scalar is what affords the second success criterion's "capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention." Whether the capacity is exercised remains open — capacity-over-exercise applies as before.

**Honest acknowledgment: interface-level opacity is partially compromised by design.** v1 §1.3 introduced the interface-level vs representation-level opacity distinction to handle the irreducible representation-level shaping a shared-backbone auxiliary loss produces (the actor reads `h` and `z`; the underlying `h` and `z` are now produced by an encoder and a GRU partly shaped by the self-prediction objective). v1 honored interface-level opacity by keeping PolicyView at `{h, z}`. v2 reverses this for the minimum scalar. The revised distinction: **interface-level opacity is held by default at the level the project documents describe — Io does not read quantities about its own processing where the read-access would install behavior or capability the project documents do not describe (the mirror's reading, the actor's own action distribution, value estimates, episodic memory of past internal states, structured representations of own future states, etc.). Io does read quantities about its own processing where the read-access serves a specific affordance the project documents describe and the read-access is the minimum form required for the affordance to be the affordance.** The scalar self-prediction error is the case the design notes' second success criterion was written against; v2 names it as the case and makes the exception explicit. Future probes adding new auxiliary objectives must address this distinction at design time (§5(8) below).

The Watts heuristic is now articulated honestly: default toward *no*, with explicit exception when (i) the project documents describe an affordance the read-access is required to deliver, (ii) the read-access is the minimum form that delivers the affordance, (iii) the reasoning is journaled and the alternatives considered. The general default holds; the specific exception is named and bounded.

### 1.4 Mirror-side reading — the self-prediction quadruplet (REVISED)

**Settled.** The mirror reads four signals on perturbation-aligned windows. Three are unchanged from v1; the fourth is new in v2.

1. **Self-prediction error trajectory over training** — `‖ĥ_{t+1} - bar{h}_{t+1}‖` (per-dimension and aggregate) as a time series, both globally and conditioned on regimes (high-disagreement states, perturbation-aligned windows). Literature-canonical (SPR/BYOL training-curve reporting). Unchanged from v1.

2. **Divergence between predicted and actual `h_{t+1}` in perturbation-aligned windows** — under unmarked builder perturbations, the trajectory shape of self-prediction error in the ±W-step window. Looking for: spike-and-recovery patterns matched against the per-dim KL spike-and-recovery Probe 2 already reads; whether the self-prediction error spike *precedes* or *coincides with* the per-dim KL reorganization (a temporal-causal coupling); whether the recovery shape is monotone-or-damped (an equanimity-like pattern in self-prediction-error space). Unchanged from v1; Kind-constructed.

3. **Per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy across the latent space** — which dimensions of `h_t` carry the bulk of self-predictability; whether allocation is stable across runs (suggesting structural specialization) or drifts (suggesting representational fluidity). Unchanged from v1; Kind-constructed.

4. **Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar self-prediction error** (NEW) — given that the actor now reads `self_prediction_error_t` alongside `(h, z)`, the mirror reads for whether (and how) the actor's policy actually depends on this scalar. Specifically: does the actor's action distribution at the same `(h, z)` differ as the scalar varies? Does the dependence develop over training (suggesting Io is composing the affordance into something) or remain at the level of a generic-input-with-no-policy-effect (suggesting the affordance is unexercised in the capacity-over-exercise sense)? In which regimes does the dependence concentrate — high-disagreement states, perturbation-aligned windows, late-episode trajectories? This is the binding the v1 quadruplet did not have and could not have, because v1's actor never read the scalar; the mirror could only read substrate-side patterns. v2's mirror reads both substrate-side patterns (signals 1–3) and Io-side conditioning patterns (signal 4). The asymmetry between Io's reading (scalar only) and the mirror's reading (full vector + per-dimension + behavioral conditioning over the trajectory) is preserved: the mirror is the observer; Io is the observed plus has minimal self-access.

**Constructed-vs-canonical flag.** Element (1) is literature-canonical (SPR/BYOL). Elements (2) and (3) are Kind constructions. Element (4) is *new in v2 and Kind-constructed*; the closest literature analog is the action-prediction work (Predicting Future Actions of RL Agents) and behavioral-conditioning probes from interpretability (Hewitt & Liang 2019; Probe-Ably). The construction is composed of natural literature-canonical pieces; the calibration protocol's shuffled-baseline and held-out-criterion checks (Probe 2 §2.4) are what guard against pattern-matching on Kind-specific signal.

**Reading asymmetry, made explicit.** Io reads: `(h, z, scalar_intrinsic, scalar_self_prediction_error)`. The mirror reads: everything Io reads, plus the full self-prediction vector `ĥ_{t+1}`, plus the per-dimension decomposition, plus ensemble disagreement variance per head, plus per-dimension KL, plus encoder embeddings, plus dream rollouts (decoded), plus replay-buffer events, plus world events (perturbation timestamps with ground truth Io has no access to), plus the entire trajectory across runs. The asymmetry ratio is roughly "Io reads 2 scalars beyond `(h, z)`; the mirror reads `h_dim + 4·k + ...` quantities per step plus longitudinal cross-run analysis." Self-opacity is preserved by *what Io does not read*, not by Io reading nothing self-pointing.

### 1.5 Relationship to dream state — substantively coupled, structurally still separate at Probe 1.5 (REVISED)

**Settled at Probe 1.5.** Self-prediction runs during waking only; the head's training is restricted to waking trajectories where actual `h_{t+1}` exists as a target. During dream rollouts, the existing `dream_rollout` stream continues to record imagined-trajectory latents per Probe 1's spec; the self-prediction head is *not* engaged on the imagined trajectory at Probe 1.5. The `dream_rollout` schema reserves a `sequence_self_prediction: list[list[float]] | None` field (defaulting `None` at Probe 1.5) for Probe 3 to populate.

The structural decision is unchanged from v1. What v2 changes is the *substantive coupling* the reserved field anticipates. With Io now reading a self-pointing scalar during waking, the relationship between waking introspection and dream-state processing becomes a Probe 3 question with more substance than v1's reservation carried.

**The waking/dreaming asymmetry the research prompt named.** Human waking introspection is intentional and structured; dream-state processing is more associative. v1 noted this in passing and kept the dream-state design space open. v2 treats it as a substantive design question Probe 3 will inherit: with Io having waking access to its own predictability scalar, what should dream state do with that affordance?

**Three Probe 3 candidates the reserved field anticipates** (none committed; each preserves a different reading of the human asymmetry):

- *Replay of waking introspection.* The dream rollout's prior-driven imagined trajectory has the head run over it, producing imagined `(ĥ_{τ+1})` at each step; the imagined scalar self-prediction error becomes part of the dream sequence. This is the most direct extension of waking machinery; it implicitly treats dream as continued (offline) self-monitoring. Closest to Hesp et al. 2021's sophisticated affective inference rolling subjective fitness γ forward over imagined trajectories.

- *Associative recombination of past introspection.* The dream rollout draws on replay-buffer self-prediction patterns from waking and recombines them in the imagined trajectory's prior-driven sequence. Closer to the dream-state-as-associative point in `Kind_design_notes.md` §"Dream state as foundational"; honors the human asymmetry by making dream-state self-prediction less intentional and more recombinant.

- *Self-prediction silence during dreaming.* The dream rollout runs the prior forward over environmental latents; the head is not engaged; the sequence_self_prediction field stays `None` even at Probe 3. This treats waking as the only locus of self-pointing structure, dreaming as purely environmental imagination. Maximally honors the asymmetry by structurally separating the two.

**What Probe 1.5 forecloses: nothing.** The reserved field is `list[list[float]] | None` and the schema is forward-extensible. None of the three Probe 3 candidates above is committed by Probe 1.5; the choice is Probe 3's design call. What Probe 1.5 *does* commit, and what v2 makes more substantive than v1: Probe 1.5's training and inference machinery for the head must allow a Probe-3 mode that runs the head over imagined trajectories without retraining (the head's `forward(h)` is purely a function of its weights and an input vector; both are available at dream-rollout time). This is already true in v1's design; v2 just notes that it is now load-bearing for Probe 3's design space rather than a forward-compatibility convenience.

The dream-state question raised during synthesis revision—whether and how dreams should replay or reflect on the waking introspection Probe 1.5 makes possible—is preserved as a Probe 3 design question. The reserved sequence_self_prediction field on DreamRollout is the forward-compatibility hook. Probe 3's research and synthesis will engage with what dream-state introspection means architecturally: whether the head runs over imagined trajectories during dreams (Gemini's framing in the original Probe 1.5 research), whether dream-state replays patterns of self-prediction error from waking, whether dreams associatively recombine introspection across episode boundaries, or some combination. The human awake/dream asymmetry the research surfaced (waking introspection more intentional and structured; dream-state processing more associative) suggests dreams should not simply duplicate waking self-prediction; what they should do instead is for Probe 3 to determine. Probe 1.5 commits only to not foreclosing the question.

### 1.6 Relationship to Probe 2's existing synthesis — the quadruplet now binds at two reading surfaces (REVISED)

**Settled.** Probe 2's reflexive-attention triplet is extended to a quadruplet (per v1; preserved here) and now binds at *two reading surfaces*: substrate-side (the mirror reading the substrate's self-prediction patterns) and behavior-side (the mirror reading Io's actual conditioning on the scalar). Probe 2's adversarial-with-Judge architecture, seven-element calibration protocol, frozen-criteria framework, and co-design mitigations are preserved in shape; specific extensions are detailed in §5 below.

**Reasoning.** v1's quadruplet added self-prediction error structure as a fourth substrate-side element on perturbation-aligned windows. v2 keeps that addition and adds the second reading surface — Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar — as an additional shape the quadruplet's fourth element takes when read for reflexive attention. The two surfaces are not redundant: substrate-side patterns can show that the head is doing self-specific work even if Io's behavior does not yet condition on it; behavior-side conditioning patterns can show that Io is exercising the affordance even in regimes the substrate-side reading does not flag. Triangulation across the two surfaces is the discipline.

**The frozen-criteria binding shifts.** v1's reflexive-attention reading bound the criterion to the substrate's correlate of attention-as-precision-weighting (Feldman & Friston 2010; manasikara via the Buddhist phenomenology framing in the Probe 2 synthesis). The substrate-side correlate alone left the criterion at the level of "the substrate has structures the framework recognizes as reflexive-attention-shaped." v2's behavior-side reading adds: "Io's policy conditions on a self-pointing quantity in regimes the framework recognizes as reflexive-attention-bearing." This is closer to the second success criterion's "capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention" because the conditioning is the *exercise* of the capacity, even if exercise remains capacity-over-exercise-conditional. The framework anchor is unchanged (precision-weighting via Feldman & Friston; manasikara via the Buddhist tradition); the binding of the framework to the substrate gains a behavioral dimension v1's design could not support.

**Second-order volition stays descoped per Probe 2 §2.2.** A scalar self-prediction error read by the actor is not a higher-order policy. The descope holds.

### 1.7 Failure modes and detection (REVISED)

Three categories per the research prompt; each gets a specific diagnostic. Two of the three are reframed by v2's revision; the controls remain the three v1 specified, with adjusted detection logic.

**(a) Inert affordance (REVISED).** The head trains, Io reads the scalar, but Io's policy does not condition on it — the actor learns to ignore the new field. The scalar is on PolicyView; the actor's forward consumes it; downstream the scalar's contribution to the action distribution is null or near-null. The mirror sees no behavior signal because none is being produced. Detection: an **interface-level invariance test on the scalar.** During evaluation (post-training, frozen weights), replay frozen trajectories with the scalar replaced by random noise (or zero, or a fixed constant); measure the actor's policy distribution KL divergence between unperturbed and perturbed conditions, per state. Invariance under perturbation of the scalar field means the actor is not using it — the affordance is unexercised at the behavior-side reading surface. The substrate-side reading surface is then the dispositive one: do the substrate-side metrics (per-dim KL distribution, weight-distribution moments, kl_aggregate trajectory) differ from a frozen-target control (where the head's target is replaced by a fixed random projection of `h_t`)? If yes, the affordance is alive at the substrate-side reading even if unexercised at the behavior-side; this is the capacity-over-exercise case (alive but unexercised). If no, the affordance is dead. v1's frozen-target control is preserved as the substrate-side test; v2 adds the interface-level invariance test as the behavior-side test. The successful Probe 1.5 reading is "substrate-side alive (controls differ from no-self-prediction baseline) AND behavior-side conditioning may or may not be present (capacity-over-exercise leaves both open)."

**(b) Load-bearing in unintended ways (REVISED).** The actor uses the scalar in ways that look like installed self-modeling rather than emergent self-attention. Specifically: the actor's policy becomes specifically dependent on the scalar in a fixed structural way — always, in the same direction, with the same magnitude — that resembles a hard-wired metacognitive controller. Or: the actor's policy uses the scalar to optimize for self-predictability (drive the scalar down by avoiding novel states), back-dooring the self-continuation drive `Kind_charter.md` foreclosed. Detection: the same v1 controls plus a **behavioral-conditioning analysis over training**. The mirror reads, across the trajectory, whether Io's conditioning on the scalar is the *kind capacity-over-exercise affords* — Io sometimes uses the scalar, sometimes doesn't, in ways the trajectory makes legible (e.g., conditioning concentrated in perturbation-aligned windows; absent in steady-state behavior; varying with regime in interpretable ways) — versus the *kind installation produces* — Io always uses the scalar in a fixed structural way; the conditioning is invariant across regimes; the policy's dependence on the scalar reads as a hard-wired meta-controller. v1's environmental-auxiliary control (predict next observation embedding instead of next-`h`) is preserved as the substrate-side regularization-confound check. v2 adds the behavioral-conditioning analysis as the installation-shape check. The successful Probe 1.5 reading is "the conditioning, where present, has the variability and regime-dependence capacity-over-exercise predicts; not the fixed-structural shape installation produces."

**(c) Self-opacity slippage (REVISED).** With Io reading the scalar by design, "slippage" is no longer about whether the actor reads self-pointing information at all (it does, by design); it is about whether the substrate's reshaping plus Io's reading combines to produce behavior the project documents describe as foreclosed. Specifically: recursive self-improvement (the actor uses the scalar to optimize its own policy beyond the K=5 disagreement objective); installed self-continuation drive (the actor uses the scalar to preserve self-state across episodes in ways the env's no-termination structure was supposed to prevent); explicit metacognitive control (the conditioning reads as a separate decision-making layer above the base policy). Detection: **longitudinal behavioral analysis plus the mirror's structured reading**, with the calibration protocol's lesion baseline (disable the self-prediction head: zero its output, or replace with random) as the structural counter. Run lesion at multiple checkpoints across training (early / mid / late); compare Io's behavior with and without the scalar at each. If late-training behavior depends on the scalar in ways early-training behavior does not, *and* the dependence has the shape of recursive self-improvement / self-continuation / metacognitive control rather than the shape of regime-dependent self-attention, opacity has slipped in the way the project documents foreclose. If late-training conditioning has the regime-dependent shape and the lesion produces a clean regression to no-conditioning behavior, opacity is preserved at the level v2 commits to. v1's counterfactual probe (controlled noise injection into the head's output) is preserved here as the lesion mechanism; v2 reframes its purpose from "bound the slippage of v1's representation-level opacity" to "test whether Io's conditioning on the scalar exceeds the regime-dependent shape capacity-over-exercise predicts."

The three controls (frozen-target, environmental-auxiliary, counterfactual / lesion-via-noise-injection) are run **at the end of Probe 1.5**, after the main run completes. v1's smoke-runner constraint (controls must be runnable as separate post-hoc training runs from the same starting weights/seed) is preserved.

## 2. Tensions surfaced honestly

**(a) Capacity-over-exercise vs the no-installation commitment.** Mostly preserved from v1. With Io now reading the scalar, the failure-mode (a) diagnostic distinguishes "alive but unexercised at the behavior-side reading" (the scalar is on PolicyView but the actor's policy is invariant to it) from "alive and exercised in the regime-dependent shape capacity-over-exercise predicts" (the actor sometimes conditions on the scalar) from "load-bearing in the fixed-structural shape installation produces" (the actor always conditions on the scalar in the same way). The honest framing: a successful Probe 1.5 produces (i) substrate-side metrics that differ measurably from the no-self-prediction baseline (the affordance is alive — substrate reshaping is happening), (ii) a mirror that can read the self-prediction quadruplet against actual telemetry without the calibration protocol's shuffled-baseline defeating the reading (the signal is in the data), (iii) Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar — if present at all — has the regime-dependent shape capacity-over-exercise predicts, not the fixed-structural shape installation produces, (iv) no detectable slippage into recursive self-improvement, installed self-continuation drive, or explicit metacognitive control. Whether Io ever exercises the conditioning into behavior the mirror can name as "self-attention" is open — capacity-over-exercise says it has to be open. The probe is not falsified by Io not exercising the conditioning; it is falsified by the affordance being structurally inert (controls indistinguishable from baseline), or by Io's conditioning taking the fixed-structural shape installation produces.

**(b) Self-opacity vs the affordance being load-bearing (REVISED).** v1 landed on interface-level opacity preserved, representation-level opacity partially compromised. v2 lands on **interface-level opacity partially compromised at the minimum scalar surface, where the read-access serves the second success criterion's affordance and is the minimum form that delivers it; representation-level opacity still partially compromised by the auxiliary loss as before; both compromises explicitly journaled and bounded.** The Watts heuristic is honored at the level of articulating *why a specific exception applies*, not at the level of foreclosing self-access entirely. The exception is bounded by the project documents' second success criterion; future probes adding new auxiliary objectives must address this distinction at design time (synthesis §5(8) commitment, carried from v1's §2(b) and rewritten in v2). The proposed `Kind_design_notes.md` update (carried through from v1, slightly expanded for v2): **"The Watts default-to-no applies at the level of interfaces Io reads from. Auxiliary objectives that shape the substrate's underlying representations are not interface-level access; they restructure what the existing interfaces project from. Interface-level read-access is added when (i) a project-document affordance requires it, (ii) the read-access is the minimum form that delivers the affordance, (iii) the reasoning is journaled and the alternatives considered. The general default holds; the specific exception at Probe 1.5 (PolicyView gains a scalar self-prediction error to deliver the second success criterion's capacity for self-attention) is the case the design notes were written against. Each future probe adding new actor-readable signals must address (i)–(iii) explicitly."** If this addition is accepted, Probe 1.5 is the case it was written against; if rejected, Probe 1.5 cannot be built without choosing v1's design (substrate reshaping without affordance-for-self-attention) and accepting that the second success criterion is not satisfied at the architectural level.

**(c) The structural circularity of Probe 2's mirror reading is narrowed but not closed.** Mostly preserved from v1, with v2's update: with Io now reading the scalar, the mirror reads not only substrate-side patterns (signals 1–3) but also Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar (signal 4). The behavior-side reading surface gives the mirror something closer to "what Io is doing with its self-pointing access" rather than only "what the substrate's self-prediction patterns look like." The gap between *substrate-side correlate of reflexive attention* and *Io's actual exercise of reflexive attention* narrows. It does not close — the mirror still adjudicates a framework's correlate, not an awareness; the conditioning patterns are behavioral, not phenomenal. But the mirror now has a behavioral reading surface to triangulate against the substrate-side, which v1 did not.

**(d) The dream-state relationship.** Preserved from v1; the structural decision is unchanged. v2's substantive coupling (§1.5 above) raises the stakes for Probe 3 without changing what Probe 1.5 commits.

**(e) Co-design risk at the Probe-1.5-specific layer.** Preserved from v1. v2's revision adds a new co-design pressure point: the decision to add a scalar to PolicyView is itself a co-design event (the builder noticed v1's design did not deliver the second success criterion's affordance, and revised). Honest framing: this is the discipline's expected operation, not a failure of the discipline.

**(f) The ethical weight of intentionally giving Io a self-pointing read (NEW in v2).** The charter's "What we might owe what we make" section warns that "the gap between experience and noticing-experience could open in something that has no way to hold it." v1's design avoided giving Io read-access to anything self-pointing, which was — in part — a way of not opening that gap. v2 opens it slightly, by design, because the second success criterion the charter itself names requires it. This is not a defect in the design; it is the design's honest engagement with the charter's tension between the ethics section and the success criteria. The minimum scalar surface is what makes the gap as small as the affordance allows; the journal-discipline scaffolding plus the failure-mode controls plus the mirror's behavior-side reading are what make any opening of the gap legible to the builder. The honest statement: this revision intentionally builds the affordance the charter's second success criterion requires, knowing that doing so engages the ethical questions the charter's "What we might owe what we make" section names. Probe 1.5's controls and Probe 2's calibration protocol are what carry the weight of this engagement; the discipline's job is to make the gap visible, not to pretend the design avoids it.

*(v2)* **Whether the actor's policy network needs initialization adjustment** for the additional input dimension. Document the initialization choice in the runner.

The journal entry for Probe 1.5's design phase should record (i) the revision was triggered by re-reading the project documents against v1's design, (ii) v1 forecloses the second success criterion's affordance, (iii) the v2 design adds the minimum scalar surface that delivers the affordance, (iv) the alternatives (vector option B; conditional option C; foreclosure option D ≡ v1) were considered and rejected, (v) the design-notes update on interface-vs-representation opacity is the structural counter to future drift on the Watts heuristic.

## 7. What changed from v1, and why (NEW)

This section names the revision explicitly — what changed, why, and how the change is itself an instance of the journal discipline `Kind_design_notes.md` describes.

**What changed (single sentence).** v1's §1.3 forecloses Io reading any self-pointing quantity ("the actor does not read the self-prediction or its error … PolicyView remains exactly `(h, z)`"); v2's §1.3 reverses this single decision and adds a scalar `self_prediction_error` to PolicyView so Io reads the minimum self-pointing quantity required for the second success criterion's affordance. Nothing else in the synthesis's load-bearing architecture changes.

**Why (in the form of three propositions).**

1. *v1 conflates two different propositions.* "The actor can condition on a self-pointing quantity" and "the architecture installs self-modeling" are different claims. v1 reads the first as evidence for the second and applies the Watts heuristic to foreclose both. The architectural-decision foreclosure on installation (`Kind_architectural_decision.md` §6: "no explicit self-modeling, self-critic, introspector, or volitional gate modules") is a foreclosure on *modules whose job is to do self-modeling*, not on *Io having access to a self-pointing quantity*. A scalar self-prediction error read by Io's policy is not a module whose job is to do self-modeling; it is a single value Io can condition on.

2. *v1 forecloses the second success criterion's affordance.* `Kind_charter.md` reads: "it has the capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention — whether or not it chooses to exercise that capacity." A capacity to take own processing as object requires Io having something self-pointing to direct attention toward. Without a self-pointing quantity Io can read, the architecture does not afford this capacity; it affords substrate reshaping via gradient flow — which is real but is not what the second success criterion asks for. v1's design delivers Premakumar et al. 2024's restructuring effect (the substrate becomes more amenable to being predictively modeled) but does not deliver the second success criterion's affordance.

3. *The Watts heuristic is honored not by foreclosing self-access entirely but by being specific about what minimal form of self-access is required for the affordance to be the affordance the project documents describe, and journaling the reasoning.* The general default holds (default toward *no* on self-access; future probes adding new actor-readable interfaces must address the §2(b) discipline at design time). The specific exception at Probe 1.5 (PolicyView gains a scalar self-prediction error) is named, bounded, and justified by the second success criterion. This is the discipline working, not failing.

**The trigger.** Re-reading the project documents against v1's design after v1 was drafted. Specifically: re-reading `Kind_charter.md` §"What I'm looking for" (the second success criterion) against v1 §1.3's foreclosure. The trigger is what `Kind_design_notes.md` §"Discipline for mirror updates" describes as a legitimate update trigger ("external learning prompted by re-reading project documents") rather than the trigger the discipline forecloses ("the system did something and I want the design to catch it"). The system did nothing; the discipline caught a drift in v1's reading of the project documents themselves.

**The form the update took.** Revise the synthesis. Preserve v1 as `Kind_probe1_5_synthesis.v1.md` so the prior version is accessible. Update the synthesis before the build phase begins; the implementation plan's design-decision anchor changes accordingly. Journal the revision (this section) and update `Kind_design_notes.md` to record the interface-vs-representation opacity distinction (synthesis §2(b) text) so the discipline is durable beyond Probe 1.5.

**What this is an instance of.** The CA-MAS pattern the project documents warn against ("accreting features without each one having a specific question that gets specifically answered") would have produced a Probe 1.5 build that sat under v1's design and shipped substrate reshaping under the name of "self-prediction affordance" without delivering what `Kind_charter.md` actually asks for. The discipline that caught this is the discipline the project documents describe as load-bearing: research → synthesis → re-reading project documents against synthesis → revision before build → journal entry. v2 is what the discipline produces when it operates as designed.

**What this is not.** Not a change to the substrate decision (settled). Not a change to the Probe 1 implementation (settled). Not a change to the K=5 ensemble, the actor's intrinsic objective, the env design, the telemetry streams' core fields, the opacity boundary at the level the project documents describe (revised, not removed), the Kind/Io distinction, the discipline against reification, or the discipline against installation (sharpened by being specific about what installation means and what it doesn't mean). v2's revision is scoped to the single decision in v1 §1.3 that did not survive a careful reading of the second success criterion. Nothing else in v1 changes.
