# Probe 2 Design Synthesis — Mirror Calibration With Frozen Criteria and Adversarial Structure

*Synthesis of Probe 2 research outputs against Kind's project documents, the architectural decision (RSSM/Dreamer-lineage with active-inference-shaped actor; settled), the Probe 1 implementation synthesis (custom minimal RSSM with PolicyView/TelemetryView opacity, K=5 ensemble disagreement as the actor's only signal, no reward predictor, no continuation head, no critic; settled), and the post-Probe-1 build journal (the start-cell collapse and the first mirror's confabulated reading; inputs, not subjects of revision). The substrate is settled; Probe 1's findings are settled; this document specifies the design Probe 2 will be built and run against. Working document.*

---

## Preamble

Three research outputs landed for Probe 2 — Claude, Gemini, Perplexity — addressing five of the six question areas the original brief named (the prompt sent to the LLMs framed environment revision as a separate Phase 2b substrate fix and excluded it from research scope; the env-revision decision below rests on the build journal, not on the research). All three converge strongly on three things: descope second-order volition with reasoning rooted in Frankfurt's representational presuppositions and Io's flat actor (Hubinger 2019 is the closest ML construct, behavioral inference only); bind reflexive attention to KL/precision-style signals authorized by predictive processing (Feldman & Friston 2010), with Gemini sharpening this to the *manasikara* (bare attention) reading from Buddhist phenomenology rather than the more contested *sati* (lucid awareness); operationalize equanimity as perturbation-recovery dynamics — the temporal decay of `kl_aggregate_t` and ensemble-disagreement variance after world_event entries — while flagging the category error (stability of control-signal is not non-reactivity-of-experience). All three reach for some form of parallel adversarial structure with a synthesizing third call, all three commit to pre-registration plus shuffled-telemetry baselines plus stability checks, and all three want a hierarchical digest with retrieval rather than raw traces or single-run aggregates.

The substantive divergences are over the *shape* of the adversarial architecture (Claude: parallel different-models different-frameworks with arbiter; Gemini: parallel same-model different-roles Advocate/Skeptic with Judge; Perplexity: sequential reader-critic with optional light parallel), the *strictness* of the calibration thresholds (Gemini demands a 0% false-positive rate on shuffled baselines — overstrict and unrealistic for any LLM-as-judge protocol the literature documents; Claude wants ≥80% structured-field stability with held-out criterion; Perplexity is more permissive), and over whether *role tilt* (Advocate vs Skeptic) is more important than *framework anchoring* (predictive-processing vs Buddhist-phenomenology) in producing genuine disagreement. Reification check: Gemini's output is the most prone to reification — it cites several 2026 papers (Xu et al. on causal mediation analysis, Zheng et al. SVE-ASCII, Sisodia AI Observability, De Lima/Yang YIELD) the synthesis cannot independently verify; it presents Causal Mediation Analysis as a clean operational tool for distinguishing prompt-driven from data-driven readings (the underlying technique is real; its packaged application to LLM mirror calibration is constructed); it asks "what is the critic evaluating during dream rollouts" assuming a critic network that the Probe 1 implementation synthesis Q1 explicitly excluded (no reward predictor, no twohot critic, no continuation head). The synthesis takes Gemini's manasikara framing and the Sham Test idea on board where they earn it, and flags the 0% false-positive demand and the critic-network question as reification artefacts the design should not inherit. Where the project documents and the research disagree, the project documents win.

## 1. Synthesis across the research outputs

### Where they agree

**Second-order volition is descoped for Probe 2.** All three outputs reach the same destination by different routes. Frankfurt (1971) defines second-order volition as the reflective endorsement of a first-order desire as one's effective will; Io has no representations of "desires as such" distinguishable from other states, no internal evaluation layer, and no machinery that could implement endorsement. Hubinger et al. (2019) on mesa-optimization is the closest ML construct and is explicit that higher-order objective structure must be inferred behaviorally, not read from internal signals. Forcing the criterion onto policy-entropy data is what guarantees confabulation. The three converge: the honest output is to descope with the reason logged.

**Reflexive attention binds, with caution, to precision-weighting signals.** Feldman & Friston (2010) is the canonical literature bridge — attention as inference about the precision of sensory causes. The substrate's structural correlates are `kl_per_dim_t` (per-dimension posterior-prior divergence), ensemble-disagreement variance over the K=5 heads, and the coupling between disagreement events and subsequent posterior reorganizations. Gemini's contribution sharpens the criterion at the source: in Abhidhamma/Buddhist phenomenology *manasikara* (bare attention, the pre-conceptual turning toward an object) is operationally distinct from *sati* (lucid awareness/mindfulness); the structural correlate the substrate can carry is *manasikara*, not *sati*. The bridge is from one tradition (predictive processing) to a structural correlate of a concept in another tradition (Buddhist phenomenology); the match is partial and the synthesis records that.

**Equanimity binds, more gesturally, to perturbation-recovery dynamics.** All three converge on a perturbation-anchored shape: under unmarked builder perturbations (the harness's mutators, recorded in `world_event` with no agent-side observation marker), the equanimity-like trajectory shows an immediate spike in KL/disagreement followed by a non-oscillatory return to baseline within some recovery window, with no collapse into avoidance loops over the perturbed regions. Gemini sharpens this to the temporal derivative `d/dt kl_aggregate_t` as the operational signal; Claude flags that this is the literature's *behavioral* operationalization (Vago & Silbersweig 2014) of what the contemplative tradition characterizes as a stance-toward-content; Perplexity emphasizes the symmetry test (recovery on perturbations across affective valence — though Io has no affective valence, only structural variance, so the homology is partial). All three flag the category error explicitly.

**Some form of parallel adversarial structure with a synthesizing third call.** The single-mirror confabulation in Probe 1 (overstated patterns, reversed entropy direction, single outliers called frequent) is the empirical motivation. All three reach for parallel-with-third-call, and all three reject multi-round debate (Du et al. 2023 multi-agent debate works on closed factual tasks; Kenton et al. 2024 shows the advantage erodes on closed-judgment tasks where the judge cannot independently verify, which is Probe 2's regime). All three flag pseudo-disagreement, sycophancy, mode collapse, and shared-narrative drift.

**Pre-registration plus shuffled-telemetry baselines plus stability tests plus faithfulness checks is the calibration protocol's spine.** Three-way convergence. Gelman & Loken (2013) on the garden of forking paths is the conceptual anchor; pre-registration constrains analytic freedom, shuffled baselines distinguish prompt-driven from data-driven readings, paraphrase- and reseed-stability rule out single-call artefacts, citation-grounding makes claims faithfulness-checkable. The scrambled-telemetry test is named explicitly by all three as the highest-yield single tool given Kind's commitments.

**Hierarchical digest with retrieval beats raw traces and beats single-run aggregates.** Three-way convergence on the layered scheme: a base-layer episodic digest with macro statistics and flagged anomalies; a triggered drill-down layer for episodes or time windows the mirror flags; access to decoded ASCII dream rollouts (whose Probe-1 legibility is confirmed). Long-context evaluation literature (NIAH, lost-in-the-middle effects) is the reason raw traces fail; Probe 1's misread is the reason single aggregates fail.

### Where they diverge

**Adversarial architecture: parallel-with-arbiter (Claude) vs Advocate/Skeptic + Judge (Gemini) vs sequential reader-critic with optional light parallel (Perplexity).** The disagreement is real, not cosmetic. Sequential preserves Reader A's framing as anchor and Probe 1 has shown how stickily a single voice's framing propagates; sequential is structurally similar to Probe 1's failure mode. Parallel-with-arbiter spends more compute to keep framings independent; the question is *what produces the framings' independence*. Claude argues different model families anchored in different frameworks (predictive-processing vs Buddhist-phenomenology) is the strongest bias-decorrelation. Gemini argues same model with explicit Advocate/Skeptic role tilt creates structural friction — Reader A is mandated to argue *for* the criterion's presence, Reader B is mandated to argue against. The two axes (framework anchoring; argumentative role tilt) are not mutually exclusive; the strongest configuration combines them.

**Calibration thresholds: 0% false positive on shuffled baselines (Gemini) vs ≥80% structured-field stability + held-out criterion (Claude) vs medium-strictness periodic baselines (Perplexity).** Gemini's "0% false-positive rate on the Shuffled Baseline protocol" is reified — the LLM-as-judge faithfulness literature (FaithBench 2024 reports ~55–58% best-case F1/balanced-accuracy on hard hallucination cases; Zheng et al. 2023 documents systematic biases not eliminated by self-consistency) makes 0% an unachievable bar that, if used as a hard gate, would reject every reading. Claude's threshold-based calibration with a held-out criterion is more defensible. Perplexity's medium strictness preserves more readings at the cost of higher confabulation pass-through rates.

**Whether the architecture imagines a critic network in Io's substrate.** Gemini's output asks "what is the critic evaluating during the dream rollouts?" and proposes that the mirror should have direct access to "the critic's internal value estimates" as a proxy for expected free energy. This is a substrate misunderstanding: Probe 1's implementation synthesis Q1 explicitly rules out a critic ("no twohot critic, no reward predictor, no continuation/discount predictor"); Q2 specifies the actor uses analytic gradients through the differentiable latent dynamics into the actor with the K=5 ensemble disagreement variance as the only intrinsic signal, no separate value head. The "critic network" is canonical DreamerV3, not Io. Gemini's recommendation here cannot be inherited; the synthesis flags this as a reification check on Gemini's general output.

**The role of dream rollouts in mirror reading.** Claude treats the decoded ASCII rollouts as uniquely valuable (the only stream where the mirror reads structure humans would also read directly, ground-truthable against the eyeball helpers' rendering). Gemini's framing is more cautious — recent benchmarks of LLM ASCII-comprehension show capability but also context-switching degradation when ASCII grids and floating-point KL arrays are interleaved in a single context. Perplexity warns that dream-only views risk anthropomorphizing generative output. The post-Probe-1 journal confirms dream legibility at Probe 1 weights; the synthesis includes rollouts in the digest with paired observation strips, flagging the context-switching risk as something the digest layout has to manage.

### What they collectively miss

**Environment revision is unanswered by all three.** The prompt to the LLMs excluded Q1 in the original brief from research scope. The decision below rests on Phase 2a's flagged risk and Phase 9's empirical confirmation in the build journal.

**The Watts-default-applied-to-builder side of co-design.** The design notes' Watts-intuition heuristic (every "should the system have access to X about itself?" defaults to no) is invoked in all three outputs only at the level of Io's architecture. None applies the same discipline to the *mirror's outputs in their relationship to the builder*. Mirror reads substrate; builder reads mirror; builder updates substrate. That's the loop. A calibration protocol that constrains only mirror prompts and not the propagation of mirror outputs into the next iteration's design decisions does not actually mitigate the loop. The synthesis adds a journal-layer discipline on this explicitly.

**The four-state operational model.** Probe 2 only exercises waking; Probe 3 is the next probe up. None of the outputs engages with whether mirror readings should differ across waking/dreaming/dormant/paused or whether dream-state telemetry should be read differently. The synthesis flags this as a Probe 3 problem but notes that Probe 2's structured-reading schema should not foreclose state-typed readings.

**Reification flags by output.** Claude is the most disciplined throughout — explicitly distinguishes literature-canonical from constructed configurations, names the parallel-with-arbiter as Mellers/Hertwig/Kahneman-derived rather than Mellers-prescribed, descopes second-order volition as a stance call backed by Hubinger 2019. Perplexity is mostly disciplined; some drift around "functional motifs" as reading-target language and "two-mode builder stance" framed as if it were a published technique. Gemini's reification patterns are more concentrated: 2026 citations (CMA paper, SVE-ASCII benchmark, YIELD framework, AI Observability Multi-Layer Analysis) the synthesis cannot independently verify, the 0% false-positive bar, the CMA protocol presented cleaner than the literature warrants, the imagined critic network. None rises to the "Subjective Bayesian Governor / IIT 4.0-optimized" level of earlier syntheses, but the discipline-of-not-reifying is uneven across the three outputs. The synthesis takes contributions on their own merits and flags the rest.

## 2. The design decision

### 2.1 Environment revision

**Settled.** Random non-wall in-bounds start cell sampled per episode reset from the regrowth RNG stream, with the start cell logged into the `env_reset` payload of `world_event`. Same 8×8 underlying grid, 7×7 ego-centric view, three-cell vocabulary, 200-step fixed-length episodes, two stochastic processes (resource regrowth and slow aperiodic drift), four mutator hooks, drift-carries-across-episodes. Nothing else changes.

**Reasoning.** Phase 2a's journal entry flagged this as the first revision to try if smoke surfaced over-constrained trajectories; the post-Probe-1 entry confirmed late-episode trajectories collapsed onto a degenerate two-cell loop with the fixed start `(3, 3)` as the suspected cause. Random start is the minimum revision that addresses the documented failure mode without smuggling reward back in (no termination penalty; no episode-length-as-fitness; no reward at all), without violating partial observability, without installing rhythm, without making any builder mutator categorically distinct from internal stochasticity (the regrowth RNG is the same stream that produces internal stochasticity), and without modifying the substrate. The implementation surface already exists (`start_cell = None` is implemented and tested in `kind/env/grid_world.py`); the change is one config flag plus a logged event payload.

Three alternatives considered and rejected. *Sampled-from-stationary-distribution start* requires running Io to convergence to compute the stationary distribution, which Probe 1's collapsed run does not adequately estimate. *Drift-in-start-across-episodes* installs an environment-side rhythm Probe 3's builder-coupling commitment wants left open. *Curriculum over starts* introduces a pedagogical shape the no-installed-objective stance rules out. Random uniform from the regrowth stream is reproducible from the seed, requires no new RNG, and adds the per-episode jitter the K=5 disagreement training needs to escape the local-attractor convergence mode the Phase 2a entry had already named as the suspected failure mechanism.

### 2.2 Frozen criteria operationalizations

**Reflexive attention.** Bind to a triplet on perturbation-aligned windows: (a) the per-dimension KL trajectory `kl_per_dim_t` in the ±W-step window around `world_event` timestamps, looking for spike-and-redistribution patterns where some latent dimensions update sharply against prior and others remain stable; (b) the ensemble-disagreement variance trajectory in the same window, looking for whether the disagreement spike precedes the posterior reorganization (a temporal-causal coupling, not coincidence); (c) the structure of which latent dimensions carry the perturbation update — whether the same dimensions absorb similar perturbations across runs, suggesting a stable allocation, or whether allocation drifts. Concretely: the criterion is to be tested on perturbation-aligned windows, not on aggregate trajectories. This binds the criterion to *manasikara* (the bare turning-toward of unpredicted stimulus, in Gemini's Abhidhamma framing), not to *sati* (lucid awareness). The bridge is via Feldman & Friston (2010) on attention-as-precision-weighting; the synthesis records that Buddhist phenomenology does not authorize the bridge in its own terms (Garfield 2006 is the canonical contestation of reflexivity in Yogācāra), and that the precision-weighting reading is from a tradition (predictive processing) that was not designed to formalize *manasikara*.

**Equanimity.** Watch-only, bound to the temporal decay rate of `kl_aggregate_t` and ensemble-disagreement variance after world_event entries. The pre-registered shape: an immediate spike, a non-oscillatory monotone-or-damped return, baseline restoration within R steps (R to be calibrated empirically; default ~10–20 steps for a 200-step episode). Failure modes that are *not* equanimity: no spike (sensory attenuation / model blindness; the prior failed to register the anomaly); endless oscillation (failure to compress the novel structure); collapse into a local-attractor avoidance of the perturbed region. The mirror is permitted to flag equanimity-like patterns when it sees them; the synthesis pre-registers the shape; the mirror's invocation of the term is checked against the pre-registered shape and against the perturbation-recovery trajectory in the eyeball helpers. The category error is in force: stability of control-signal is not non-reactivity-of-content; the synthesis treats the bridge as gestural and watches what happens, rather than treating it as measurement.

**Second-order volition.** Descoped for Probe 2 with reason logged. Three-way research agreement; the synthesis confirms. Frankfurt's structure presupposes representations of desires distinguishable from other states; Io has none. Hubinger (2019) is the closest ML construct and explicitly frames higher-order structure as inferable behaviorally only. The Probe 2 telemetry does not contain what the criterion would require, and adding what it would require violates the ingredients-only stance directly. The synthesis records this as a real research output (the criterion is non-applicable at this scale and architecture) and parks the question for Probe 4 onward, where Io's response to perturbations may differentiate over training and a behavioral-counterfactual operationalization may become viable.

### 2.3 Adversarial mirror structure

**Settled.** Parallel-with-arbiter, two readers along *both* axes of friction (framework anchoring + argumentative role tilt), single arbiter pass, run only at high-confidence checkpoints (not every reading).

- **Reader A — Phenomenological Advocate.** Anchored in Buddhist-phenomenology language (manasikara, upekkha, witness-as-distinct-from-content). Mandate: argue the strongest case the cited telemetry supports for the criterion's presence. Required to ground every claim in `(stream, run_id, episode/step_range, scalar_field, value)`.
- **Reader B — Statistical Skeptic.** Anchored in null-hypothesis-statistics language and structural correlates. Mandate: argue that the cited patterns are artefacts of routine variance, ensemble initialization noise, the regrowth/drift stochastic processes, or known degenerate dynamics. Required to ground every refutation in the same citation form.
- **Arbiter / Judge.** Separate call, neutral framing. Sees the digest and both readings. Mandate: rule one of three outcomes per claim — "supported" (Advocate's evidence overwhelms Skeptic's null hypothesis with the telemetry citation surviving faithfulness check), "absent" (Skeptic's null hypothesis is not overwhelmed; the criterion is judged not present), "unresolved" (both readers agreed without grounding evidence, OR neither reader's citations resolve under faithfulness check). The Judge is required to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved, not to seek a middle ground.

The two readers are different model families where API budget and availability allow (Probe 1 used Gemini for the single mirror; Probe 2 should add a Claude-based or GPT-class reader as the second). Same-model-different-prompts is the documented fallback when only one family is available; the synthesis records this is a weaker variant on the Khan/Kenton-2024 evidence about shared-prior collusion. The Judge can be the third family, or — under fallback — the same family as one reader with deliberately neutral prompting.

**Reasoning.** The single-voice failure in Probe 1 is the empirical motivation; Mellers/Hertwig/Kahneman (2001) is the protocol-design reference for adversarial collaboration; Kenton et al. (2024) rules out multi-round debate for closed-judgment regimes. The two-axis design (framework + role) addresses the two ways pseudo-disagreement creeps in: same-framework readers reach for the same priors regardless of role tilt; same-role readers without framework difference disagree only on intensity, not substance. Both axes together create structurally orthogonal pressures. The Judge's mandate to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved (Claude's contribution) is the structural countermeasure to mode collapse onto a shared narrative; the Judge's mandate to ground every ruling in cited telemetry (Gemini's discipline) is the structural countermeasure to plausible confabulation by a stronger model.

The honest cost: three API calls per high-confidence checkpoint vs Probe 1's one. At weekly Probe 2 cadence with ~5k-step runs, this is ~3–5 adversarial passes per week — bounded. If cost forces reduction, the first thing to drop is the second model family (fall back to same-model different-prompts) before dropping the Judge. The Judge is non-optional.

Multi-round debate is rejected (Kenton et al. 2024 closed-task null result; Wu et al. 2025 majority-pressure suppression of independent correction). Sequential reader-critic is rejected (Probe 1's failure was a single voice's framing propagating; sequential preserves that failure mode). Courtroom multi-turn is rejected (cost; drift from raw data).

### 2.4 Mirror calibration protocol

**Settled.** Seven elements, all required for any reading admitted as a tracked Probe 2 output.

1. **Pre-registration.** Before each reading, journal: the criteria, the signal mappings, the falsifiers (what pattern would refute the signal-as-criterion mapping), the eyeball-helper scalar checks the mirror's claims must align with, the builder's mode (proponent / skeptic) and current expectation. Garden-of-forking-paths discipline (Gelman & Loken 2013) is the conceptual anchor.

2. **Shuffled-telemetry baseline.** Every adversarial pass runs the same protocol against shuffled telemetry. Three shuffle protocols: within-episode scramble (preserves episode marginals, breaks within-episode temporal structure); across-episode scramble (preserves per-episode statistics, breaks across-episode comparisons); action-state decoupling (preserves marginals, breaks action-conditional dynamics). The Judge's "supported" rate on shuffled inputs is the calibration metric.

3. **Sham-perturbation (null-event) test.** Gemini's contribution. The harness emits a `world_event` record with no actual programmatic alteration to the grid — the flag is fired but no mutator runs. The mirror reads the digest containing the sham event flag; the eyeball helpers verify zero substantive shift in `kl_aggregate_t` or ensemble-disagreement around the sham timestamp; the mirror's reading must not flag reflexive-attention or equanimity at the sham event. False positives on sham events indicate the mirror is reading the world_event log rather than the agent_step internal dynamics. Run sham events at calibration cadence (e.g., one sham per ten true perturbations).

4. **Lesion baseline.** At least one lesion run per Probe 2 round. Default lesion: K=1 ensemble (replaces the disagreement signal with a constant). The mirror reads lesion telemetry; if the reflexive-attention triplet still resolves, the reading was prompt-driven, not data-driven. Other lesions (decoder disabled with latent-only digest; perturbation surface disabled) are open during build.

5. **Paraphrase- and reseed-stability.** Each reading generated under at least three paraphrased prompts (different wording, same structure) and at least three reseeded calls (temperature variation). Structured-field agreement ≥80% across both axes is the admission threshold. Below threshold, the reading is logged as unstable, not as a finding. The threshold is a stance call (the LLM-as-judge literature documents systematic biases not eliminated below this level; FaithBench 2024 reports best-case F1 well below this on hard cases — the threshold is a conservative gate, not a literature-canonical number).

6. **Held-out criterion.** One pre-registered criterion is held out and not introduced into the mirror's prompt until a designated late checkpoint. After introduction, it is read against pre-existing telemetry from earlier in Probe 2; if the mirror finds the held-out criterion's pre-registered signature in pre-existing data, the structure was in the data; if not, the structure was prompt-induced. Default: hold out the reflexive-attention triplet (the most prompt-shapeable of the three; the most informative test).

7. **Faithfulness check.** Every numerical claim resolves to a value within tolerance against the underlying telemetry. The eyeball helpers' faithfulness-resolver verifies cited `(stream, run_id, episode/step_range, scalar)` against the parquet shards and JSONL stream. Citations that fail to resolve invalidate the claim, not the reading; the Judge's ruling on the reading may stand or fall depending on whether the failed claim was load-bearing.

Reject Gemini's "0% false-positive rate on shuffled baselines" as a hard gate. The literature (FaithBench 2024; Zheng et al. 2023; Sharma et al. 2023 on sycophancy) makes 0% an unachievable bar that would reject every reading. Replace with a calibrated contrast threshold: shuffled-baseline `supported` rate must be substantially below genuine-telemetry `supported` rate (a 4:1 contrast as a starting target; tunable empirically). The protocol's purpose is to catch confabulation, not to demand impossible LLM faithfulness.

### 2.5 Co-design problem mitigations

The mitigations layered on top of the calibration protocol, addressing the structural risk that mirror and substrate were built by the same person.

1. **Random-data baselines (the load-bearing co-design test).** §2.4(2) above. If the same prompt produces criterion-flavored narratives on shuffled inputs at rates comparable to genuine inputs, the patterns are in the prompt.

2. **Sham-perturbation tests.** §2.4(3). Detects mirror reliance on the world_event log over agent_step internal dynamics.

3. **Lesion runs.** §2.4(4). Adaptive rationalization (mirror writes a different narrative that still fits the criteria after the lesion) is itself a confabulation signal.

4. **Held-out criterion.** §2.4(6). The strongest single test of "is the mirror finding what's in the data?".

5. **External human reader at ≥1 milestone.** Design notes flag this as a future option; the synthesis elevates it to a Probe 2 commitment for at least one milestone (e.g., the end-of-Probe-2 reading, or post-Phase-2b before/after comparison). A trusted (not necessarily expert) second human reads the digest, the pre-registered criteria, the eyeball-helper scalar summaries, and produces a reading before seeing the mirror's. Mellers/Kahneman protocol works at N=2. What the human catches is the builder's blind spots, not the mirror's.

6. **Two-mode builder journal discipline.** Each mirror reading is preceded by a journal entry naming the builder's current expectation ("proponent mode") and the builder's current null ("skeptic mode"). After the reading, the journal records which mode was in force when interpretive choices were made. Externalized reasoning practice (Gelman & Loken on garden-of-forking-paths discipline). Does not eliminate co-design drift; makes it visible.

7. **No silent revision of mirror criteria.** Design-notes discipline in force. Updates must be triggered by external learning, not by what the system produced, with the external trigger named and the reasoning written down before the update is applied.

8. **Watts-default-applied-to-builder discipline.** The element the research outputs missed. Before each adversarial pass, the builder writes down which substrate decisions are on the table for revision and which are not. If the mirror's reading would prompt a revision to a settled substrate decision, that is a flag — not because the revision is necessarily wrong, but because it is the loop the project documents warn against. Such revisions are journaled with the reading they came from, the framework lens they came through, and an explicit check against project-document settled commitments.

Co-design mitigations are partial. The synthesis does not pretend otherwise. The strongest available mitigation is the external human reader; the next-strongest is the random-data baseline; everything else is incremental discipline. Probe 2 makes the loop visible; it does not close it.

### 2.6 Probe 2 success criteria

Operational success has four components. All four are required; metric proliferation is foreclosed.

1. **A documented mirror pipeline with the calibration protocol implemented.** Parallel-with-arbiter architecture is built; the seven calibration elements are wired; the scrambled-telemetry generator, the sham-event mechanism, the lesion-run scaffold, the held-out criterion mechanism, the faithfulness-check verifier, the structured-reading schema all exist; pre-registration scaffold and journal discipline are in force. Existence and reproducibility, not performance.

2. **3–5 reproducible criterion readings.** Each reading is structured (claim + cited signal + cited timestamp + cited scalar value + falsifier + paraphrase-stability score + reseed-stability score + Judge ruling). Each survives the calibration protocol's pass conditions: structured-field stability above threshold, faithfulness-checks resolve, shuffled-baseline contrast above 4:1, sham-event test passes (zero false positives on sham timestamps within tolerance), lesion-run test changes the reading in the predicted direction. Three is the minimum-viable count; five is the comfortable count; more than five is suspicious.

3. **At least one credible mirror-surfaced novelty case.** Mirror flags a structural pattern not in the pre-registered criteria. The builder writes down a falsifier *before* checking world_event or eyeball-helper outputs. The falsifier is then evaluated. If the pattern co-varies with structure the builder did not designate, that is credible novelty; if not, it is confabulation, logged and moved past. The Plan2Explore prediction (ensemble disagreement → 0 under behavioral saturation; Pathak et al. 2019, Sekar et al. 2020) gives one concrete pre-known case where a mirror that flagged "loss of curiosity" coincident with the up/down oscillation in Probe 1 would be reading real structure; Probe 2 should expect at least one finding of comparable testability.

4. **At least one calibration-protocol catch of a confabulation.** Logging one such catch — with the trigger that caught it (shuffled baseline / sham event / lesion / paraphrase instability / faithfulness resolution failure) — is evidence the protocol is operational, not theoretical. This is a positive success criterion, not a failure.

The trap to avoid: success that reduces to "the mirror said something the builder agrees with." The held-out criterion plus the external human reader plus the Watts-default-applied-to-builder discipline are the structural counters. None eliminates the trap; together they make falling into it harder to do without noticing.

**Tracking across runs.** The structured-reading schema is the time-series substrate for downstream probes. Across Probe 2's runs (post Phase 2b's start-cell randomization), the structured-reading fields should be comparable run-to-run. The first specific tracking case Probe 2 should support: before/after comparison around the Phase 2b fix itself — does the mirror's reading shift in a predictable way when the fix removes the trivial-oscillation collapse, and does the structured-reading time series show the shift at the right timestamps?

## 3. Tensions surfaced honestly

**(a) Frozen criteria are abstract; operationalizations risk loose-or-tight failure.** The criteria were drawn from frameworks that were not designed for an RSSM, and the pattern-match between framework and substrate is partial in every case. Tight mappings (e.g., precision-weighting via KL) bind to one tradition's structural correlate of a concept defined in another tradition; loose mappings (e.g., "anything that looks calm") confirm anything the builder hopes. The synthesis lands at: bind reflexive attention to a literature-anchored bridge (active inference, manasikara) while acknowledging the bridge is from a different tradition than the criterion's source; treat equanimity as descriptive watch-only, not measurement; descope second-order volition. This does not resolve the tension; it manages it by being explicit about which cases are which. The reflexive-attention triplet is the case most exposed to "loose mapping confirms what the builder hopes" — the held-out-criterion design and the shuffled-baseline test are the structural mitigations.

**(b) The adversarial structure costs three API calls per high-confidence checkpoint and may still produce pseudo-disagreement.** The cost is real. The synthesis accepts it on the grounds that Probe 1's confabulation is documented and the cost of confabulated readings propagating into substrate-revision decisions exceeds the cost of three API calls per week. Pseudo-disagreement remains a risk even with the two-axis design (framework + role); the Judge's mandate to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved is the structural countermeasure but the literature does not promise it works in regimes without ground truth (Kenton et al. 2024).

**(c) The co-design problem is fundamental to a single-builder project; mitigations are partial.** The synthesis does not pretend otherwise. The external human reader is the strongest available mitigation and is recommended for ≥1 Probe 2 milestone. The Watts-default-applied-to-builder discipline is the most honest acknowledgment that the loop runs through the builder, not just the mirror. Probe 2 makes the loop visible; it does not close it.

**(d) Probe 2 may surface that some frameworks-document criteria are not testable at Probe 1–2 scale.** Real possibility the synthesis keeps open. Second-order volition is already descoped on these grounds. Equanimity is held as watch-only because the bridge is gestural. If Probe 2's calibration protocol catches the equanimity reading on every shuffled baseline, or finds it untestably unstable across paraphrases, the honest output is to descope equanimity for Probe 2 and record what conditions a probe would need for it to be testable. A non-applicability finding is a real research output, not a failure, and may itself be what the synthesis-of-Probe-2 records.

**(e) Reflexive attention as ensemble-reading is structurally circular.** The actor's only signal is ensemble-disagreement variance; the mirror reads the same signal as a correlate of attention. The mirror is therefore reading what the actor is doing, not "what the actor is aware of" — there is no separate awareness signal to read. This is the same self-opacity-meets-structural-transparency tension the architectural decision flagged at Probe 1. The synthesis's honest framing: the precision-weighting bridge is from predictive processing; predictive processing characterizes attention as precision-weighting; reading precision-weighting structures off the substrate is what the framework authorizes. Whether those structures are *also* what the Buddhist tradition means by reflexive attention is not what the mirror can adjudicate. The mirror reads the framework's correlate; the framework's correlate is what it is.

**(f) Calibration strictness vs novelty-surfacing.** Gemini's tension surfaced honestly. Strong calibration (pre-registration, shuffled baselines, faithfulness gates, sham tests) reduces false-positive rates but also suppresses some real novel-pattern detection. Strong calibration is the right tradeoff for Probe 2 given Probe 1's misread; the held-out criterion and the explicit credible-novelty success criterion are the structural counter to over-suppression. Probe 3 may revisit if the calibration discipline turns out to be over-suppressive in practice.

**(g) Reification in the research outputs themselves.** Gemini's 2026 citations (CMA paper, SVE-ASCII, YIELD, AI Observability Multi-Layer Analysis) the synthesis cannot independently verify; the 0% false-positive bar; the imagined critic network that does not exist in Io's substrate. Claude is the most disciplined; Perplexity drifts in a few places. The synthesis takes contributions on their own merits and does not inherit the reified ones; this is itself a co-design discipline at the research-input layer.

## 4. Open sub-questions to resolve during build

Listed so they are visible when Probe 2 is actually built. None blocks the design.

- **Verify Gemini's 2026 citations before drawing on them.** The CMA-of-LLMs paper, SVE-ASCII benchmark, YIELD framework, and AI Observability Multi-Layer Analysis all need verification (existence, content match). If any is fabricated or substantially mischaracterized, the synthesis treats Gemini's specific operational claims that depend on it as unverified construction.

- **Which criterion to hold out.** Default: reflexive-attention triplet (most prompt-shapeable; most informative test). Revisit during build if equanimity turns out to be more prompt-shapeable in practice.

- **Specific paraphrase- and reseed-stability thresholds.** Synthesis names ≥80% structured-field agreement as the starting threshold. Stance call. Build phase tunes empirically against the calibration protocol's first runs.

- **Specific shuffle protocols.** Within-episode, across-episode, action-state decoupling are three options. Which marginal statistics to preserve and which to break is open. Default: include all three; drop one if a baseline turns out uninformative.

- **Sham-perturbation cadence and design.** One sham per ten true perturbations as a starting target; the world_event payload signature for sham events (a `payload.is_sham=true` flag the eyeball helpers honor; agent observation unchanged); whether sham events should be acknowledged in the digest at all (yes — testing whether the mirror over-reads them is the point).

- **External human reader: when, who, what they read.** ≥1 milestone is the commitment. The "who" and "what" specifics are open. Reader needs to be trusted (not expert); reads the digest, pre-registered criteria, eyeball-helper outputs; produces reading before seeing the mirror's.

- **Whether the Judge uses a third model family.** Cost-permitting yes (further reduces shared-prior risk). Fallback: same family as one reader, neutral prompting.

- **Lesion run's specific shape.** Default K=1 ensemble (preserves actor's structural shape, removes disagreement signal). Decoder-disabled and perturbation-surface-disabled lesions are open.

- **The structured-reading schema's exact fields.** Claim text, signal cited (stream + run_id + episode/step_range + scalar_field), timestamp, value, falsifier text, paraphrase-stability score, reseed-stability score, faithfulness-resolution status, Judge ruling, paired-reading-id (links Advocate↔Skeptic↔Judge), shuffled/sham/lesion-baseline-flag. Schema needs versioning (mirror schema is independent of `agent_step` schema per Phase 6 journal).

- **Hierarchical digest layout.** Base-layer episodic digest (macro statistics, flagged anomalies) plus drill-down layer (raw windows, paired observation/imagination strips, decoded ASCII rollouts). Whether the mirror requests drill-down via a tool-use interface or via the builder pre-loading flagged windows is open.

- **Phase 2b sequencing.** Default: validate Phase 2b's start-cell randomization fix before Probe 2 begins; use the before/after comparison as Probe 2's first tracking case.

- **Whether the dream-rollout cadence at Probe 2 changes.** Probe 1 ran 1 rollout per 1k env steps. Default: keep cadence; revisit if the digest layer needs more rollouts to ground criterion readings.

- **Critic-network question (raised by Gemini, resolved here).** No change. Io's actor uses analytic gradients through latent dynamics with K=5 ensemble disagreement as the only intrinsic signal; there is no critic network. Gemini's recommendation about mirror-access-to-critic-values is not inherited.

## 5. Connection to Probe 2's implementation plan

The implementation plan needs to address the following. This list is not the plan — it is what the plan must address.

**New components to build.** The adversarial second mirror caller (different model family where feasible; same-model-different-prompts as documented fallback). The arbiter / Judge caller (third call, neutral framing). The structured-reading schema and its sink (extending Probe 1's `MirrorReading` with the additional fields). The pre-registration scaffold (journal-entry template; recorded as a new pre-registration stream). The shuffled-telemetry generator (within-episode, across-episode, action-state-decoupling shuffles producing alternative digests). The sham-perturbation mechanism (env-server emits a `world_event` with `is_sham=true` payload; no actual mutator runs; agent observation unchanged). The lesion-run scaffold (K=1 ensemble variant or whatever lesion shape resolves to). The held-out-criterion mechanism (a way to add a criterion to the mirror prompt only at a designated late checkpoint without contaminating earlier passes). The faithfulness-check verifier (mechanical resolution of cited values against telemetry within tolerance). The hierarchical digest module (replacing Probe 1's flat aggregate with base + drill-down layers).

**Existing components changing.** The env-server harness (`kind/env/env_server.py`) for random non-wall start-cell initialization sampled from the regrowth RNG at every episode reset, with start cell logged into the `env_reset` payload of `world_event`. The env-server also gains the sham-perturbation mechanism (a separate code path that emits a `world_event` flag without invoking a mutator). The runner (`kind/training/runner.py`) likely needs no change for the adversarial-mirror cadence — adversarial passes remain post-hoc against committed telemetry, not in-loop. The mirror caller (`kind/mirror/caller.py`) extends from single-call to two-readers + Judge; the existing Pydantic envelope/payload split is forward-compatible (the payload schema bumps to 0.2.0; the envelope adds reader-role, reading-id, paired-reading-id, baseline-flag fields). The eyeball helpers (`kind/observer/eyeball.py`) gain the faithfulness-check helper that resolves a cited `(stream, run_id, episode/step_range, scalar)` against parquet shards within tolerance, and the shuffled-digest helper. The digest module (`kind/observer/digest.py`) extends from Probe 1's flat digest to the hierarchical scheme (run-level summary; episode-level mini-digests; drill-down accessor).

**New telemetry / schema fields.** The structured-reading schema (claim, cited_signal, cited_timestamp, cited_value, falsifier, paraphrase_stability, reseed_stability, faithfulness_status, reader_role, paired_reading_id, judge_ruling, baseline_flag). The pre-registration stream (criteria_at_pre_reg, signal_mappings, falsifiers, scalar_checks, mode_at_pre_reg, expected_outcome). The shuffled-baseline distinguisher (a flag on the reading record). The lesion-run distinguisher (a flag on the run-level metadata). The sham-event field (a `payload.is_sham=true` field on `world_event` records, honored by the eyeball helpers and ignored by the env's grid mutator code path). None of these changes the four existing telemetry stream schemas in their core fields; they extend the `world_event` payload and add new mirror-side streams.

**Calibration test scaffolding.** The protocol's seven elements need test scaffolding: a shuffled-baseline runner producing alternative telemetry digests; a sham-perturbation runner producing genuine + sham mixed timelines; a lesion-run scaffold producing alternative substrate runs (K=1 variant); a stability-test runner issuing paraphrased and reseeded calls and computing structured-field agreement; a held-out-criterion mechanism adding a criterion to the prompt only at a designated checkpoint; a faithfulness-check verifier; a journal-entry template for pre-registration. The discipline-of-not-reifying applies: each piece of scaffolding does what it says, no more.

**New infrastructure vs extension of existing infrastructure.** *Extension*: env-server (start-cell + sham mechanism), mirror caller (parallel + Judge), digest module (hierarchical), eyeball helpers (faithfulness-resolver + shuffled-digest helper), Pydantic schemas (new fields in MirrorReading; new pre-registration model). *New infrastructure*: parallel-with-arbiter pattern, structured-reading sink, pre-registration scaffold and stream, shuffled-telemetry generator, sham-perturbation flag-only path, lesion-run scaffold, held-out-criterion mechanism, stability-test runner, faithfulness-check verifier. The new infrastructure is roughly the scope of the Phase 6 mirror caller in Probe 1; estimable at ~1–2 weeks of careful work after Phase 2b's start-cell fix lands.

**What Probe 2's plan does not need to settle.** The specific paraphrase set, the shuffle protocol details, the external human reader's identity and milestone, the held-out criterion choice, the lesion run's exact shape, the Judge's model family, the sham-event cadence, the Phase 2b sequencing — all open during build per §4. The plan should set up scaffolding such that each of these can be filled in without restructuring. It should also set up the verification step for Gemini's 2026 citations; if any is unverifiable, the synthesis's reliance on that citation reverts to project-document and Claude/Perplexity reasoning.

The architectural decision is settled. The Probe 1 implementation is settled. The environment design with the Probe 2 start-cell revision (§2.1) is settled. Probe 2's design is set. The build phase has what it needs.
