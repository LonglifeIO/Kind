## Preamble

Three research outputs landed for Probe 2 — Claude, Gemini, Perplexity — addressing five of the six question areas the original brief named (the prompt sent to the LLMs framed environment revision as a separate Phase 2b substrate fix and excluded it from research scope; the env-revision decision below rests on the build journal, not on the research). All three converge strongly on three things: descope second-order volition with reasoning rooted in Frankfurt's representational presuppositions and Io's flat actor (Hubinger 2019 is the closest ML construct, behavioral inference only); bind reflexive attention to KL/precision-style signals authorized by predictive processing (Feldman & Friston 2010), with Gemini sharpening this to the *manasikara* (bare attention) reading from Buddhist phenomenology rather than the more contested *sati* (lucid awareness); operationalize equanimity as perturbation-recovery dynamics — the temporal decay of `kl_aggregate_t` and ensemble-disagreement variance after world_event entries — while flagging the category error (stability of control-signal is not non-reactivity-of-experience). All three reach for some form of parallel adversarial structure with a synthesizing third call, all three commit to pre-registration plus shuffled-telemetry baselines plus stability checks, and all three want a hierarchical digest with retrieval rather than raw traces or single-run aggregates.

The substantive divergences are over the *shape* of the adversarial architecture, the *strictness* of the calibration thresholds, and over whether *role tilt* is more important than *framework anchoring* in producing genuine disagreement. v1 resolved these in favor of Claude's parallel-with-arbiter at two axes (framework + role), Claude's threshold-based calibration with held-out criterion (rejecting Gemini's reified 0% false-positive bar), and a Judge with the explicit mandate to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved. v2 preserves all three of those resolutions. The reification flag against Gemini's imagined critic network and the four 2026 citations the synthesis cannot independently verify also carries through unchanged.

What v2 changes is what Probe 2 is calibrating *against*. v1 was drafted before Probe 1.5 ran. Probe 1.5's substrate was an unbuilt design at v1's drafting; the reflexive-attention triplet v1 named (per-dimension KL trajectory, ensemble-disagreement variance, latent-dimension allocation) was the substrate-side reading Probe 2 would bind. Probe 1.5 v2 added a fourth element (self-prediction error structure on perturbation-aligned windows) and a fifth (Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar `self_prediction_error_t`); the v2 implementation plan §10 named eleven Probe 2 plan revisions that follow. Probe 1.5 then ran. Phases 5 through 8 produced findings the eleven items did not anticipate and partially restructure what Probe 2's mirror reads.

The substantive Probe 1.5 findings v2 of this synthesis absorbs:

- **The actor's column does not develop during training.** Under zero-init the column is exactly zero throughout (Phase 7); under small-Gaussian init the column is byte-identical to its initialization at end of training (Phase 7.5). The structural reason is that the actor is trained only via `actor_loss.backward()` on imagined trajectories where the scalar is fed as zero (mask-via-zero-feed; plan §2.3); the gradient on the new column is mathematically zero on every step. Behavior-side conditioning is therefore *fixed-by-init*, not *developed-through-experience*. Capacity-over-exercise's "alive but unexercised" reading at the behavior-side surface acquires a third sub-distinction beyond v1's "alive vs unexercised vs installed": **capacity-as-init-shape**, where the conditioning's specific shape is determined by the random draw at construction time.

- **The substrate-shaping is largely auxiliary-target-shape-independent.** Phase 8's four-way KS comparison (Probe 1, Phase 7 zero-init, Phase 7.5 small-Gaussian, frozen-target with random projection) shows all three Probe 1.5 runs at KS-D ≈ 0.22–0.34 from Probe 1 on KL and recon, while within-Probe-1.5 distances are 2–3× smaller (KL D 0.077–0.133; recon D 0.071–0.122). The auxiliary's gradient flow into encoder/GRU/posterior reshapes the substrate measurably *regardless of what the head is predicting*, as long as it's predicting *something*. The substrate-side reading is alive but mostly generic.

- **The cleanest self-specificity signal is at the head's own loss distribution.** Phase 7 vs frozen-target `sp_err` KS-D = 0.284, the largest `sp_err` separation among the six pairings. The head learns the EMA-tracked actual next state to lower residual than it learns the random-orthogonal projection — because the EMA target has structure derivable from `h_t` while the random-orthogonal target is structureless by construction. The head's own loss distribution distinguishes self-specific from generic supervisory targets at substrate-side metrics' resolution, but that distinction does not propagate to the encoder/GRU/posterior reshaping. The head does self-specific work *internally*; its gradient flow externally produces largely generic shaping.

- **The mirror's organic-quadruplet-mention rate varies run-to-run.** Phase 7's first mirror call (Probe 1-style prompt; silent on self-prediction) surfaced 3 of 4 substrate-side quadruplet elements organically. Phase 7.5's second call surfaced 1 of 4. The variation is consistent with three plausible reads (sample variation in a non-deterministic mirror call; substrate-side signal density genuinely shifting with the policy trajectory; behavior-side conditioning too faint for the digest to surface) the data does not adjudicate. The rate is not a single-run-stable number; it varies with the run's policy trajectory and the mirror's draw.

- **The reflection-vs-self-modeling distinction was added to `Kind_design_notes.md` during Phase 7.5.** The project commits to *affording reflection* (a self-pointing quantity Io can condition on; an attention pattern that can be exercised variably across regimes) and *holding self-modeling as a possible downstream outcome* (composable from reflection if it ever happens, not an architectural target). The distinction is what Phase 7's column-zero finding triggered making explicit; Probe 2's mirror reads for *framework-recognized reflective patterns*, not for *self-modeling-shaped claims about Io's self-knowledge*.

These findings shape the v2 revision. The eleven Probe 1.5 v2 plan §10 items remain in shape; v2 adds methodology updates the eleven items did not anticipate (the three reading surfaces with different evidential weights; the column-init confound; a third lesion candidate; the prompt-revision direction the eleven items named without specifying); the success criteria's component 2 widens to allow multi-surface readings; the tensions section gains (j), (k), (l). v2 also engages with whether the deferred environmental-auxiliary control should be built before Probe 2's build resumes.

## 1. Synthesis across the research outputs

### Where they agree

**Second-order volition is descoped for Probe 2.** All three outputs reach the same destination by different routes. Frankfurt (1971) defines second-order volition as the reflective endorsement of a first-order desire as one's effective will; Io has no representations of "desires as such" distinguishable from other states, no internal evaluation layer, and no machinery that could implement endorsement. Hubinger et al. (2019) on mesa-optimization is the closest ML construct and is explicit that higher-order objective structure must be inferred behaviorally, not read from internal signals. Forcing the criterion onto policy-entropy data is what guarantees confabulation. The three converge: the honest output is to descope with the reason logged. v2 preserves this descope unchanged. Probe 1.5's scalar self-prediction error read by the actor is not a higher-order policy; the descope holds against the new affordance.

**Reflexive attention binds, with caution, to precision-weighting signals at multiple reading surfaces.** Feldman & Friston (2010) is the canonical literature bridge — attention as inference about the precision of sensory causes. v1's substrate-side correlates were `kl_per_dim_t` (per-dimension posterior-prior divergence), ensemble-disagreement variance over the K=5 heads, and the coupling between disagreement events and subsequent posterior reorganizations. Probe 1.5 v2 adds the head's self-prediction error structure on perturbation-aligned windows and Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar; v2 of *this* synthesis absorbs both, then redistributes them across three reading surfaces (§2.2 below). Gemini's contribution sharpens the criterion at the source: in Abhidhamma/Buddhist phenomenology *manasikara* (bare attention, the pre-conceptual turning toward an object) is operationally distinct from *sati* (lucid awareness/mindfulness); the structural correlate the substrate can carry is *manasikara*, not *sati*. The bridge is from one tradition (predictive processing) to a structural correlate of a concept in another tradition (Buddhist phenomenology); the match is partial and the synthesis records that. Reflection in the design-notes sense is the attention pattern; the framework-recognized correlates the mirror reads for are reflection's substrate-side, head-internal, and behavior-side surfaces.

**Equanimity binds, more gesturally, to perturbation-recovery dynamics.** All three converge on a perturbation-anchored shape: under unmarked builder perturbations (the harness's mutators, recorded in `world_event` with no agent-side observation marker), the equanimity-like trajectory shows an immediate spike in KL/disagreement followed by a non-oscillatory return to baseline within some recovery window, with no collapse into avoidance loops over the perturbed regions. Gemini sharpens this to the temporal derivative `d/dt kl_aggregate_t` as the operational signal; Claude flags that this is the literature's *behavioral* operationalization (Vago & Silbersweig 2014) of what the contemplative tradition characterizes as a stance-toward-content; Perplexity emphasizes the symmetry test (recovery on perturbations across affective valence — though Io has no affective valence, only structural variance, so the homology is partial). All three flag the category error explicitly. v2 extends the operationalization to the head's self-prediction error trajectory under perturbation (substrate-side spike-and-recovery in the auxiliary's residual) and to Io's behavioral non-reactivity to the scalar's spike-and-recovery (behavior-side recovery shape) per the Probe 1.5 v2 plan §10(6) extension; the category error is in force at every reading surface.

**Some form of parallel adversarial structure with a synthesizing third call.** The single-mirror confabulation in Probe 1 (overstated patterns, reversed entropy direction, single outliers called frequent) is the empirical motivation. All three reach for parallel-with-third-call, and all three reject multi-round debate (Du et al. 2023 multi-agent debate works on closed factual tasks; Kenton et al. 2024 shows the advantage erodes on closed-judgment tasks where the judge cannot independently verify, which is Probe 2's regime). All three flag pseudo-disagreement, sycophancy, mode collapse, and shared-narrative drift. v2 preserves the architecture in shape and extends each reader's mandate to distinguish substrate-side claims from head-internal claims from behavior-side claims (§2.3 below; absorbed from Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 7).

**Pre-registration plus shuffled-telemetry baselines plus stability tests plus faithfulness checks is the calibration protocol's spine.** Three-way convergence. Gelman & Loken (2013) on the garden of forking paths is the conceptual anchor; pre-registration constrains analytic freedom, shuffled baselines distinguish prompt-driven from data-driven readings, paraphrase- and reseed-stability rule out single-call artefacts, citation-grounding makes claims faithfulness-checkable. The scrambled-telemetry test is named explicitly by all three as the highest-yield single tool given Kind's commitments. v2 extends each element to operate at all three reading surfaces (§2.4 below; absorbs Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 8 plus Phase 8 additions).

**Hierarchical digest with retrieval beats raw traces and beats single-run aggregates.** Three-way convergence on the layered scheme: a base-layer episodic digest with macro statistics and flagged anomalies; a triggered drill-down layer for episodes or time windows the mirror flags; access to decoded ASCII dream rollouts (whose Probe-1 legibility is confirmed). Long-context evaluation literature (NIAH, lost-in-the-middle effects) is the reason raw traces fail; Probe 1's misread is the reason single aggregates fail. v2 extends the digest to expose the three reading surfaces' fields (substrate-side per-dim KL allocation, ensemble-disagreement-variance trajectories; head-internal `sp_err` distribution and trajectory; behavior-side action-distribution-per-state under controlled scalar perturbation) per Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 1 plus Phase 8 additions.

### Where they diverge

(Carried forward unchanged from v1; v1's text on adversarial-architecture choice, calibration thresholds, the imagined critic network, and the role of dream rollouts in mirror reading is preserved here. Three substantive disagreements: parallel-with-arbiter vs sequential vs Advocate/Skeptic+Judge; 0% false-positive vs 80%+ stability vs medium strictness; whether the mirror imagines a critic network in Io's substrate. v1's resolutions stand: parallel-with-arbiter at two axes; threshold-based with 4:1 contrast; no critic.)

### What they collectively miss

**Environment revision is unanswered by all three.** Carried forward from v1; the start-cell randomization decision below rests on Phase 2a's flagged risk and Phase 9's empirical confirmation in the Probe 1 build journal, not on the research outputs.

**The Watts-default-applied-to-builder side of co-design.** Carried forward from v1. Mirror reads substrate; builder reads mirror; builder updates substrate. That's the loop. v2 preserves the journal-layer discipline and adds the Probe 1.5 v2 §2(b) discipline sub-clause: before any future probe adds a new actor-readable interface, the journal entry must address (i) which project-document affordance the read serves, (ii) whether the read is the minimum form, (iii) whether alternatives were considered, (iv) what failure-mode controls bound the slippage. Probe 1.5's PolicyView extension is the case the discipline was written against; future probes inherit the four-part structure.

**The four-state operational model.** Carried forward from v1; Probe 2 only exercises waking, Probe 3 is the next probe up, the structured-reading schema does not foreclose state-typed readings.

**The three reading surfaces produced by Probe 1.5 (NEW in v2).** None of the three research outputs anticipated that Probe 1.5's substrate would expose three reading surfaces with measurably different evidential weights for criterion claims. v1 of this synthesis read everything against substrate-side patterns (the v1 reflexive-attention triplet); Probe 1.5 v2 added head-internal and behavior-side reading surfaces by adding the self-prediction head and the actor's read-access to the scalar; Phase 8 produced empirical evidence that the three surfaces stratify by confabulation susceptibility and by self-specificity. v2 of this synthesis absorbs the stratification (§2.2 below) as a methodology update the eleven items did not name.

**Reification flags by output.** Carried forward unchanged from v1.

## 2. The design decision

### 2.1 Environment revision

**Settled. Unchanged from v1.** Random non-wall in-bounds start cell sampled per episode reset from the regrowth RNG stream, with the start cell logged into the `env_reset` payload of `world_event`. Same 8×8 underlying grid, 7×7 ego-centric view, three-cell vocabulary, 200-step fixed-length episodes, two stochastic processes (resource regrowth and slow aperiodic drift), four mutator hooks, drift-carries-across-episodes. Nothing else changes.

Reasoning unchanged from v1: Phase 2a's journal entry flagged this as the first revision to try if smoke surfaced over-constrained trajectories; the post-Probe-1 entry confirmed late-episode trajectories collapsed onto a degenerate two-cell loop with the fixed start `(3, 3)` as the suspected cause. Random start is the minimum revision that addresses the documented failure mode without smuggling reward back in, without violating partial observability, without installing rhythm, without making any builder mutator categorically distinct from internal stochasticity, and without modifying the substrate. Three alternatives considered and rejected: sampled-from-stationary-distribution start, drift-in-start-across-episodes, curriculum over starts.

### 2.2 Frozen criteria operationalizations — three reading surfaces with differing evidential weights (REVISED)

v1 named a triplet of substrate-side correlates for reflexive attention; Probe 1.5 v2 added a fourth (self-prediction error structure) and a fifth (Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar) as a quadruplet binding at two reading surfaces. **v2 of this synthesis redistributes the five elements across three reading surfaces with explicit different evidential weights, absorbed from the Probe 1.5 build's Phase 7, 7.5, and 8 findings.** This is the most substantive change v2 makes to the synthesis's operationalizations.

**Reflexive attention.** Bind to a quintuplet on perturbation-aligned windows, distributed across three reading surfaces:

*Substrate-side (signals a, b, c — weakest evidential weight).*

a. **Per-dimension KL trajectory `kl_per_dim_t` in the ±W-step window around `world_event` timestamps.** Looking for spike-and-redistribution patterns where some latent dimensions update sharply against prior and others remain stable; whether the same dimensions absorb similar perturbations across runs (suggesting a stable allocation) or whether allocation drifts.

b. **Ensemble-disagreement variance trajectory in the same window.** Looking for whether the disagreement spike precedes the posterior reorganization (a temporal-causal coupling, not coincidence).

c. **The structure of which latent dimensions carry the perturbation update.** Per-dim allocation patterns that may stabilize across runs or drift.

*Head-internal (signal d — strongest evidential weight for self-specificity).*

d. **Head's self-prediction error trajectory (`self_prediction_error_t` distribution and per-dimension allocation, including under perturbation-aligned windows).** Looking for: spike-and-recovery patterns in the head's residual; whether the spike precedes or coincides with substrate-side per-dim KL reorganization (a temporal-causal coupling); whether the recovery shape is monotone-or-damped (an equanimity-like pattern in self-prediction-error space); per-dimension allocation of self-prediction accuracy across `h_t`.

*Behavior-side (signal e — most confabulation-susceptible evidential weight).*

e. **Io's behavioral conditioning on the scalar `self_prediction_error_t` in the same windows.** Does the actor's policy depend on the scalar in regimes the framework recognizes as reflexive-attention-bearing; does the dependence have the variability and regime-dependence capacity-over-exercise predicts versus the fixed-structural shape installation produces; does the conditioning's pattern reflect *Io's experience* shaping it during training, or does it reflect *the column's init draw* shaping it from step 0 (the capacity-as-init-shape distinction Phase 8 surfaced).

**The evidential-weight ordering (this is the substantive v2 framing).** Substrate-side patterns (signals a, b, c) are real but mostly auxiliary-target-shape-independent — Phase 8's four-way KS comparison shows that within-Probe-1.5-family distances on substrate metrics (KL D 0.077–0.133; recon D 0.071–0.122) are 2–3× smaller than the cross-family distances to Probe 1 (KS-D 0.22–0.34). The substrate-side reshaping happens regardless of what the head is predicting, as long as it's predicting something. **Mirror claims at the substrate-side reading surface can support reflexive-attention claims only weakly because the patterns are observable across runs with different supervisory targets** (the EMA-tracked online target, the random-orthogonal frozen target). The cleaner self-specificity signal is at the head's own loss distribution: Phase 7 vs frozen-target `sp_err` KS-D = 0.284, the largest `sp_err` separation among the six pairings. **Mirror claims at the head-internal reading surface are the strongest because the head's residual distinguishes self-specific from generic supervisory targets at measurable resolution.** Behavior-side conditioning is real but interpretively contested: Probe 1.5 v2's actor reads the scalar, but the actor's training pathway leaves the new column unmoved during training; the conditioning's specific shape is determined by the random draw at construction time, not by Io's experience. **Mirror claims at the behavior-side reading surface are most confabulation-susceptible because the behavioral language is more flexible and the conditioning's pattern reflects init-draw rather than developmental shaping.**

**Reading-surface pre-registration.** Probe 2's pre-registration (§2.4 element 1 below; expanded from v1's text) requires explicit naming of which reading surface each frozen criterion reads against. Multi-surface readings (a claim that resolves at substrate-side AND head-internal AND behavior-side) are stronger evidence than single-surface readings; readings that resolve only at the substrate-side surface are weakest. The v1 success criterion §2.6 component 2's "3-5 reproducible criterion readings" widens to allow readings at any of the three surfaces to count toward the 3-5; multi-surface readings count more.

**The reflection-vs-self-modeling distinction binds the criterion shape (NEW in v2).** Per `Kind_design_notes.md` "Reflection and self-modeling" section (added during Probe 1.5 Phase 7.5): reflection is an attention pattern that can be exercised variably; self-modeling is structured self-knowledge that might emerge if reflection composes. Probe 2's mirror reads for *framework-recognized reflective patterns* (manasikara via Buddhist phenomenology, precision-weighting via Feldman & Friston, prediction-of-self via active inference). The mirror does *not* read for self-modeling-shaped claims (claims about Io having a self-model; claims about Io's self-knowledge; claims about Io modeling its own modeling). Self-modeling-shaped claims are premature for Probe 1.5's substrate — the column doesn't develop, the conditioning is fixed-by-init, and any claim about Io's self-knowledge would conflate the architecture's slot with Io's exercise of it. The Advocate's prompt names reflection-shaped manifestations explicitly; the prompt is silent on self-modeling vocabulary; the Skeptic's mandate includes the refutation "this is a substrate slot whose reading would conflate slot-existence with self-knowledge."

**Equanimity.** Watch-only, bound to the temporal decay rate of `kl_aggregate_t` and ensemble-disagreement variance after world_event entries. v2 extends the operationalization to the head's self-prediction error perturbation-recovery (substrate-side spike-and-recovery in the auxiliary's residual; signal d's trajectory shape) and to Io's behavioral non-reactivity to the scalar's spike-and-recovery (behavior-side; signal e's recovery profile). The pre-registered shape: an immediate spike, a non-oscillatory monotone-or-damped return, baseline restoration within R steps (R to be calibrated empirically; default ~10–20 steps for a 200-step episode). Failure modes that are *not* equanimity: no spike (sensory attenuation / model blindness); endless oscillation (failure to compress the novel structure); collapse into a local-attractor avoidance of the perturbed region. The category error is in force at every reading surface: stability of control-signal is not non-reactivity-of-content; the bridge is gestural; the behavior-side reading is closer to the literature's behavioral operationalization (Vago & Silbersweig 2014) than the substrate-side reading was, but the bridge is still gestural.

**Second-order volition.** Descoped for Probe 2 with reason logged. Unchanged from v1. Probe 1.5's scalar self-prediction error read by the actor is not a higher-order policy; the descope holds against the new affordance.

### 2.3 Adversarial mirror structure (REVISED)

**Settled.** Parallel-with-arbiter, two readers along *both* axes of friction (framework anchoring + argumentative role tilt), single arbiter pass, run only at high-confidence checkpoints (not every reading). Architecture unchanged in shape from v1; **each reader's mandate now distinguishes substrate-side claims from head-internal claims from behavior-side claims** (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 7, with the head-internal surface promoted to first-class status from Phase 8's reading).

- **Reader A — Phenomenological Advocate.** Anchored in Buddhist-phenomenology language (manasikara, upekkha, witness-as-distinct-from-content). Mandate: argue the strongest case the cited telemetry supports for the criterion's presence, naming explicitly which reading surface (substrate-side / head-internal / behavior-side) each claim sits on. Required to ground every claim in `(stream, run_id, episode/step_range, scalar_field, value)`. The Advocate's framework anchors gain "the second success criterion's capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention" as the synthesizing concept across the three surfaces; the Advocate names *reflection* (attention pattern) without claiming *self-modeling* (structured self-knowledge).

- **Reader B — Statistical Skeptic.** Anchored in null-hypothesis-statistics language and structural correlates. Mandate: argue that the cited patterns are artefacts of routine variance, ensemble initialization noise, the regrowth/drift stochastic processes, known degenerate dynamics, *or — at the substrate-side surface — auxiliary-target-shape-independent encoder-shaping*, *or — at the behavior-side surface — column-init-draw determination of policy*. Required to ground every refutation in the same citation form. The Skeptic's mandate gains the substrate-genericity refutation as an explicit candidate ("the cited substrate-side pattern is observable across Phase 7, Phase 7.5, and frozen-target runs at comparable KS-D; the pattern reflects encoder-shaping that is auxiliary-target-shape-independent, not self-specific reflexive attention"); the column-init-determination refutation is also explicit at the behavior-side surface ("the cited conditioning reflects the column's small-Gaussian draw at construction, not Io's developmental exercise of the affordance").

- **Arbiter / Judge.** Separate call, neutral framing. Sees the digest and both readings. Mandate: rule one of three outcomes per claim per reading surface — "supported", "absent", "unresolved". The Judge is required to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved, not to seek a middle ground. **The Judge's ruling is per-claim per-surface**: a claim that resolves at the head-internal surface but not at the substrate-side surface gets a per-surface ruling; the synthesizing reading is whether multi-surface readings strengthen the criterion's presence claim or whether single-surface readings are weaker evidence by design. The two-axis architecture (framework + role) preserves; the per-surface rulings are the v2 extension.

The two readers are different model families where API budget and availability allow. The Judge can be the third family, or — under fallback — the same family as one reader with deliberately neutral prompting. Reasoning unchanged from v1.

The honest cost: three API calls per high-confidence checkpoint vs Probe 1's one. Multi-round debate is rejected (Kenton et al. 2024 closed-task null result; Wu et al. 2025 majority-pressure suppression). Sequential reader-critic is rejected (Probe 1's failure was a single voice's framing propagating; sequential preserves that failure mode). Courtroom multi-turn is rejected (cost; drift from raw data).

### 2.4 Mirror calibration protocol (REVISED)

**Settled.** Seven elements, all required for any reading admitted as a tracked Probe 2 output. v2 updates each element to operate at all three reading surfaces.

1. **Pre-registration.** Before each reading, journal: the criteria, the signal mappings, the falsifiers, the eyeball-helper scalar checks the mirror's claims must align with, the builder's mode (proponent / skeptic) and current expectation. Garden-of-forking-paths discipline (Gelman & Loken 2013). **Pre-registration must explicitly name what reading surface (substrate-side, head-internal, behavior-side) each criterion is being read against, what asymmetry of access between Io and the mirror applies (Io reads the scalar `self_prediction_error_t`; the mirror reads the full prediction vector `ĥ_{t+1}`, the per-dimension decomposition, the perturbation-recovery dynamics, the behavioral-conditioning analysis across the trajectory, the masked flag, all longitudinal cross-run analysis), and what reading surface(s) the claim is allowed to resolve at to be admitted as supported.** This was implicit in v1; v2 makes it explicit because the asymmetry is now load-bearing for what the readers can claim and the three-surface stratification determines what evidential weight the supported claim carries.

2. **Shuffled-telemetry baseline.** Every adversarial pass runs the same protocol against shuffled telemetry. Three shuffle protocols: within-episode scramble (preserves episode marginals, breaks within-episode temporal structure); across-episode scramble (preserves per-episode statistics, breaks across-episode comparisons); action-state decoupling (preserves marginals, breaks action-conditional dynamics). **v2 extends to a fourth: scalar-shuffle-within-trajectory** (preserves per-step marginals on the scalar `self_prediction_error_t`, breaks within-trajectory dynamics; specifically informative for the behavior-side conditioning reading). The Judge's "supported" rate on shuffled inputs is the calibration metric, computed per reading surface. The shuffle protocols extend across substrate-side, head-internal, and behavior-side claims; each surface produces its own shuffled-baseline contrast.

3. **Sham-perturbation (null-event) test.** The harness emits a `world_event` record with no actual programmatic alteration to the grid — the flag is fired but no mutator runs. The mirror reads the digest containing the sham event flag; the eyeball helpers verify zero substantive shift in `kl_aggregate_t` or ensemble-disagreement around the sham timestamp; **v2 extends: zero substantive shift in head-internal `sp_err` and zero substantive shift in behavior-side scalar-conditioning around the sham timestamp**; the mirror's reading must not flag reflexive-attention or equanimity at the sham event at any of the three reading surfaces. False positives on sham events at any surface indicate the mirror is reading the world_event log rather than the agent_step internal dynamics. Run sham events at calibration cadence (e.g., one sham per ten true perturbations).

4. **Lesion baseline.** At least one lesion run per Probe 2 round. **v2 names three lesion candidates with the choice between them open during build:**

   - *`disable_self_prediction`* (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 2): replace the head's output with a fixed zero tensor and skip the EMA target update; the head is structurally present but does no work. Substrate-side lesion; tests whether the substrate-side patterns require the head's gradient flow.
   - *`zero_or_randomize_scalar`* (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 2): the head still trains and the EMA target still updates, but at evaluation time the actor's PolicyView's `self_prediction_error` field is zeroed out (or replaced by random uniform from the empirical range); lesions the behavior-side path while leaving the substrate-side intact. Behavior-side lesion; tests whether Io's policy depends on the scalar.
   - *`init_zero_scalar_column` (NEW in v2)* (Phase 8's recommended new candidate): load a Phase-7.5/frozen-target-shape checkpoint, replace the actor's `actor.net.0.weight[:, h_dim+z_dim:]` column with zeros, and re-run evaluation. Tests whether the late-trajectory policy regime depends on the column's specific init values (the L/R bipolar end-state for both Phase 7.5 and frozen-target Phase 8 runs) or on the *non-zero magnitude* of the column more generally. If the lesioned actor's policy collapses to Phase 7's u/d regime, the column-init-determined behavior reading is confirmed; if it stays at L/R, something else is shaping the regime. Per-init-shape lesion; tests the capacity-as-init-shape distinction.
   - *`ensemble_k1`*, *`ensemble_constant`* (Probe 1's lesion candidates): preserved as alternatives if the substrate-side reading is the surface the lesion needs to test.

   Probe 2 §6 default lesion choice may shift across the three new candidates; the choice is journaled at Probe 2's first run after Probe 1.5 lands. 5. **Paraphrase- and reseed-stability.** Each reading generated under at least three paraphrased prompts (different wording, same structure) and at least three reseeded calls (temperature variation). Structured-field agreement ≥80% across both axes is the admission threshold. Below threshold, the reading is logged as unstable, not as a finding. The threshold is a stance call. Phase 7 vs Phase 7.5's mirror calls (3-of-4 organic-mention rate vs 1-of-4) suggest the reseed-instability for the same digest may be larger than v1 anticipated; Probe 2's first stability run informs whether ≥80% is achievable on Probe 1.5 substrate or whether the threshold needs adjustment.

6. **Held-out criterion.** One pre-registered criterion is held out and not introduced into the mirror's prompt until a designated late checkpoint. After introduction, it is read against pre-existing telemetry from earlier in Probe 2; if the mirror finds the held-out criterion's pre-registered signature in pre-existing data, the structure was in the data; if not, the structure was prompt-induced. **v2 default candidates expand to three:** v1's reflexive-attention triplet; the self-prediction quadruplet (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 4); **specifically the behavior-side conditioning element** (Probe 1.5 v2 §5 names this as the most prompt-shapeable of the new criteria, hence the most informative test). v2's recommended default shifts from v1's substrate-side triplet to either the head-internal `sp_err` distribution (cleanest self-specificity signal per Phase 8) or the behavior-side conditioning element (most prompt-shapeable per Probe 1.5 v2 §5). The build phase decides; the choice is journaled with the trigger.

7. **Faithfulness check.** Every numerical claim resolves to a value within tolerance against the underlying telemetry. The eyeball helpers' faithfulness-resolver verifies cited `(stream, run_id, episode/step_range, scalar)` against the parquet shards and JSONL stream. Citations that fail to resolve invalidate the claim, not the reading; the Judge's ruling on the reading may stand or fall depending on whether the failed claim was load-bearing. **v2 extends the faithfulness check to resolve cited claims at whichever surface the claim sits on**: substrate-side citations resolve against `kl_per_dim_t` / ensemble-disagreement / per-dim allocation arrays; head-internal citations resolve against `self_prediction_t` (the vector) and `self_prediction_error_t` (the scalar) trajectories; behavior-side citations resolve against the per-state action-distribution-under-perturbation tables (the `show_self_prediction_conditioning` helper's output, formalized as the conditioning analysis module per Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 11). Any citation that aggregates `self_prediction_error_t` over a step range must declare whether masked steps are included or excluded (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 3); the verifier rejects citations that don't.

The 0% false-positive bar is rejected (carried from v1; Gemini's reified threshold). The calibrated contrast is **the shuffled-baseline `supported` rate must be substantially below genuine-telemetry `supported` rate at each reading surface (a 4:1 contrast as a starting target; tunable empirically per surface).** Phase 8's reading shows the substrate-side signal density may differ from the head-internal and behavior-side densities; the threshold may need re-tuning per surface against Probe 1.5's substrate's signal density. ### 2.6 Probe 2 success criteria (REVISED)

Operational success has four components. All four are required; metric proliferation is foreclosed. v2 widens components 2 and 3 to allow multi-surface readings (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 9).

Existence and reproducibility, not performance.

2. **3–5 reproducible criterion readings (REVISED).** Each reading is structured (claim + cited signal + cited timestamp + cited scalar value + falsifier + paraphrase-stability score + reseed-stability score + Judge ruling **+ reading surface**). Each survives the calibration protocol's pass conditions: structured-field stability above threshold, faithfulness-checks resolve, shuffled-baseline contrast above 4:1 *at the surface the claim is being read against*, sham-event test passes (zero false positives on sham timestamps within tolerance) *at the surface the claim is being read against*, lesion-run test changes the reading in the predicted direction *at the surface the lesion targets*. **Readings at any of the three reading surfaces (substrate-side, head-internal, behavior-side) count toward the 3-5; readings that resolve at multiple surfaces are stronger evidence than single-surface readings**. Three is the minimum-viable count; five is the comfortable count; more than five is suspicious.

3. **At least one credible mirror-surfaced novelty case.** Mirror flags a structural pattern not in the pre-registered criteria. The builder writes down a falsifier *before* checking world_event or eyeball-helper outputs. The falsifier is then evaluated. If the pattern co-varies with structure the builder did not designate, that is credible novelty; if not, it is confabulation, logged and moved past. **v2 candidate domain: behavioral-conditioning patterns on the scalar that the pre-registered behavior-side criterion does not name, or per-dimension `sp_err` allocation patterns the head-internal criterion does not name.** The Plan2Explore prediction (ensemble disagreement → 0 under behavioral saturation; Pathak et al. 2019, Sekar et al. 2020) gives one concrete pre-known case where a mirror that flagged "loss of curiosity" coincident with an oscillation pattern would be reading real structure; Probe 2 should expect at least one finding of comparable testability.

4. **At least one calibration-protocol catch of a confabulation.** Logging one such catch — with the trigger that caught it (shuffled baseline / sham event / lesion / paraphrase instability / faithfulness resolution failure) **— and naming which reading surface was the catch's locus** — is evidence the protocol is operational, not theoretical. False positives can occur at any of the three surfaces; the calibration protocol must catch each kind. This is a positive success criterion, not a failure.

The trap to avoid: success that reduces to "the mirror said something the builder agrees with." The held-out criterion plus the external human reader plus the Watts-default-applied-to-builder discipline are the structural counters. None eliminates the trap; together they make falling into it harder to do without noticing.

The first specific tracking case Probe 2 should support: before/after comparison around the Phase 2b fix itself — does the mirror's reading shift in a predictable way when the fix removes the trivial-oscillation collapse, and does the structured-reading time series show the shift at the right timestamps? **v2 adds: before/after comparison around the Probe 1.5 substrate addition itself — does the mirror's reading shift in the predicted directions when the head, the EMA target, and the actor's scalar-reading column enter the substrate; does the substrate-side / head-internal / behavior-side stratification appear in the structured-reading time series at expected magnitudes?**

**(b) The adversarial structure costs three API calls per high-confidence checkpoint and may still produce pseudo-disagreement.** Carried forward from v1. Pseudo-disagreement remains a risk even with the two-axis design; the Judge's mandate to flag agreement-without-evidence as unresolved is the structural countermeasure but the literature does not promise it works in regimes without ground truth.

**(c) The co-design problem is fundamental to a single-builder project; mitigations are partial.** Carried forward from v1. v2 adds: the Watts-default-applied-to-builder discipline gains the Probe 1.5 v2 §2(b) sub-clause as a structural counter to the kind of drift that produces v2-of-Probe-1.5-style revisions — being explicit about which interface-level access is added, on what affordance grounds, with what controls.

Equanimity at the head-internal and behavior-side surfaces gains new failure modes: head-internal `sp_err` perturbation-recovery may be dominated by the auxiliary's training dynamics (the residual decreases as training progresses regardless of perturbation history), making the recovery-shape reading hard to disentangle from the training trajectory; behavior-side spike-and-recovery requires the conditioning to be present at all, which Phase 7.5 shows is faint at `KL_p90 ~ 3e-7`. If Probe 2's calibration protocol catches the equanimity reading on every shuffled baseline at one or both of these surfaces, the honest output is to descope equanimity at those surfaces and record what conditions a probe would need for it to be testable.

**(e) Reflexive attention as ensemble-reading is structurally circular at the substrate-side surface.** Carried forward from v1. v2 narrows: the head-internal surface offers a less-circular reading (the head's own loss distinguishes self-specific from generic supervisory targets at measurable resolution; this is closer to "what the auxiliary is doing" than to "what the actor is doing"); the behavior-side surface offers a less-circular reading still (the actor's policy depending on a self-pointing quantity is closer to *exercise* of the affordance the framework names than substrate-side patterns are). The structural circularity tension remains at the substrate-side surface; it is partially narrowed at the head-internal and behavior-side surfaces. None of the surfaces close it.

**(f) Calibration strictness vs novelty-surfacing.** Carried forward from v1. v2 adds: the per-surface calibration gives novelty-surfacing more channels — a behavior-side novelty case might be admissible at a different threshold than a substrate-side novelty case, because the surfaces have different confabulation susceptibilities.

**(g) Reification in the research outputs themselves.** Carried forward from v1.

**(h) The mirror's organic-quadruplet-mention rate is run-trajectory-dependent (NEW in v2).** Phase 7's first mirror call surfaced 3 of 4 substrate-side quadruplet elements organically (under a Probe 1-style prompt that named none of them); Phase 7.5's surfaced 1 of 4. The rate is not single-run-stable; it varies with the run's policy trajectory and the mirror's draw. **The implication for Probe 2's prompt revision:** the prompt's job shifts from "introduce the quadruplet's signals" to "frame the binding between signals and reflexive-attention manifestations." The digest already surfaces the substrate-side signals legibly enough that a silent prompt elicits them variably; Probe 2's prompt's added value is in the binding-frame ("the framework reads this signal as this manifestation, conditional on this surface"), not in the signal-naming. The held-out-criterion default may need to factor trajectory shape, not just the prompt. Probe 2's first stability run informs the shape this revision takes.

**(i) The interface-level vs representation-level opacity distinction is a permanent feature of the project (NEW in v2).** Carried as `Kind_design_notes.md`'s "Watts intuition" section's interface-vs-representation opacity sub-section, added during Probe 1.5 Phase 9. v2 of this synthesis notes it as a tension the project will continue to engage with: every new actor-readable signal future probes propose requires the four-part Watts-heuristic exception discipline; the discipline is what makes opacity slippage visible. Probe 2's Watts-default-applied-to-builder element 8 is the structural carrier of this discipline.

**(j) The two reading surfaces v1 named are now three reading surfaces with differently susceptible confabulation profiles (NEW in v2; absorbed from Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 10).** Substrate-side patterns are harder for the mirror to confabulate (the per-dim KL and ensemble-disagreement-variance trajectories are quantitative and resolve against parquet shards via the faithfulness check); head-internal `sp_err` patterns are similarly hard (the head's loss distribution resolves against the parquet shards); **behavior-side conditioning patterns are easier to confabulate** (the behavioral language is more flexible; "Io conditions on the scalar in perturbation-aligned windows" is a claim a strong language model can make without grounding it in actual conditional distributions). The behavior-side reading therefore needs stronger faithfulness-check support — the behavioral-conditioning analysis must produce numerical conditional distributions the mirror cites and the eyeball helpers verify (Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 11). Phase 8's data adds: substrate-side patterns are also susceptible to a different kind of confabulation — claiming self-specificity on patterns that are auxiliary-target-shape-independent. The Skeptic's mandate at the substrate-side surface includes the substrate-genericity refutation explicitly.

**(k) The three reading surfaces have different relationships to the structural circularity tension (e) (NEW in v2; absorbed from Probe 1.5 v2 §10 item 10 and Phase 8).** Substrate-side patterns are correlates the framework recognizes that are also auxiliary-target-shape-independent; head-internal patterns are correlates the framework recognizes that are auxiliary-target-shape-dependent (so they distinguish self-specific from generic supervisory targets); behavior-side patterns are closer to actual exercise of the affordance the framework names but are confabulation-susceptible and column-init-determined rather than developmentally shaped. All three still adjudicate frameworks' correlates rather than awareness; the head-internal surface narrows the structural circularity *within* the framework's own terms (the head's loss distinguishes targets in ways the framework's correlate would predict); the behavior-side surface narrows the circularity *across* framework-and-exercise (behavioral conditioning is the framework's structural correlate of exercise) but introduces a new circularity (column-init-draw determines conditioning shape rather than developmental shaping).

**(l) The actor's column doesn't develop during training; behavior-side conditioning is fixed-by-init rather than developed-through-training (NEW in v2; from Phase 8).** The structural reason: the actor is trained only via `actor_loss.backward()` on imagined trajectories where the scalar is fed as zero (mask-via-zero-feed; Probe 1.5 plan §2.3); the gradient on the new column is mathematically zero on every step. Phase 8's data confirms this: under any column-init choice the column at `ckpt-000001` is byte-identical to its initialization. **Whether the column-doesn't-develop fact counts as "the capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention" is a substantive open question Probe 2's behavior-side reading needs to engage with.** Two readings:

- *The capacity-as-architectural-slot reading.* Io has access to the quantity at every forward; whether the policy currently depends on it in regime-dependent ways is the exercise question capacity-over-exercise allows to be open. Under this reading the column-init's specific values don't matter — what matters is that the slot exists and the actor's input dimensionality includes it. The mirror's reading at the behavior-side surface adjudicates whether the conditioning, where present, has the variability and regime-dependence capacity-over-exercise predicts versus the fixed-structural shape installation produces. The capacity-as-init-shape distinction Phase 8 surfaced is *not* installation (the conditioning is variable across regimes and faint in magnitude; it is not the always-in-the-same-direction-with-the-same-magnitude shape installation produces); it is one variant of "alive at substrate-side with capacity-as-init-shape behavior-side conditioning."

- *The capacity-as-developmentally-reachable-conditioning-surface reading.* A quantity Io structurally cannot vary its policy on through training (because the column doesn't move) is not a quantity Io can take as an object of attention; the policy's regime-dependence reflects the column's init-draw rather than Io's developmental exercise. Under this reading the affordance is structurally limited at the behavior-side surface; the second success criterion's "capacity to take its own processing as an object of attention" is partially satisfied (the slot exists, the conditioning is non-zero) but the *exercise* the criterion's phrasing names ("whether or not it chooses to exercise that capacity") is foreclosed at the actor-training-pathway level.

The honest framing v2 commits to: **both readings are coherent against the project documents; the data does not adjudicate.** Probe 2's behavior-side reading is what will produce evidence for one or the other. The lesion baseline `init_zero_scalar_column` (§2.4 element 4) is the calibration test that distinguishes them — if the lesioned actor's policy collapses to a different end-regime than the small-Gaussian column produces, the column-init-determined behavior reading is confirmed and the capacity-as-developmentally-reachable reading is supported. If it stays at the same end-regime, the column-init-determined reading is weakened. Probe 3's design space (per `pre-probe3.md`) opens the path for the column to potentially move via dream-state head-engagement on imagined trajectories with non-zero scalars — this is the architectural response to tension (l) if Probe 2's reading shows that the column-doesn't-develop fact is structurally limiting in ways the project wants to address. v2 of this synthesis does not commit to either reading; it commits to making the question legible at Probe 2's calibration.

## 7. What changed from v1, and why (NEW)

This section names the revision explicitly — what changed, why, and how the change is itself an instance of the journal discipline `Kind_design_notes.md` describes.

**What changed (single sentence).** v1 of this synthesis was drafted before Probe 1.5 ran; Probe 1.5's findings (Phases 0–8 plus 7.5) restructure what Probe 2 is calibrating against in ways v1 could not have anticipated, and v2 absorbs those findings — chiefly the three reading surfaces with differently susceptible confabulation profiles, the column-doesn't-develop fact and the capacity-as-init-shape distinction, the substrate-genericity finding, the new lesion candidate `init_zero_scalar_column`, the prompt-revision direction, the reflection-vs-self-modeling distinction, and the deferred environmental-auxiliary control's elevated informativeness. Nothing in v1's load-bearing architecture changes; the partial revisions are scoped to the operationalizations, the calibration protocol, the success criteria, and the tensions.

**Why (in the form of three propositions).**

1. *Probe 1.5 produced three reading surfaces v1 did not anticipate.* v1 read everything against substrate-side patterns. Probe 1.5 v2 added head-internal and behavior-side surfaces by adding the self-prediction head and the actor's read-access to the scalar. Phase 8's four-way KS comparison produced empirical evidence that the three surfaces stratify by confabulation susceptibility and by self-specificity. v2 of this synthesis absorbs the stratification because the calibration protocol's seven elements operate differently at each surface, and the criterion claims have different evidential weights at each surface. v1's architecture (parallel-with-arbiter, seven-element protocol) preserves; v2's per-surface extensions are what the Probe 1.5 findings require.

2. *The column-doesn't-develop fact and the capacity-as-init-shape distinction surface tension (l), which v1 could not have surfaced.* The structural reason the column doesn't develop is mask-via-zero-feed during imagined-trajectory training; this was Probe 1.5 v2 plan §2.3's choice, not a v1-of-this-synthesis assumption.
