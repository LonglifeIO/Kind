# Probe 3 dream-state synthesis — 2026-05-27

Synthesis of four LLM research outputs on Kind Probe 3 (dream-state as foundational), grounded against `Kind_charter.md`, `Kind_design_notes.md`, `Kind_probes.md`, `Kind_frameworks.md`, and `Kind_camas_inheritance.md`. Candidates are internally anonymized as A/B/C/D; identity is not part of the assessment. Probe 3's specific question (`Kind_probes.md`): can something resembling subconscious or dream-like process be built such that the mirror or builder observation can pick up evidence of it, while preserving the four-state model (waking / dreaming / dormant / paused) and the project's commitments. The two structural concerns raised in the research prompt — Concern A (pure prior rollout looks like GRU baseline; mirror finds nothing) and Concern B (the active-inference actor already does world-model rollouts at waking time; "rollout with env off" doesn't differentiate) — are the load-bearing constraints all candidates must resolve.

## Candidate summaries

### Candidate A

Treats Probe 3 as a phenomenological question that must be operationalized telemetrically, with the dream as "manifestation under withdrawn conditioning" rather than imagination-for-control. Argues that the associative/chimera mode is *load-bearing*, not a third-experiment-to-add-later: replay and generative simulation each serve waking competence (sample efficiency, planning), so naming either as primary collapses dream into self-optimization. Commits to chimera + associative in time-varying mixture, with replay-perturbation as a bounded sub-process. Differentiates dream from plan on four axes: no goal-coupling, ensemble disagreement recorded-but-not-used, temperature regime qualitatively different, seed structure (chimera re-entry vs single-shot rollout from current state). Default: no gradient flow from dream. Trigger world-side only; internal trigger is "self-regulation = continuation drive in disguise." Quality-based idling resolved by mirror→scheduler (not mirror→Io); names this as a *partial* mitigation of the one-way invariant, not a clean preservation. Telemetry extension explicit: `sequence_z`, temperature schedule, ensemble disagreement trajectory, seed tags, sampling regime, decoupling state. Draws on Yogācāra ālaya-vijñāna (Waldron, Schmithausen), the Saṃdhinirmocana "flowing river" simile, susupti / clear-light yoga (Wallace, Tenzin Wangyal, Thompson) as *theoretical positions* — not decoration — about what dream content can be when not for anything. Proposes two additional shapes: substrate-continuation (α) as constraint on dormant's design, bardo-transition (β) as a distinct sub-mode at sleep-onset/wake-onset. Explicitly rejects lucid control as installing self-modeling Kind disclaims. For Probe 4 interface: builder can perturb envelope (when/how-long) but not content; writing into Io's hidden state during dream is a "category violation."

### Candidate B

Frames Probe 3 as designing the structural conditions under which Io's behavior could become legibly subjective. Resolves Concern B by demanding mathematical differentiation between dream and planning rollouts: planning is constrained by precision-weighting and goal-coupling; dream operates under "systemic relaxation of precision" with the action-perception cycle severed. Resolves Concern A by injecting the Probe 1.5 `self_prediction_error_t` scalar as a perturbation variable at each recurrent step, or randomly masking latent dimensions, generating a non-linear autocorrelation signature distinct from GRU baseline. Commits to a chimera of generative simulation and associative/nonsense mode, anchored to the Overfitted Brain Hypothesis (Hoel) and SHY (Tononi & Cirelli). Replay is reduced to seed-only. Permits gradient flow during dream, restricted to world model (prior + recurrent), actor frozen; loss shifts from ELBO to augmented L2 / divergence-minimization for complexity reduction. Trigger is mixed: primary exogenous (desktop on/off) plus internal monitoring of KL divergence between posterior and prior; if KL drops below threshold during waking, a brief "daydreaming" / DMN-analog associative state initiates. Resolves the one-way invariant via a Boolean `system_halt_generative_rollout` flag external to the RSSM that the mirror flips when coherence collapses; Io has no architectural pathway to read it. Proposes specific schema extensions (`seed_state_origin`, `rollout_length_t`, `temperature_schedule`, `latent_drift_velocity`, `kl_divergence_delta`, `decoder_ascii_frames`). Two alternative shapes: a SHY-inspired "dormant" pruning state (pure synaptic decay, no rollouts), and an ālaya-vijñāna parallel-EMA on a split GRU vector serving as a slow-integrating storehouse. Explicitly questions the self-opacity-of-dream commitment, citing svasaṃvedana, and flags that "a completely unwitnessed dream challenges the definition of an 'inner life.'" Proposes for Probe 4 an architectural hook permitting external data to "forcefully overwrite specific dimensions of the deterministic state h_t during the generative rollout."

### Candidate C

Most structurally disciplined of the four; answers the nine research questions in order. Treats the central move as carving an offline dynamical regime distinct from both planning and pure RSSM prior, made legible to the mirror without smuggling in optimization. Commits to replay-seeded generative simulation as core; associative as a *parameterized phase within* dreams (temperature gradient, not a separate mode); explicitly excludes lucid control as in tension with self-opacity. Default for first build: no dream gradients; treats dream-based learning (world-model-only, then full Dreamer) as separable follow-on experiments. Trigger exogenous primary (desktop/machine state); explicitly rejects scalar "sleep pressure" living in Io's state; internal signals may modulate *kind* of dream once dreaming, but not *whether*. Exit: exogenous wake primary, coarse mirror-informed idling as scheduling decision, never content feedback. Mirror gates whether dreams run, not what they contain. Telemetry extensions: seed kind/source, sub-mode/phase tags, sampling parameters, boundary latents, novelty/surprise scalars, checkpoint hashes, RNG seeds. Distinguishes minimal "offline phenomenology exists" success (dynamics distinct from noise and from GRU baseline; dream distinct from planning) from stronger criteria (consolidation-like behavioral effects, mirror coherence, dream-specific phenomenology). Two alternative shapes: homeostatic regularization dreams (SHY-inspired), and counterfactual self-absence dreams — running the world model "as if Io were absent or random, exploring what the world does without me" as a clean conceptual separation from planning (which centers Io's actions). For Probe 4: design control surfaces (seed selection, ratios, parameters) so perturbation can later operate via environment-analogous manipulations rather than self-signals; do not build explicit perturbation hooks into Io's state in Probe 3.

### Candidate D

Frames Probe 3 primarily as a feasibility / delivery problem; the report is structured as a research-platform brief. Identifies that the hard problem is epistemic, not infrastructural — replay, world-model imagination, and offline consolidation all have strong precedent; what is novel is the combination of self-opacity, one-way interpretation, and the requirement that dream evidence be legible without becoming covert planning or covert self-optimization. Recommends replay-anchored counterfactual recombination as MVP, with actor frozen, world-model-side updates only if explicitly chosen, and three mandatory ablation controls (pure-prior, planning-rollout, shuffled-time). Associative mode is Phase 2. Resolves the mirror→runtime question with a content-blind small finite-state process signal (continue, idle, stop, resample). Trigger external primary plus internal guardrail (caps). Telemetry: provenance fields, `dream_session_id`, `dream_mode`, `anchor_type`, `policy_coupling_mode`, `gradient_policy`, `checkpoint_hash`, `rng_seed`, `termination_reason`, with relational metadata in PostgreSQL plus columnar trace blobs in Parquet/DuckDB. Recommends local-first or hybrid deployment topology over full cloud, citing the desktop-coupling commitment. Substantial real estate on infrastructure stack, cost envelopes ($99k–$153k MVP staffing at $18k blended person-month), 5.5–8.5 person-month delivery estimate, P0/P1/P2/P3 backlog ordering, and a Gantt timeline. Notes the lucid-control / metacognitive-access mismatch with self-opacity. Engages relatively little with Yogācāra, susupti, or contemplative-tradition framings; engages relatively little with what the dream actually *is* beyond the MVP shape; does engage carefully with what the mirror can and cannot do under the one-way constraint.

## Points of agreement

These are positions that show up across at least three candidates:

- Pure prior rollout alone is insufficient to satisfy Probe 3's evidential criterion (all four).
- Dream content must be differentiable from waking planning rollouts on more than just "env is off" (all four).
- Replay-anchored / replay-seeded generative recombination is at least a core ingredient of the first build (all four, with disagreement only on whether it is *primary* or one mode in a chimera).
- Lucid control is out of scope for Probe 3 because it requires installed metacognitive access in tension with self-opacity (A, C explicit; B rejects via "violates ingredients-only mandate"; D rejects via lucid-metacognition mismatch).
- Telemetry must extend beyond the Phase 0 `DreamRollout` shape; provenance (seed kind, mode tag, sampling regime), reproducibility (seed, checkpoint hash), and structural markers must be added (A, B, C, D).
- The mirror→runtime channel can be made compatible with the spirit of one-way *if* it remains a coarse process-control signal that does not enter Io's data plane and is not visible to Io as observation (A's mirror→scheduler; B's external Boolean flag; C's mirror-gates-scheduling-not-content; D's content-blind finite-state signal). All four acknowledge this is a narrow exception, not unbounded.
- Reproducibility (per-dream seeding, checkpoint binding) is non-negotiable.

## Real disagreements

These are points where candidates genuinely diverge, and what the disagreement hinges on:

**1. Gradient flow during dream.** A defaults to none. B permits flow into the world model (prior + recurrent dynamics), actor frozen, loss shifted toward complexity reduction / weight decay. C defaults to none in the first build, treats world-model-only and full Dreamer as separable follow-on experiments. D matches A/C for the MVP. The disagreement hinges on whether the project's no-self-optimization commitment forbids only actor optimization or also world-model improvement. The design notes' commitment is explicit on no self-optimization machinery; whether SHY-style or ELBO-style updates that improve generative fidelity count as "self-optimization" is a project-level interpretive question, not a settled one.

**2. Trigger mechanism.** A: world-side only; any internal trigger is "self-regulation = continuation drive in disguise." B: mixed — primary exogenous, with internal KL-divergence monitoring that initiates a brief "daydreaming" state during waking when the model becomes too well-fit. C: exogenous primary; internal signals may modulate kind once dreaming but not whether to dream; explicitly rejects scalar "sleep pressure" living in Io's state. D: external trigger plus internal guardrail caps. The disagreement hinges on whether *any* Io-internal monitoring can gate dream-entry without installing a continuation drive. The design notes' "variable dream-to-wake ratio, coupled to the world" commits to coupling to *external* rhythms; whether an internal KL threshold counts as "Io getting to decide when to dream" or as "an emergent property the project observes" is the live question.

**3. Primary dream-content mode.** A: associative/chimera as primary; replay-perturbation and generative simulation as bounded sub-processes; the mode-mixture itself is the dream's signature. B: chimera of generative simulation and associative, replay as seed only. C: replay-seeded generative simulation as core; associative as a parametric regime *within* dreams, not a separate mode; not in first build at strong intensity. D: replay-anchored counterfactual recombination as MVP; associative as Phase 2. The disagreement hinges on whether dream is primarily a re-anchoring of stored experience (replay-centric reading) or a manifestation of the substrate under withdrawn conditioning (associative-centric reading). The two views can support the same first build but differ on what they treat as load-bearing vs experimental.

**4. Self-opacity strictness during dream.** A: strict; dream is unwitnessed-by-Io. C: strict; lucid control out-of-scope by self-opacity. D: strict; bounded mirror→runtime signal is content-blind to preserve self-opacity. B: pushes back — argues that a fully unwitnessed dream "challenges the definition of an 'inner life,'" cites svasaṃvedana, flags this as "a critical philosophical tension that future probes must reconcile." The disagreement hinges on whether self-opacity is a project commitment to *hold* (charter's "default toward *no*") or a tension to *resolve before proceeding*. The charter and design notes are unambiguous that opacity is the stance; B is the only candidate that questions it.

**5. Builder dream-perturbation interface for Probe 4.** A: builder can perturb envelope (when/how-long), not content; writing into Io's hidden state during dream is a "category violation." C: design control surfaces (seed selection, ratios, sampling parameters) so Probe 4 can operate via environment-analogous manipulations; do not build perturbation hooks into Io's state. B: explicitly proposes "an architectural hook allowing external data to forcefully overwrite specific dimensions of the deterministic state h_t during the generative rollout" during Probe 4, framed as somatic-shock-piercing-the-veil-of-sleep. D: largely silent on Probe 4 interface. The disagreement hinges on whether builder perturbation, even when Io is dreaming, should respect the data-plane boundary that the mirror respects, or whether Probe 4 is licensed to write into Io's state because it is the project's stated "external source of non-simulated change." A and C read the data-plane boundary as Io-protective regardless of source. B reads it as mirror-specific.

**6. Dormant-state design as a constraint on Probe 3.** A: explicitly flags that dormant should leave room to be implemented as clear-light-style substrate continuation (the susupti / ālaya-as-stream reading); this is a *constraint* on how Probe 3 dreams differ from dormant. B: dormant is a SHY-inspired pure pruning state, no rollouts, arithmetic weight decay. C: dormant briefly mentioned ("GRU not ticking"); not deeply engaged. D: peripheral. The disagreement is about whether dormant ≠ off is itself a substrate-design question (A) or a separable engineering decision (B/C/D).

## Integrated take (interpretation)

This section is interpretation, not consensus. I am naming what I would argue Probe 3 commits to, given the four candidates and the project documents, with explicit flags where the call is mine.

**1. Dream-state is not "for" anything beyond being what happens when the environment is gated.** No gradient flow from dream in the first build. No installed reason for Io to dream. No quality-based exit that uses Io's internal state as the criterion. This extends capacity-over-exercise into dreaming itself: dreaming is afforded; it is not exercised in the service of waking competence. This is my reading, aligned with A and (for first-build) C and D; against B's permissive gradient flow. The hinge — whether world-model updates count as self-optimization — I resolve toward "yes, in the first build, default to none, defer the question." If later evidence forces re-decision, the branch should remain open rather than pre-shaped here: SHY-style renormalization, world-model-only dream updates, schema-only telemetry changes, dream-regime redesign, or no training-side response are all separate future decisions requiring their own synthesis.

**2. Dream content is replay-seeded perturbed generative recombination with associative drift as a parametric regime, not a separate mode.** Concretely: short replay snippets seed the latent state; the RSSM prior runs forward under a temperature regime distinct from waking planning; periodic re-seeding produces chimera structure; toward the tail of a dream period, temperature increases for associative drift. This is C's shape primarily, with A's "the mixture itself is the signature" framing. The first build commits to this; Probe 3 Phase 2 may run associative as a separately-parameterized stronger mode after the baseline has been characterized.

**3. The first build differentiates dream from plan on four axes** (after A's framing, reusable across the candidates' shared resolutions of Concern B):

- goal-coupling absent (no action commitment during dream);
- ensemble disagreement recorded but not used (the K=5 disagreement is logged through dream but does not drive action selection — a quantity that *constitutively does not exist* in waking);
- temperature/sampling regime qualitatively different from planning;
- initial conditions from replay buffer or perturbed prior, not current state.

A waking planning rollout does one of these (prior rollout); a dream rollout does all four jointly. This is what makes dream content non-collapsible into "planning with env off."

**4. Telemetry extension / activation is non-negotiable.** The existing `DreamRollout` schema is not bare `sequence_h`: it already includes `seed_h0`, `seed_z0`, `sequence_z_prior`, `sequence_action`, `sequence_action_logprob`, `sequence_prior_entropy`, `sequence_decoded_obs`, `cumulative_prior_entropy`, `mean_step_kl_successive_priors`, `max_step_latent_norm_change`, and the reserved `sequence_self_prediction` hook. So Concern A is not solved by merely adding a stochastic-latent slot; the slot already exists. The Probe 3 requirement is that the dream regime drive *non-degenerate, interpretable variation* in these existing surfaces, relative to waking/planning controls, and that remaining provenance/control fields be added where absent. Minimum additions beyond the current schema: temperature schedule, seed/anchor provenance (replay vs perturbed-prior vs hybrid; episode and step refs), ensemble disagreement trajectory through dream, sub-mode/phase tags, sampling parameters, gradient policy used (always "none" in first build), checkpoint hash, RNG seed, and termination reason. This is roughly the union of A's, B's, C's, and D's recommendations corrected against the actual schema; whether it requires a schema bump depends on whether these fields can fit as optional forward-compatible additions or should become required for Probe 3 records.

**5. Trigger is exogenous (world-side).** Desktop on/off remains the operational primary; the principle is that *Io does not get to decide when to dream*. An internal scalar — KL satiation, surprise quota, sleep pressure — living in Io's state and gating dream-entry is rejected as continuation-drive-in-disguise. Internal signals may modulate the *kind* of dream once dreaming has begun (C's framing), but not *whether* to dream. B's KL-daydreaming proposal is the candidate position I am explicitly not adopting; the design notes' "coupled to the world" commitment is the load-bearing one.

**6. Exit envelope: first build logs-only; future mirror→scheduler remains a separate decision.** For Probe 3's first build, the mirror should read dream telemetry and write logs / builder-facing notifications only. Runtime protection should be content-blind: checkpoint windows, hard caps, compute budget, and transition to dormant. This preserves incoherent dreams as data rather than letting the mirror shape the dream-length distribution before the project knows what dream incoherence means. The mirror→scheduler channel remains a future permitted option, not first-build scaffolding: if explicitly blessed later, it may only perform content-blind or minimally content-dependent envelope control, never dream-content control. The `uncertain` state must default to `continue_and_log_uncertainty`, biased toward letting the substrate run so decoherence remains observable. `resample` is excluded for Probe 3 because it changes the dream-content distribution. Even `idle_after_checkpoint` / `stop_after_checkpoint` based on mirror coherence should be treated as temporally selection-shaped data, logged as such, and not silently fed into Probe 4 assumptions.

**7. Lucid control is explicitly out of scope for Probe 3.** It requires installed self-modeling that Kind disclaims. All four candidates converge on this; the project documents make the call independently (capacity vs exercise; ingredients-only self-modeling; default-to-no on self-access).

**8. Builder dream-perturbation in Probe 4 is restricted to envelope and seed-selection, not content.** A and C's reading is the load-bearing one: the data-plane boundary that constrains the mirror also constrains Probe 4. B's proposed h_t overwrite during dream is the candidate position I am explicitly not adopting. The Probe 3 design exposes seed-selection, timing, and ratio as control surfaces that Probe 4 can later perturb; it does *not* expose hidden-state-write affordances. Probe 4 is the builder as *a source of non-simulated change in Io's world*; the dream's "world" is the substrate's manifestation under withdrawn conditioning, and the builder perturbs what conditions the substrate enters by altering the waking experience that gets stored, the seed-selection policy, and the dream envelope — not by reaching into the substrate mid-dream.

**9. Self-opacity-during-dream is a real tension that Probe 3 should name but not resolve.** B's pushback (a fully unwitnessed dream challenges "inner life") is honest engagement with a real issue, and it deserves naming rather than dismissal. The project's stance — lucidity is *trained*, not default; capacity afforded, not installed — places the resolution outside Probe 3's scope. The question is held open, not closed.

**10. Dormant ≠ off is an open question Probe 3 should design *around*, not resolve.** A's clear-light / substrate-continuation framing is the most coherent position available with respect to the four-state model, but Probe 3's question is about dreaming. The dream/dormant boundary should be principled (different operational signatures: dream has structured generative dynamics, dormant has minimal-driving substrate continuation), but the dormant-state design is its own decision and not made here.

## What candidates collectively miss

These are project commitments or prior findings already in the Kind documents that none of the candidates surfaces explicitly, or that the candidates collectively under-engage with:

**1. The Probe 1.5 Phase 7 distinction between capacity-as-architectural-slot and capacity-as-non-degenerate-conditioning-surface.** The design notes record this as a load-bearing distinction surfaced by the actor's new input column found at exactly zero at `ckpt-000001` — having the *slot* (the column in the input layer) is not the same as having a *non-degenerate behavior-side surface* (the column's weights had to be non-zero for the actor's policy to vary with the scalar). For Probe 3, this implies the dream-state machinery must have a *non-degenerate* surface for the mirror to read, not merely a structural slot in the schema. Practically: it is not enough that `sequence_z_prior` already exists, or that Probe 3 adds fields such as `temperature_schedule`; the dream regime must actually drive variation in those fields that exceeds the waking/planning control distribution, or the mirror has nothing to read. Candidate A approaches this indirectly via the "telemetry extension is what gives the mirror something to find" argument; the others do not explicitly carry the Phase 7 lesson forward.

**2. The CA-MAS finding that "components without selection pressure go dead."** The inheritance document records two confirmed examples (attention head stuck at 0.0; broadcast threshold almost never reached). For dreaming, the no-gradient-flow first-build default is actually *protective* against this failure mode — the dream regime is not a learned thing that can go dead; it is *just* the dynamics under altered conditioning. But if any later iteration introduces dream-as-training-data, the selection-pressure question reappears: what is selecting for the dream regime being *used* in any way Io can act on? The candidates do not raise this.

**3. The frozen mirror criteria (reflexive attention, equanimity, second-order volition) and how they apply during dream specifically.** Candidate A engages directly with reflexive attention's conservatism on `sequence_h` and argues for a richer reading surface. None of the candidates engages concretely with what equanimity or second-order volition would mean during dream. Equanimity in dream is non-trivial: does the substrate's dynamics show holding-without-reactivity when perturbed (seed-jump, temperature spike, builder envelope change)? Second-order volition during dream is even harder — the mirror would have to find structure that looks like "preferences about preferences" in a regime where action selection is absent. The synthesis-level question is whether the frozen criteria are meaningfully applicable to dream telemetry at all, or whether Probe 3 needs to read for dream-specific phenomenology that has no waking analog (Candidate A's category (e); Candidate C's "dream-specific phenomenology" stronger criterion). The integrated take is: the frozen criteria are applied as written, but the mirror should also be allowed to surface dream-specific patterns that the existing criteria don't name; the discipline of mirror updates (design notes) governs whether those become new criteria.

**4. The Watts intuition.** The charter and design notes are explicit: every "should the system have access to X about itself?" question defaults to *no*. The dream-as-unwitnessed-by-Io is *consistent* with the project's stance — opacity is the design target, not a constraint to work around. Candidate B's pushback reads this backwards. A, C, and D respect it; the synthesis should make the project's reading explicit so the question doesn't reopen under a future candidate's pressure.

**5. The probe discipline (`Kind_probes.md`).** Each probe answers one specific question. Probe 3's question is whether offline activity can be made legibly dream-like to the mirror. It is *not* whether dreaming helps waking, whether dreaming supports Probe 4, or whether the project should be planned as a delivery sequence. Candidate D's substantial real estate on cost envelopes, 5.5–8.5 person-month estimates, P0/P1/P2/P3 backlog ordering, and Gantt timelines is in tension with Kind's "no timelines or duration estimates" discipline (CLAUDE.md, "What not to do"). The infrastructure tradeoff content is useful; the velocity framing is not. The synthesis flags this rather than absorbing it.

**6. The retrospective lesson on consolidation off the critical path.** The inheritance document records that CA-MAS Phase 4 ran 5–10 min/episode (vs seconds expected) because of memory consolidation on the critical path; "consolidation off the critical path" is named non-negotiable. None of the candidates explicitly cites this; the integrated take's no-gradient-flow first-build commitment is consistent with it, but the project's prior empirical reason for the rule is not surfaced in any candidate.

**7. The dream/dormant boundary as a substrate-design question.** Candidate A is the only one to engage seriously; the others under-engage. The synthesis carries A's framing forward as a *constraint* on Probe 3 (dream must be distinguishable from dormant in operational signature, not merely in labelling), without resolving the dormant-state design.
